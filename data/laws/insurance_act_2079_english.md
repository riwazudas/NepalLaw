Act No. 27 of the year 2079


An Act to amend and consolidate the law relating to insurance


Preamble: It is desirable to amend and consolidate the existing laws relating to insurance in order to develop the insurance system and the insurance business by making it systematic, regular, competitive and reliable to provide quality and reliable insurance services to the public through healthy competition in the insurance business, and to protect the rightful interests of the insured by effectively regulating the insurance business,

## Chapter-1 Preliminary

1) The name of this Act is ” Insurance Act, 2079.”


2) This Act shall also apply in respect of branch offices established by the insurer in the State of Nepal and anywhere outside Nepal.

### Section 1: Short title and commencement

1) The name of this Act is “Insurance Act, 2079.”


2) This Act shall also apply in respect of branch offices established by the insurer in the State of Nepal and anywhere outside Nepal.

---

### Section 2: Definitions

Unless the subject or context otherwise requires, in this Act:


(a) “Chairman” means the Chairman of the Board of Directors pursuant to section 6.


(b) “Other insurance service provider” means a person in an institution other than an insurance intermediary licensed by the Authority to act in relation to insurance.


(c) “Basic shareholder” means a person, company or institution owning one percent or more of the paid-up capital of an insurer and includes a unitary house where the members of the family of the unitary house separately own one percent or more of the shares.


(d) “Licence” means a licence granted to carry on the business of insurance in accordance with this Act and includes a licence granted to an insurance intermediary and other insurance service provider to carry on the business as specified.


(e) “Family of one household” means the husband or wife, son, daughter-in-law, daughter, son-in-law, adopted son, adopted daughter, father, mother, stepmother and any other person required to maintain or maintain himself.


(f) “Executive Head” means the Chief Executive Officer responsible for the administrative, managerial or business operations of an insurer appointed in accordance with section 57.


(g) “Self-Asset” means the whole of the liability, other than the paid-up capital of the insurer and any named reserves, funds or accumulated profits to which the shareholder is entitled, or any reputation of the insurer, including the accumulated losses, if any, of the insured.


(h) “Life insurance” means an agreement entered into by the insurer with the insured in respect of the life of any person for payment of any specified sum of money in a lump sum or instalment on the basis of his age, by the person who has paid the sum assured by him or herself or, in the event of his death, by the person who has paid the sum assured by himself or, as the case may be, by the person who has given the sum assured by himself or, as the case may be, by the person who has willed it or by the holder of the sum assured by himself.


(i) “Third Party Facilitator” means a licensed insurance intermediary acting as a conduit between the insurer and the insured for the conduct of insurance.


(j) “Prescribed” or “as prescribed” means prescribed or prescribed in this Act or the rules or regulations made under this Act.


(k) “Close Relative” means father-in-law, mother-in-law, brothers, sisters, sisters-in-law, brothers-in-law, sister-in-law, uncle, aunt, marital uncle, marital aunt, grandson, granddaughter, granddaughter-in-law, nephew, niece, partial uncle, partial aunt, nephew, niece, cousin, and the husband, wife, son-in-law or daughter-in-law of those relatives. 


(l) “Non-life insurance” means insurance other than life insurance and reinsurance. The term shall mean the agreement entered into by the insurer with the insured for payment of any specified amount of damages or compensation in respect of the risk of any property or liability after accepting the risk as mentioned in the insurance statement by charging an insurance fee.


(m) “Reinsurance” means an agreement entered into with such insurer to bear by the reinsurer or another insurer a higher proportion of the risk borne by any one insurer.


(n) “Proposed insurance company” means a public limited company proposed to be set up in accordance with the law in force for the purpose of carrying on the business of insurance.


(o)” Authority” means the Nepal Insurance Authority pursuant to Section 3.


(p) “Insurer” means an insurance company or reinsurance company licensed to carry on the business of insurance in accordance with this Act.


(q) “Insurance Agent” means a licensed insurance intermediary person or institution acting on a commission basis on behalf of an insurer, other than an employee of the insurer.


(r) “Insurance contract” means a written agreement between an insurer and an insured on the condition that the insured bears the risk pursuant to an agreement on payment of a fixed insurance fee.


(s) “Insurance Broker” means an insurance intermediary person or institution licensed to act as an insurance broker in relation to the insurance business.


(t) “Insurance Pool” means a collective insurance fund established by insurers to collectively bear the risk in respect of insurance or risk of a particular nature, to collect insurance charges at one place, to arrange for reinsurance and to arrange for payment of claims.


(u) “Insurance Intermediary” means an insurance broker, third-party facilitator or other insurance intermediary licensed by the Authority.


(v) “Insurance Business” means life insurance business, non-insurance business or reinsurance business.


(w) “Insured” means a person who has obtained the title of insured from a recognised institution for the purpose of assessing the assets and liabilities of the insured.


(x)”Account” means an instrument of insurance issued by the insurer to the insurer to the insured acknowledging any risk.


(y)”Insurance Surveyor” means an insurance intermediary person or institution licensed to assess damaged property and liabilities.


(z)”Insurance Fee” means the amount charged by the insurer as a lump sum or instalment from the insured as a consideration while insuring.


(aa) “Insured” means an insured person and includes an entitled person from insurance.


(bb)” Bonus” means the share or amount of profit to be insured on the basis of the sum insured under the life insurance that accrues to the profit.


(cc)”Ministry” means the Ministry of Finance of the Government of Nepal.


(dd)”Micro-Insurance” means micro-insurance provided in accordance with Chapter 8, aimed at low-income, economically, socially and geographically disadvantaged groups.


(ee) ” Auditor” means an auditor who has obtained a license to audit pursuant to prevailing law.


(ff)” Financial Interest” means the involvement of the insurer of the following persons, institutions, firms, companies or organisations:


(1) The director or the principal shareholder or the executive head or a member of his unit,


(2) A single or joint firm, company or organised institution having the right to nominate a director,


(3) The director, executive head or family member of the firm, company or organisation pursuant to sub-clause  (2),


(4) Where a person, firm, company or body corporate pursuant to sub-clause (1) or (2) has taken ten percent or more of the paid-up capital of any other single or joint firm, company or body corporate, the firm, company or body corporate has taken such shares.


(gg) “Foreign Insurance Company” means an insurance company licensed to carry on insurance business in a foreign country and licensed to carry on insurance business in Nepal in accordance with this Act.


(hh) “Administrator Level” means a manager who is involved in the decision-making process relating to the operation and management of an organised institution and includes a person holding the designation of manager or manager appointed to the post of head of an organised institution or any branch or unit thereof, or an officer of the second class or above, so as to exercise final responsibility or authority.


(ii) “Board of Directors” means the insurer’s Board of Directors to be constituted per Section 47.


(jj) “Member” means a member of the Authority’s Board of Directors and includes the Chairperson.


(kk) “Committee” means the Board of Directors of the Authority constituted in accordance with Section 6.


(ll) ” Collective Insurance Fund” means a collective insurance fund (insurance pool) established by insurers to collectively bear the risk in respect of insurance or risk of particular nature, to collect insurance charges at one place, to arrange for reinsurance and to arrange for payment of claims.


(mm) “Permanent Resident Permit” means the Diversity Immigrant Visa (DV), Permanent Resident Visa (PR) or Green Card issued to a Nepali citizen by a foreign country to enable him to permanently reside in that country without any conditions and the term also refers to the Permanent Resident Permit in any name issued to a Nepali citizen to permanently reside abroad.

## Chapter-2 Establishment of Authority and Its Functions, Duties and Power

### Section 3: Establishment of the Authority

1) Nepal Insurance Authority has been established to act as a regulatory body for the insurance business.


2) The central office of the authority shall be situated in Kathmandu Valley, and the authority may, as required, open its branch or contact office within or outside Nepal.

---

### Section 4: The Authority shall be an autonomous and incorporated body

1) The Authority shall be a self-governing body with a perpetual succession and shall have legal personality.


2) The authority shall exercise all the rights and abide by all the duties and responsibilities, as per prevailing laws, in the capacity of the legal person.

---

### Section 5: Functions, Duties and Power of the Authority

In addition to the functions, duties and powers set out elsewhere in this Act, the functions, duties and powers of the Authority shall be as follows:


(a) Act as an advisor of the Government of Nepal regarding insurance,


(b)  Draft the National Policy on insurance and make recommendations to the government of Nepal,


(c) Grant prior approval for establishing an Insurance company, issue a license or revoke such license,


(d) Formulate necessary byelaws, directives, guidelines or orders for the insurance business,


(e) Issue, renew and revoke licenses to Insurance intermediaries,


(f) Determine the capital and capital fund required for the insurance business,


(g) Prepare and implement the necessary programs to make the insurance business systematic, regular, competitive and credible,


(h) Conduct or cause to conduct the study, research, training, orientation or outreach programs for the development and expansion of the Insurance business,


(i) Make decisions on determining the insurance liability against the complaints filed by the insured against the insurer,


(j) Dispute resolution of various parties of insurance,


(k) Take necessary actions for minimisation of risk in the insurance business,


(l) Prepare and implement the programs for the development of human resources and for the promotion of insurance education for the development of insurance in the business,


(m) Prepare and implement the insurance promotion programs to increase access to insurance for all people,


(n) Promote micro-insurance businesses to ensure the access to insurance for the people with low income,


(o) Cooperate, coordinate and exchange information with other governmental, or non-governmental organisations for the regulation, inspection, supervision and development of the insurance  business,


(p) Cooperate with foreign Insurance Regulatory Agency, international organisations or other organisations that conduct studies, research and training in insurance,


(q) Represent the Government of Nepal or an Authority in an International organisation relating Insurance  Regulation and t obtain membership in such organisation,


(r) Operate Fund for the protection  of the interest of Policy Holders and carry out our cause to carry out other necessary activities,


(s) Coordinate with provinces regarding insurance,


(t)  Carry out other necessary activities relating to insurance as a Regulatory Agency.

---

3) An officer of the rank of an officer appointed by the Authority shall act as Secretary to the Committee.

---

### Section 7: Appointment of Chairperson or Member

1) There shall be a committee consisting of the Secretary of the Ministry and an expert member related to the insurance sector under the coordination of the member looking after the relevant area of the National Planning Commission to recommend the name before the Government of Nepal for the appointment of the chairman of the committee and on the recommendation of the committee, the chairman shall be appointed in accordance with this section.


2) For the appointment of Chairperson under sub-section (1), the recommendation committee shall recommend the names of at least three persons to the Government of Nepal from among the persons qualified under Section 8 of this Act.


3) The Government of Nepal shall appoint the Chairperson from the persons recommended as per sub-section (2).


4) The appointment of Chairperson pursuant to sub-section (3) shall be made one month prior to the vacancy of such post.


5) The Government of Nepal shall appoint the members pursuant to clauses (d) and (e) of sub-section (1) of section 6 from among the persons who have attained the qualification pursuant to sub-section (1) of section 8. In such an appointment, at least one woman will have to be appointed to represent.

---

### Section 8: Qualification and Disqualification of Chairperson and Member

1) The following qualifications in order to be eligible to be appointed as Chairperson or member of the Authority:


(a) One who has obtained a Master’s degree or equivalent in the subject of Insurance, monetary, banking, commerce, public administration, statistics, mathematics, economics, or law and has gained at least five years of higher management-level experience in the related field,


(b) Who has maintained higher moral character,


(c) One who is not disqualified under sub-section (3).


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), such qualification shall not be required in respect of a member pursuant to clauses (b) and (c) of sub-section (1) of section 6.


3) The following persons shall be disqualified from being appointed as chairperson or member of the Authority or, if already appointed, shall be removed:


(a) Not Nepali Citizen,


(b) Incumbent Employee or Director or Advisor of Insurer,


(c) Substantial shareholder of any insurer or any of his family members is a substantial shareholder of any insurer or having a financial interest or substantial shareholder of any corporate body in which any insurer has a financial interest,


(d) One who is adjudicated as an insolvent as per prevailing laws,


(e) One who is unable to continue to serve in the council due to physical or mental circumstances,


(f) One who is sentenced to punishment by the court upon being convicted of an offence of corruption or banking related or insurance-related or fraud, human trafficking, money laundering, or organised crime or any offence involving moral turpitude,


(g) One who is blocklist as per prevailing laws and a period of at least three years has not been completed after removal name from blocklist,


(h) One who has been punished by a regulatory authority established as per prevailing laws or by the court,


(i) One who has obtained the permission of permanent residency in any foreign country,


(j) One who has separated from any insurer from the capacity of Chairperson, director, Executive Chief, or Officer of Top Level Management and a period of 2 years has not lapsed upon separation.

---

### Section 9: Tenure of Chairperson and Members

1) The term of office of the Chairperson and the members appointed pursuant to clauses (d) and (e) of sub-section (1) of Section 6 shall be four years from the date of appointment.





2) The Government of Nepal may, if it deems appropriate, re-appoint the Chairperson or a member for one additional term.

---

### Section 10: Conditions of Service of the Chairperson and Members

1) The chairperson shall be a full-time employee of the authority.


2) The remuneration and other conditions of service and facilities of the chairperson or members of the Authority shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 11: Oath to be taken

Before assuming their duties, the Chairperson and the members appointed pursuant to clauses (d) and (e) of sub-section (1) of Section 6 shall take an oath of office and secrecy before the Minister of Finance of the Government of Nepal in the format as per the Schedule.

---

### Section 12: Vacancy in the office of Chairperson or Member

1) The Chairperson or a member shall not continue in office in any of the following circumstances:-


(a) If one submits a written resignation to the Government of Nepal.


(b) If one becomes disqualified as a Chairperson or member pursuant to Section 8 of sub-section (3).


(c) If the Government of Nepal removes him due to reasons of misconduct or incompetence to carry on his functions or inability to fulfil his duties and responsibilities pursuant to sub-section (2).


2) For the purpose of removal of the Chairperson or member pursuant to sub-section (1) clause (c), the Government of Nepal may release the Chairperson or member upon the recommendation of the Inquiry Committee formed as under:





(a)
Appointed by the Government of Nepal from amongst retired judges of the High Court
Chairman


(b)
Appointed by the Government of Nepal from amongst persons who had acquired master’s degrees and obtained a minimum of ten years experience in the field of insurance or financial sectors (2 members from different sectors)
Member



3) The Chairperson or member against whom inquiry is being made by the Inquiry committee shall remain ipso facto suspended for the period of inquiry made by the committee so formed.


4) The tenure of the inquiry committee formed under sub-section (2) shall be a maximum of three months from the date of formation.


5) If due to any reasons, the post of Chairperson or member of the inquiry committee formed under sub-section (2) becomes vacant, the Government shall appoint any person to that vacancy for the remaining tenure by fulfilling the same procedures as the person was appointed.

---

### Section 13: Functions Duties and Powers of the Chairperson

The functions, duties and powers of the Chairperson as mentioned elsewhere in this Act shall be as follows:-


(a) To act as Chief Administrative and Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of the authority,


(b) To prepare and review the Authority’s insurance-related and other policies, rules, regulations, directives, working procedures, periodic plans and annual programs and submit them to the committee for approval,


(c) To implement or cause to implement such long-term and short-term plans and policies, directives, rules, regulations, work procedures and annual work plan as necessary to be adopted by the Authority for the management and development of insurance business,


(d) To operate and manage the Authority,


(e) To manage and regularise the activities of the Authority,


(f) To act as a mediator to resolve disputes between the insurer and insured,


(g) To do or cause to do functions associated with regulation, inspection and monitoring of insurance business,


(h) To provide permission to open or close branches, sub-branches or other offices of insurance companies,


(i) To take action with regard to regulation by using the powers conferred upon him by Section 134 sub-sections (1) clauses (a to e) and 134 (2) (b) and (c) of the Act,


(j)  Delegation of functions, power and duties to employees under their jurisdiction and cause them to operate such functions as may be delegated,


(k) To represent or cause to represent the Nepal Insurance Authority in International forums or foreign organisations.

---

### Section 14: Information to be provided to the Authority

The chairperson and members of the Authority shall have to provide information about the following matters to the Authority within 30 days of appointment:


(a) Details about the profession, occupation or organisation in which he had been involved,


(b) Details as to whether the person or any of his family members had subscribed to any shares of any insurer and number of such shares if subscribed,


(c) Details as to whether the person or any of his family members had been a director or any officer or auditor or Advisor of any insurer during the period of previous   three years,


(d) Details as to whether the person or any of his family members had worked as insurance mediators during the period of previous three years,


(e) Details about the moveable and immovable assets held by the person and his family members.

---

### Section 15: Non-abuse of regulatory opportunity

1) The Chairperson, Member of the Committee, Authority employee, or Auditor shall not disclose any information that has come to their knowledge while performing their duties in their official capacity, whether or not it has been made public and is required to be kept confidential according to law, to any person other than the person concerned or disclose its source.


2) The official referred to in sub-section (1) shall not use or cause to be used any confidential information that comes to his knowledge while performing his duties in his official capacity for the benefit, interest or advantage of himself or any member of his immediate family.


3) The Chairperson of the Committee, member, employee of the Authority, auditor, representative or any person working under any contract with the Authority, firm or company shall not, while performing any work on behalf of the Authority, exercise the regulatory authority of the Authority and receive or give any benefit, facility, concession or advantage to himself, his immediate family or any other person.


4) If any work is done in a manner contrary to this Section, the concerned official shall be deemed to have abused his authority in accordance with the prevailing law, and information thereof shall be sent in writing to the concerned body.

---

### Section 16: Meeting of the Board

1) The board’s meeting shall be held, as necessary, at least once every month.


2) The meeting of the board shall be held on such date, time and place as may be prescribed by the Chairperson.


If at least three members of the Board request in writing to convene a meeting, the chairperson or, in his absence, the senior most member shall convene a meeting of the Board within 7 days.


3) The board secretary shall make the agendas available for discussion to the members at least 24 hours before the meeting.


4) The presence of more than fifty percent of total members of the board shall be deemed to constitute a quorum for a board meeting.


5) The board meeting shall be presided over by the chairperson and by the senior most member in his/her absence.


6) The majority of opinions at the board meeting shall be deemed to be the decision of the board, and in the case of a tie, the person presiding over the meeting shall exercise a casting vote.


7) The board may, if deemed appropriate, invite any expert or officials in the related field to attend a meeting as an observer.


8) Minutes regarding agendas discussed and decided over shall be recorded in a separate book, and such minutes shall be signed by the chairperson as swell as the members of the board attending the meeting.


9) The decision of the board must be certified by the board secretary.


10) Other rules of procedures relating to the board meeting shall be determined by the board itself.

---

### Section17: Restrictions on decision-making in cases of conflict of interest

1) Where any proposal in which the chairperson or members or any of their family members or any member has direct or indirect financial or personal or professional interest is to be discussed at
the meeting of the Board, such person shall give information as to such interest to the Board prior to the commencement of discussion on such matter.


2) In accordance with sub-section (1), such person shall not be entitled to participate in the discussion and decision of such a matter in which such person has any direct or indirect financial or personal or professional interest.


3) A decision made contrary to sub-section (1) or (2) shall not apply to such a member.

---

### Section 18: Committees and Sub-Committees may be formed

1) The board may, as per this act or rules and regulations made thereunder, form any committee or sub-committee, as per necessity, for the operation of its business.


2) According to sub-section (1), functions, duties, powers, terms of reference, facilities allowances and procedures of the committee or subcommittee, as the case may be, shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 19: Provision related to Employee

1) The Nepal Insurance Authority shall have such numbers of employees as may be necessary for the efficient operation of the functions of the Authority.


2) The appointment, remuneration, facilities and terms and conditions of service of the employees of the Authority shall be as prescribed.


3) While adding the number of employees or adding facilities and terms and conditions of service of the employees, prior permission from the Ministry shall be obtained

## Chapter-3 Fund, Accounts and Audit of the Authority

### Section 20: Fund of the Insurance Authority

1) The Authority shall have a separate fund of its own.


2) The following amounts shall be credited to the fund as referred to in sub-section 1:


(a) Amounts received from the Government of Nepal,


(b) Amounts received by way of fees from incorporation, prior approval, and licensing of insurance companies,


(c) Amounts received by way of fees from licensing or renewal of insurance intermediaries,


(d) Amounts received by way of the fees for regulatory functions of the authority,


(e) Amounts received from foreign governments, international organisations, institutions or bodies,


(f) Amounts received from any other sources.


3) The approval of the Ministry of Finance shall be obtained in receiving any amount from the government, international organisations, institutions or bodies referred to in clause (e) of sub-section (2).


4) All amounts to be obtained to the fund of the Authority pursuant to sub-section (2) shall be credited to an account to be opened with a licensed bank or financial institution.


5) The expenditures to be made on behalf of the Authority shall be charged to the fund as referred to in sub-section (1).


6) Out of the amounts deposited in the fund maintained as per this section, fifty percent of the amount remaining from the fund after deducting amounts allocated by the authority for operation, management and development of the insurance sector to be used by the authority per year, shall be deposited in provincial reserve fund every year.


7) Other provisions related to the operation of the fund pursuant to sub-section (1) shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 21: Accounting of Income and Expenditure

1) The Authority shall maintain its accounting details, books of accounts and financial statements so as to truly reflect its financial position, as per prevailing Nepal Accounting Standards.


2) The authority shall maintain an internal control system as prescribed for the operation of its activities.

---

### Section 22: Audit

1) The accounts and books of the Authority shall be Audited by the Auditor General.


2) The authority shall conduct its internal audit regularly.

---

### Section 23: Audit Committee

1) The Board shall form an audit committee, making the committee accountable to the board itself, which shall consist of following members:





(a)
One director appointed by the board
 Convener


(b)
Expert on the concerned subject nominated by the board
 Member



2) The Head of Internal Audit Department of the authority shall act as Secretary to the Audit committee.


3)  The functions, duties and powers of the Audit Committee shall be as prescribed in sub-section (1).

## Chapter-4 Provision Regarding License

### Section 24: Restriction on Insurance Business without license

1) No person shall without obtaining license as per this act, carry out or cause to do directly or indirectly, any insurance transactions, business with or without using the name like insurance, re-reinsurance or insurance fund.


2) No person shall use the words insurance, reinsurance, or insurance fund in the name of their firm or company while carrying out any insurance transactions or business or for any other purpose in contravention of sub-section (1).


3) No person shall operate or cause to operate any transaction relating to the insurance business with other person or corporate body other than the insurer licensed under this Act.

---

### Section 25: Incorporation of Insurance Company

1) Any person who is desirous of undertaking an insurance business according to this Act shall have to incorporate it by getting such insurance business registered as a public limited company in accordance with the prevailing laws.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), provided that the insurance companies that have already been incorporated and are in operation during the commencement of this Act are not required to be incorporated.


3) Any person or his family members or a corporate body in which he or his family members have a substantial interest shall not invest more than 15% of the paid-up share capital of the insurance company to be incorporated under this section.


However, it shall not be applicable in case of shares subscribed by the Government of Nepal, Province Government or Foreign Investors.


4) Any person or entity holding shares of insurance companies in limits more than prescribed under sub-section 3, such person shall, within 2 years from the commencement of this Act, maintain the share investment within specified limits.


5) While investing in other insurance companies by any person or their family member, firm, company or institution having investment in one insurance company pursuant to sub-section (3), investment shall have to be made subject to be less than one percent of the paid-up capital of said insurance companies.

---

### Section 26: Prior approval required

1) Any person willing to incorporate a public company as per section 25 to conduct insurance business has to submit an application to the Nepal Insurance Authority along with the prescribed fee for obtaining prior approval, having enclosed therewith the following documents:


(a) Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association of the proposed insurance company,


(b) Feasibility Study report and details about The personal details of applicants are infrastructures of the proposed insurance company,


(c) Personal details of applicants in the form as prescribed by the Authority,


(d) A certified copy of the agreement, if any, made between the applicants prior to the incorporation of the insurance company and its summary,


(e) Evidence of payment of taxes by promoters up to the fiscal year immediately preceding the year in which prior approval is applied,


(f) If any application is made by any body corporate, the following details:


(1) a Copy of the Board’s decision of the corporate body to incorporate insurance in the company,


(2) Memorandum of Association and Articles of association of the concerned company,


(3) Documents proving capacity to make investments,


(4) Any corporate body operating business or transactions subject to a regulatory body under prevailing laws, copy of permission granted by such regulatory body to make investment invest in the insurance business.


(g) Other details or documents specified by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Any foreign insurance company, with prior approval of the Nepal Insurance Authority, may open its branch or incorporate an insurance company in Nepal in a joint venture with a corporate body registered in Nepal or a Nepali citizen.


3) Any foreign company desirous of incorporating an insurance company or opening a branch in Nepal in a joint venture shall submit an application to the Authority along with the prescribed fee for prior approval.


4) While submitting the application pursuant to sub-section 3, the following details and documents shall be included along with the application in addition to the details and document as referred to in sub-section (1):


(a) Copy of licenses of the foreign insurer obtained from the concerned country for carrying on insurance business,


(b) Copy of renewal of such license obtained from the concerned country for carrying on insurance transactions,


(c) Copy of permission granted by regulatory authority of the concerned foreign country to open a branch in Nepal,


(d) Copy of the board’s decision of such a foreign  corporate body to open a branch  or incorporate an insurance company in Nepal in a Joint Venture,


(e) Previous Five year’s audit report and audited financial statements reflecting financial capability,


(f) Name and shareholdings of directors of such foreign company and details about the corporate body in which it has a financial  interest,


(g) Other details and documents are as specified by the Nepal  Insurance Authority.


5) The Nepal Insurance Authority may, if it finds appropriate to grant approval upon examination of the application submitted for prior approval pursuant to sub-section (1) and sub-section (3) and enclosed documents, grant it approval within sixty days after filing the application, in the prescribed format.


6) If an insurer needs to amend its memorandum or articles of association, prior approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority must be obtained.

---

### Section 27: Power to Refuse Prior Approval

1) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in Section 25, the Insurance authority may refuse to issue prior approval to incorporate an insurance business or open a branch in Nepal by a Foreign  insurance company in the following circumstances:


(a) If the name of the proposed insurance company is similar to the name of any previously registered insurance company.


(b) If the name of the proposed insurance company or the activities of the insurer to be carried out are found to be inappropriate or prejudicial to the public interest, good conduct, religion, ethnicity, community or traditional belief.


(c) If based on a study of the feasibility report, details, and documents, other infrastructures submitted by the proposed insurer do not provide grounds to believe that it may carry on insurance transactions in a healthy and competitive manner.


(d) If the objective of the proposed insurance company is contrary to the prevailing laws.


(e) If documents and details as provided in section 26 is not submitted.


(f) If every promoter does not undertake to subscribe to at least TEN THOUSAND shares in the proposed insurance company or if the details about the shares to be conducted are not clearly businesses in addition to the disclosed.
(g) If the proposed insurance company has not proposed to maintain share capital as per Section 36.


(h) If any of the proper has been convicted and sentenced of any offence involving insurance, banking, finance, fraud, forgery, money laundering, corruption, human trafficking, kidnapping, hostage or other similar offences involving moral turpitude and a period of 5 years has not lapsed after serving such punishment.


(i) If the proposed insurance company has included objectives of other business in addition to insurance business.


(j) If the prior approval is not to be granted as per Section 31 of the Act.


(k) If any other promoters or his family members had been blocked as per prevailing acts and three years have not lapsed after removal from such blocklist.


(l) If it is found that an investment in the proposed insurer by the single promoter or his family members or corporate body or group is fifteen percent or more of the paid-up capital.


However, this provision shall not be applicable to the shares held by the Government of Nepal or province Government or Foreign Investors.


(m) If the person or his family member is a substantial shareholder in another insurance company undertaking a similar insurance business as proposed by the insurer.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (1), the Nepal Insurance Authority shall not grant prior approval to undertake more than one insurance business at a time except for the reinsurance business.


3) In case when the Nepal Insurance Authority had denied to grant prior approval, the applicant shall be provided with reasons of refusal, in writing, within 60 days from the date of application.

---

### Section 28: Application to be made

1) Any person who had obtained prior approval as per section 26 and is registered as a company as per prevailing acts, desires to undertake insurance business, shall submit an application to the Nepal Insurance Authority along with following details and documents:


(a) Prior approval was granted by the authority to incorporate the insurance company. approval was


(b) Registration Certificate of the Incorporated insurance company.


(c) Copy of memorandum of association and articles of association of the insurance company.


(d) If the promoter of an insurance company is any company or corporate body, a copy of the previous three year’s audited financial statements and evidence of tax clearance.

(f) the type of insurance business to be undertaken by the insurance company.


(g) A portfolio of the insurance business is to be undertaken by the insurance company. The insurance company is to undertake a portfolio of the business


(h) Particulars of an office building equipped with all infrastructures required by the insurer to carry on financial transactions or,   if such building is to be rented, a copy of the lease agreement and the particulars of the building to be rented, including that there are enough bases of making available insurance services and facilities


(i) Personal details of Chief Executive Officer and Other managerial level officers.


(j) Documents relating to Risk Management System.


(k) Details about investment policies, risk calculation, claims, reinsurance and information technology.


(l) proposed insurance contract and in case of reinsurance company proposed reinsurance contract.


(m) Document evidencing that the amount of the shares undertaken to be subscribed by promoters is paid and deposited.


(n) Details about organisational structure.


(o) Employees’ terms, conditions and facilities by-laws along with their remuneration.


(p) Financial, administration and procurement by laws.


(q) Other details or documents as specified by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) While applying for undertaking insurance business by any branch office of a foreign insurer, it shall provide the following details in addition to those matters mentioned in sub-section (1).


(a) Letter of Consent or approval given by the government of the concerned country or regulatory authority formed according to the laws of the concerned country of the foreign insurer for operating insurance transactions in Nepal by such foreign insurer.


(b) Registration Certificate of Branch registered in Nepal.


(c) Upon obtaining prior approval from Nepal Insurance Authority, if any details and comments submitted under Section 26 are altered, details about such along with a copy of such documents.


(d) Other details or documents as specified by the Nepal Insurance Authority.

### Section 29: Granting License

1) In case the Nepal Insurance Authority, upon examination of the application under Section 28 and the documents enclosed therewith, finds that the applicant is capable of operating an insurance business, after collecting prescribed fees, issue a license within 60 days from the date of receipt of application.


2) While granting license as per sub section (1), the Nepal Insurance Authority shall clearly specify the type of insurance business that can be undertaken by the insurers pursuant to Section 61.


3) Unless any date is mentioned from which the license shall be effective, any issued license under sub-section (1) shall be effective from the date of issuance of such license.


4) The Nepal Insurance Authority may while granting license specify necessary terms and conditions by giving due consideration to the existing position of the insurance company and interest of the insured.


5) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (4), the Nepal Insurance Authority may alter the terms and conditions to be complied with by the insurer while undertaking the insurance business by issuing directives under this act.


6) It shall be the duty of the concerned insurer to abide by such terms and conditions prescribed in sub-sections (4) and (5).


7) The license issued under this section to the Section to the Section insured under this section insured shall not be transferred.


8) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section 34, if the applicant had not submitted the required details and documents as per section 28 or If the infrastructure to operate insurance transactions is not adequate or due to any reasons if the Nepal Insurance Authority thinks that it is not reasonable and appropriate to issue license for operation of insurance transactions for protection of interests of insured, the Nepal Insurance Authority may deny to issue license to operate insurance transactions and in case when the Nepal Insurance Authority had denied to grant license, the applicant shall be provided with reasons of refusal, in writing, within 60 days from the date of application.

### Section 30: Commencement of Insurance Business

1) The insurer shall commence its insurance business within 6 months from the date of obtaining the license.


2) If the insurer cannot commence its business within the period specified in sub-section (1), the insurer may submit an application along with reasons to the Authority before the expiry of such period.


3) On examination of an application received as per sub-section (2), if the authority finds it reasonable, the authority may grant an extension of a further six months.


4) The concerned insurer shall commence its insurance business within six months and provide information to the authority as per sub-section (2).


5) The authority can cancel the license of the concerned insurer if such an insurer cannot commence its business even within the extended period sub-section (2).

### Section 31: Refusal for Prior Approval and License

Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this act, on the basis of market survey, study and analysis, the Nepal Insurance Authority may, by specifying a certain period, prohibit to issue prior approval pursuant to section 26 to incorporate an insurance company or open a branch by foreign insurance company or prohibit to issue a license under section 29.

### Section 32: Can open a branch office

The insurer may, by obtaining prior approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority, open a branch in Nepal or outside Nepal as prescribed.

---

### Section 33: Can open a liaison office

1) Any foreign insurer may open a liaison (contact) office in Nepal after obtaining prior approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority.


Provided that such liaison office shall not undertake insurance business.

### Section 34: Cancellation of License

2) If the authority upon examination of the application under sub-section 1, the authority if it, upon examination of the application under sub-section 1, the authority, if it deems it appropriate, shall cancel the license of one or more portfolios deems appropriate, shall cancel the license of one or more portfolio of a particular insurer.

### Section 35: List to be Published

2) The published list shall include the date of commencement of the insurance business, paid-up capital, insurance charges, claims and other details as deemed necessary by the Authority in accordance with sub-section (1).

## Chapter-5 Provision on Capital Fund and Shares Allotment of Insurer

### Section 36: Paid Up Capital of the Insurer

1) The minimum paid-up capital of the insurer shall be as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority from time to time.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (1), on the basis of the financial or capital market of the country, liabilities of the insurer or the financial position of insurers, the Nepal Insurance Authority may give orders to increase the paid-up capital by prescribing a time limit.


3) On receipt of an order pursuant to sub-section (2), the insurers must fulfil the minimum paid-up capital within the time frame set by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


4) The Nepal Insurance Authority may cancel the license of insurers failing to maintain the paid-up capital as per sub-section (1) or (2).


5) Other provisions relating to capital of insurers shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 37: May order capital reduction

1) Notwithstanding anything in the prevailing laws, the Authority may order the insurer’s paid-up capital reduced under the following circumstances.


(a) If the license of any insurance business (portfolio) that the insurer had been carrying out has been cancelled.


(b)  If the insurer is rendered problematic pursuant to Section 101.


2) The paid-up capital of the insurer shall not be decreased below the limit prescribed by sub-section (1) of section 36.

---

### Section 38: Insurance Fund

1) The Insurer shall maintain an insurance fund in the quantity and ratio prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) The Insurer shall maintain separate funds for each category (Portfolio) of the Insurance Business and the amount to be received from each Insurance Business shall be deposited in the concerned fund.


3) The fund maintained for one category (Portfolio) of Insurance Business shall not be deposited or utilised to bear the liabilities relating to another category (Portfolio) of Insurance Business.

---

### Section 39: Compulsory Reserve Fund

1) Every insurer shall maintain a reserve fund, as specified by the Board, for liability relating to the insurance business.


2) Amounts to be deposited to compulsory reserve fund (CRF) Life Insurer in accordance with sub-section (1):


(a) An amount not less than the total liability as determined by the Actuary on the basis of the Insurance Policies published by the insurer of the Life Insurance Business within Nepal.


(b) An amount not less than the amount specified by the Nepal Insurance Authority for the net insurance premium shown in the income and expenditure of a non-life insurance business.


(c) Fifty percent of the profit earned until the amount equals the paid-up capital of the insurer operating the Non-life insurance business.


3) An insurer may establish other reserve fund for operation of business, as deemed necessary,


4) Other provisions relating to the compulsory reserve fund to be maintained by re-insurance companies shall be as prescribed.


5) Other provisions related to compulsory reserve fund or other reserve funds shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 40: Catastrophic Fund

1) Every insurer shall maintain a catastrophic fund for any losses that may be incurred due to catastrophes that may occur in future.


2) Other arrangements related to the catastrophic fund shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 41: Claim Payable Fund and Reserve Fund

1)  Every insurer shall maintain a claim payable fund and reserve fund and deposit following amounts in those funds:


(a) The amount of the liability for non-payment of the claim within the prescribed period,


(b) Amount for insurance claims, in respect of which an event has occurred in relation to the property and liability insured by the insurer but has not been reported to the insurer (recorded but not reported) or has not been reported sufficiently,


(c) Amount for unexpired risks reserve of the insurer.


2) Other provisions related to claim payable fund and reserve fund shall be as prescribed in sub-section (1).

---

### Section 42: Assets to be maintained more than liabilities

1) The total assets of the insurer shall be more than the total liabilities.


2) For the purposes of sub-section (1), the solvency margin (ratio of total assets to total liabilities) of insurer shall be as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


3) The insurers undertaking insurance business and branches of foreign insurers existing at the time of commencement of this act shall, within the time prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority, maintain the solvency margin as prescribed in sub-section (2).


4) For the purpose of this section, the valuation of total assets and total liabilities shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 43: Restriction on Distribution of Dividend

1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, no insurer shall be allowed to declare and distribute dividends to shareholders unless:


(a) All its preliminary expenses are recovered and all losses sustained until the previous year are recovered and all the funds prescribed by this act and the Nepal Insurance Authority are provisioned,


(b) Paid-up Capital pursuant to section 36 is maintained,


(c) Solvency Margin Ratio pursuant to section 42 is maintained,


(d) Shares allotted to the general public are completely sold and paid up.


2) Every insurer must obtain the approval of the Nepal Insurance Authority before declaring and distributing dividends, as per prevailing law.

---

### Section 44: Allotment of Shares

1) The insurer shall set aside at least thirty percent of its total issued capital for subscription to the general public.


Provided that insurers registered as branch offices of foreign insurers shall not issue and sell their shares.


2) The insurer may allocate five percent of the shares set aside for the general public to its employees pursuant to sub-section (1).


3) The insurer may reduce the shareholding of the promoter shareholders to not less than fifty-one percent of the total issued share capital by converting the remaining shares to public shares.


4) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, the provision related to the allotment of shares of insurance companies incorporated with ownership of the Government of Nepal or the provincial government shall be as prescribed by the Government of Nepal.


5) Other provisions related to the issue and allotment of shares of the insurer shall be as prescribed in the prevailing acts related to securities.

---

### Section 45: Prospectus and Issue of Shares

1) An insurer shall, before making a public offer of its securities, have to submit the prospectus duly approved by the Securities Board to the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Unless the prospectus is submitted pursuant to sub-section (1), the said insurer or anyone on its behalf shall not be allowed to publish the prospectus of such insurer.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in subsection (1), unless the Nepal Insurance Authority receives a written notification of the approval from the securities board to register the prospectus, the authority shall not register such prospectus.


4) An insurer shall issue its shares to the general public only after competition of one year of its insurance business. While making such an issue, the insurer shall have completed an audit of its previous year.


5) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in prevailing laws, an insurance company, while calling upon application from the general public for subscription of its shares, shall demand payment of a hundred per cent amount of the face value of its shares along with its application.

---

### Section 46: Restriction on sold, mortgaged, or transferred

1) The promoter of the insurance company shall not be entitled to sell or pledge any share registered under his/her ownership for at least five years from the date of incorporation of such company.


Provided that if any person had, before commencement of this act, hold shares more than the prescribed limit provided by this act, such person shall, within two years of commencement of this act, sell such excess shares.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), in case of a special circumstance due to the emergence of any obstruction or hindrance in the operation of the insurance business or the promoter shareholder is included in the blocklist as per prevailing laws, shares may be sold or brought amongst promoters by obtaining approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority.


Explanation: For the purpose of this Section, “special circumstance” means failure to hold a meeting of the Board of Directors due to lack of quorum for a consecutive period of three times or a situation of indecision because of disputes amongst Directors.


3) If the promoter of the insurance company wishes to sell or pledge the shares held in their name after five years he may sell or pledge such shares by complying the terms and conditions prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


4) The Chief Executive Officer, Auditor, Company Secretary or the person directly involved in management and account of insurer shall not buy or sell, mortgage or cause to be mortgaged, transfer or transact or give or accept as donation the securities of the concerned insurance company or of its subsidiary company in his/her name or in name of member of his/her family or a firm, company or institution under the control of such person or to any other person until he/she is in such position or until one year from the date of retirement from such position.


Provided that this sub-section shall not hinder to buy and sell securities while issuing bonus shares, rights shares or the shares allotted for employees or issuing new shares or while merging or amalgamating insurance companies in each other according to provisions made in Chapter-14 or while acquiring all assets or liabilities of one insurer by another insurer or while carrying out purchase or sale of securities with the approval of the Nepal Insurance Authority.


5) Other provisions relating to the sale or purchase or other insurer shall be as prescribed by prevailing laws.

## Chapter-6 Board of Directors and Chief Executive

### Section 47: Formation of Board of Directors

1) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in prevailing laws, every insurance company shall have a Board of Directors, along with one independent director, consisting of not less than five and not more than seven Directors.


2) The insurance company directors shall be appointed by the general meeting of the insurance company pursuant to sub-section (1).


Provided that,


(a) The Board of Directors should be appointed by promoters until the first annual general meeting.


(b) In the event of any vacancy in the post of a director prior to the holding of the Annual General Meeting, the Board of Directors should appoint the director at the next General Meeting.


(c) In case shares are held by the Government of Nepal or province government or another insurance company or another corporate body or company or foreign insurer. The concerned Government of Nepal or province government another insurance company or another corporate body or company or foreign insurer may appoint director(s) in a number proportionate to the value of the shares held by it. The concerned government of Nepal, province government, another insurance company, corporate body, company, or foreign insurer shall also appoint an alternate director to work without such a director.


3) No two or more persons from the same family can become directors in the same insurance company or a similar insurance company.


4) The board of directors shall appoint at least one independent (professional) director from the persons with qualifications and experience provided under section 49 and inform about such appointment at the first annual general meeting.


5) A director chosen by the directors from among themselves by a majority decision shall be the chairperson of the Board of Directors.


Provided that an independent (professional) director cannot be chairperson of the Board of Directors.


6) The person appointed as Chairperson of the Board of Directors shall take the oath of honesty and secrecy before the Chairperson of Nepal Insurance Authority, and the members of the Board of Directors shall take the oath of honesty and confidentiality before the Chairman of the Board of Directors in the format as provided in annexure.


7) In case the office of the Board of Directors becomes vacant for any reason, the Board of Directors shall appoint another person as a member until the next General Meeting within thirty-five days of such vacancy.

---

### Section 48: Term of Office of the Director

A director’s term of office is a maximum of four years, and such person may also be re-nominated or reappointed.


Provided that the independent director may be appointed for only a single tenure of office.

---

### Section 49: Qualifications of Directors

1) The person to be appointed to the office of Director shall have to possess the following qualifications:


(a) Having gained at least five years of experience as a director or at the executive level in national or foreign insurance companies or banks or financial institutions or corporate bodies in related fields or at the officer level of Nepal Government or corporate bodies,


(b) Having acquired at least a bachelor’s degree and gained at least three years of experience as a director or at executive level in national or foreign insurance companies or banks or financial institutions or corporate bodies in related fields or as officer level of Nepal Government or corporate bodies, or


(c) Chartered Accountant or person with a master’s degree in the subject of insurance, monetary or banking finance, economics or management, public administration or statistics, mathematics or economics or commercial law.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (1), a person with a master’s degree or equivalent in the subject of insurance monetary or, banking or finance, economics, the management or public administration, statistics or mathematics, economics or commercial law and has gained at least five years of experience as a director or in executive level in national or foreign insurance companies or corporate bodies in a related the field, shall be qualified to be appointed as an independent director.


Provided that the director or family member of such director auditor,   Advisor or substantial shareholder, or any person with a financial interest in the insurance company shall not be qualified to be appointed as an auditor or Advisor or substantial shareholder or any person with a financial interest in the insurance company, shall not be qualified to be appointed as an independent director.

---

### Section 50: Disqualifications of Directors

The following persons shall not be eligible to become a director of the insurance company:


(a) One who has not attained the age of 25 years,


(b) One who is unable to work as a director due to physical or mental illness,


(c) One has been declared bankrupt, as per prevailing laws,


(d) One has been blocked as per prevailing laws, and a period of at least three years has not lapsed after the removal of one’s name from the blocklist,


(e) An incumbent/serving director or an employee of any corporate body carrying out transactions of insurance or banking or financial transactions or any institution collecting any type of deposits or incumbent (existing) director with substantial shareholding or carrying out transactions related to insurance,


(f) An incumbent auditor or an advisor of the concerned insurance company or a person, firm or company who/which is a partner in any kind of contract with the concerned insurance company or has personal interests


(g) Who has acquired the membership in the Stock exchange to act as a securities broker or a merchant banker,


(h) Incumbent Director of another insurance company,


(i) A person who has not subscribed to a minimum number of shares required to subscribe to be eligible for appointment as a director pursuant to the Articles of Association of the insurance company,


(j) A person who has not subscribed to the qualification shares required to hold the position of director,


(k) A person holding any constitutional post or incumbent employee of the Government of Nepal, or a corporate body having ownership of the Government of Nepal, or the Nepal Insurance Authority or of any insurance company, bank or  financial institution,


Provided that nothing in this clause shall prevent from nominating a director in a different c category of insurance company of which shares has been purchased by the Government of Nepal or an institution having under the ownership of the Government of Nepal or insurance company,


(l) Who, having a liability to pay taxes pursuant to the laws in force, has failed to pay such taxes,


(m) If one has not completed a period of five years after serving out the sentence upon being convicted of an offence involving theft, cheating, forgery, fraud, corruption, any offence involving moral turpitude or offence related to insurance or banking or money laundering from a court of Nepal or that of a foreign country,


(n) If action had been taken against a person by a regulatory agency as per the law for committing an act in c contravention of the law and if they have not completed a period of five years after being taken such action,


(o) If one has not served the full sentence or is yet to clear the dues of the fine imposed by a court of law or if one is in arrears to pay a government due,


(p) If one has not completed a period of three years after serving out the sentence provided by the Nepal Insurance Authority due to non-compliance with the directions of the Authority.

---

### Section 51: Discontinue to hold the office of Director

1) No person shall continue to hold the office of a director in any of the following circumstances:


(a) If they are disqualified, pursue to Section 50,


(b) In cases where a motion to remove from the office of a Director is adopted by a majority of the General Meeting at the recommendation of the group from which he/she was appointed to the office of Director that is passed by the shareholders having representation of at least fifty-one percent of the shares of the same group,


Explanation: For the purpose of this Section, “group” means a group of promoters and shareholders from the general public.


(c)  If the resignation tendered by a director from their position is approved,


(d) If the Nepal insurance authority directs the removal of such person from the office, stating that they have carried out activities against the rights and interests of the insured or insurer.


2) If any insurance company has reasons to believe that any of its directors is disqualified from continuing to hold the office of director or any of its directors no longer holds his office, the insurance company shall give a written notice about such disqualification to the Nepal insurance authority within 15 days.


3) On receipt of written notice under sub-section (2), the Nepal insurance authority may issue necessary directions after necessary inquiry and examination.

---

### Section 52: Meetings of Board of Directors

1) The meetings of the board shall be held at least six times in a year.


Provided that the interval between any two meetings shall not exceed two months.


2) The chairperson shall convene a Board of Directors meeting at any time when at least one-fourth of the directors request in writing to convene the meeting and mention the agenda to be discussed in the meeting.


3) The chairperson shall specify the place, date, and time of the board meeting.


4) The chairperson shall preside over board meetings. In the absence of the senior most director shall preside over the meeting chairperson, the meeting shall be presided over by the senior most director.


5) No meeting of the board shall be held unless it is attended by at least fifty-one percent of the total number of directors.


6) The decision of a majority in the board meeting shall be binding, and only in the event of a tie may the chairperson exercise the casting vote.


7) A Separate book shall be maintained for the minutes of the meeting, recording the names of directors present, subject matters discussed, and decisions taken. The book should be duly signed by all the members present.


Provided that if any director puts forward any opinion opposed to or differing from the decision in the course of discussions on any subject in a meeting, they may mention the same in the minute book.


8) Where any agenda in which any member director has a direct or indirect personal interest is to be discussed at the meeting of the board, such a member shall give information as to such interest to the board prior to the commencement of discussion on such matter, and such member shall not take part in such meeting.


9) The board of directors may, if necessary, invite any national or foreign expert in a related field to attend the meeting as an observer.


10) The member of the board of directors shall receive meeting and other allowances, as prescribed by the general meeting within the purview of the directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority, for his presence in the meeting.

---

### Section 53: Functions, Duties and Powers of the Board

1) All functions to be performed and all powers to be exercised by the insurance company, other than those to be performed by the general meeting of shareholders, shall be performed and exercised by the Board, subject to this Act, laws in force and the memorandum of association and articles of association.


2) It shall be the duty of directors to operate an insurance company in the interest of insured or general public shareholders by managing and mitigating overall risks, maintaining proper corporate governance, and guaranteeing that there will be no obstruction in the insurance company’s day-to-day operation.


3) The other functions, duties and powers of the board of directors shall be:


(a) For systematic performance of the operation of the insurance company in a well-managed manner, to prepare and implement necessary rules, regulations, work plans, directives, etc. under this act, relevant laws or Nepal Insurance Authority directives,


(b) To prevent risks or risky situations in the operation of  the insurance  company and to prepare guidelines for risk management, claim payment, management of assets and liabilities, internal control system  budget and other necessary matters as per policies and strategies of the insurance company,


(c) To prepare policies for all activities to be performed by the insurance  company and to supervise the daily operations to manage the insurance company efficiently,


(d) To maintain the capital, capital fund or other moveable and immovable assets as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(e) To regularly monitor the policies adopted by the insurance company, analyse them and provide necessary directions to the management of the insurance company,


(f) To present the audited financial report to the general meeting and


(g) To perform other functions as directed by the Nepal Insurance Authority from time to time.

---

### Section 54: Committee or sub-committee may be formed

1) Except otherwise expressly provided for in this Act that particular work shall be carried out by any particular agency or official, the Board of Directors may form one or more sub-committee/s for any specific purpose subject to the directives of the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) The functions, duties, powers and rules of procedure of the sub-committee formed and the remuneration or allowances receivable by the member for attending its meetings shall be as prescribed by the Board of Directors in accordance with sub-section (1).

---

### Section 55: Delegation of Powers

The board may delegate some of its powers by disclosing the purpose and reasons to any director, a subcommittee formed pursuant to Section 54, the chief executive, or any person acting as the chief executive of the insurance company, on the condition that such power shall be exercised under its supervision or direction.


Provided that management rights shall not be delegated to the board of directors.

---

### Section 56: Management of Insurance Company

1) The insurance company may hire employees as necessary to operate its insurance business.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (1), a person with financial interest in the insurance company shall not be hired as employee.


3) While making management expenses during the operation of the insurance business by the insurer, the interest of the insured shall be protected.


4) The limit of management expenses and other provisions pursuant to sub-section (3) shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 57: Appointment of the Executive Director

1) For the management of an insurance company, the board shall appoint the chief executive of the insurance company subject to this Act and the memorandum of association and articles of association.


2) The chief executive’s office shall be for a maximum of four years, and he or she may be reappointed for the next one-tenure only.


3) Notwithstanding anything specified in sub-section 2, if the performance of the chief executive officer is unsatisfactory, the board of directors can remove them at any time.


Provided that a reasonable opportunity of being heard shall be provided to defend themselves.


4) While making the appointment of the chief executive, a person with the following qualifications and experience shall be appointed and information thereof shall be provided to the Nepal Insurance Authority about the appointment within 7 days:


(a) Having attained a Master’s Degree or equivalent in the subject of insurance, monetary, banking, finance, economics, commerce, management, public administration, statistics, mathematics or commercial law and having work experience of at least five years as an executive level in insurance sector or having work experience of at least eight years as an Executive level in banking or financial sector or having work experience of at least seven years with at least one year in the insurance sector if such person has experience in both insurance and banking or financial sector or,


(b) Chartered Accountant or having bachelor’s or equivalent in the subject of insurance, monetary, banking, finance, monetary, economics, commerce, management, public administration, statistics, mathematics or engineering or commercial law and having work experience of at least ten years as an executive level in insurance sector or having work experience of at least fifteen years as an executive level in banking of financial sector or having work experience of at least twelve years with at least one year in insurance sector if such person have experience in both insurance and banking or financial sector.


5) The following persons shall not be eligible to become a Chief Executive Officer of an insurance company:


(a) If he or she is unable to work as director due to physical or mental illness,


(b) If they have been blocked as per prevailing laws, and a period of at least three years has not been lapsed after removal of their name from the blocklist,


(c) An incumbent/serving Director of the insurance company or his family member or an employee of the insurance company or insurance mediator or advisor,


(d) Served as auditor of such insurance company during previous three years,


(e) Working as securities business person or is a member of securities market,


(f) With financial interest with the insurance company,


(g) Already been chairman of board of directors,


Provided that nothing in this clause shall prevent from nominating a Director or chairman by Government of Nepal,


(h) A person who had been chief executive officer and a period of six months had not expired since he left the post,


(i) If they have not completed a period of three years after serving out the sentence of imprisonment as per prevailing laws,


(j) Having obtained permission of permanent residency of any foreign country,


However, this clause shall not be applicable for appointment of chief executive officer of foreign insurer or insurance company in joint investment with foreign insurer,


(k) If they have been convicted for an offence of theft, cheating, forgery, fraud, corruption, any offence involving moral turpitude or offence related to insurance or banking or money laundering,


(l) Holding any constitutional post.


6) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, insurance company in which majority shares is held by foreign insurer or foreign insurance company may appoint its employee as Chief Executive officer, subject to provisions of this Act.


7) Branch of foreign insurer located in Nepal may appoint a person as a Chief Executive Officer who is qualified and have experience as provided in this Act.


8) Remuneration and other terms, conditions and facilities with regard to service of chief executive officer, shall be prescribed by the board of directors and shall be fixed at the time of appointment itself.

---

### Section 58: Functions, Duties and Power of Chief Executive

1) The major duties of the chief executive officer shall be to undertake the daily management and administrative functions of the insurance company as per the policies passed by the Board of Directors, directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority and prevailing laws.


2) The following are the functions, duties and powers of the Chief executive:


(a) To implement the decisions of the Board and supervise and control the activities and transactions of the insurance company, subject to this Act and the Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association,


(b) To prepare annual budgets and action plans of the insurance company and present them before the Board for approval,


(c) To manage necessary human resources, subject to the personnel bye-laws of the insurance company,


(d) To implement, or cause to be implemented, the decisions of the Board and the General Meetings,


(e) To operate the insurance company as per this Act and directions of the Nepal Insurance Authority and ensure efficient internal control system and risk management in the insurance company,


(f) To present on time all such particulars, documents, decisions, etc., as are required to be submitted by the insurance company to the Nepal Insurance Authority or any other body subject to this Act and the Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association.


3) The Executive Chief shall be accountable to the Board for all of their functions.

---

### Section 59: Technical or Management Service Agreement

1) The insurer may enter into technical or management service contracts or agreements with Nepali Individuals or foreign corporate bodies by obtaining prior approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Before entering into a contract as per sub-section (1), the draft of such agreement shall be presented to the Nepal Insurance Authority for approval.


3) Other technical or management service contracts or agreement provisions shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 60: Able to create a whistleblowing policy

1) In order to comply with this act or rules or regulations made thereunder or directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority, for providing information in secret ways of any acts or activities done or that may be done in irregular ways carried out by Insurer’s management or officials or employees, may prepare and implement a whistle-blowing policy.


2) In accordance with sub-section (1), it shall be the duty of the board of directors of the concerned insurer to protect the professional safety of the person who provides secret information about whistle-blowing done by the insurer’s chief executive officer or officials or auditor or employees or insurance mediators.

---

## Chapter- 7 Operation of Insurance Business

### Section 61: Type of Insurance Business

1) There are three type of insurance business:


(a) Life insurance business,


(b) Non-life insurance business,


(c) Reinsurance business.


2) The insurer operating life insurance business may undertake any portfolio of insurance business as prescribed, subject to the license.


3) The insurer operating non-life insurance business may undertake any portfolio of insurance business as prescribed, subject to the license.


4) The portfolio of insurance business to be operated by reinsurer shall be as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.

---

### Section 62: Insurance Contract

1) Insurance Contract shall be in written form.


2) Essential elements of insurance contract shall be insurance proposal, insurance policy and its annexures and all the ratifications issued in relation to such.


3) While entering into an insurance contract, both parties (i.e. insurer and insured) shall disclose to each other, all the factual information related to proposed risk in absolute good faith or belief or trust.


4) The person getting insured must have insurable interest in the object of insurance.


5) Other provisions related to insurance contract shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 63: Insurance Policy

1) The insurer shall issue an insurance policy in the format as approved by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Insurance contract between insurer and insured shall be effective once insurance policy is issued, and the terms and conditions specified in such insurance contract shall be binding for both parties i.e. insurer and insured.


3) The insured cannot raise any issues in relation to the matters not specified in the insurance contract after expiry of one year form the date of issue of insurance policy.


4) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (3), after issue of insurance policy, if it is found that the insured had, with mala fide intention, concealed some material and persuasive facts related to insurance risks or had submitted false details, the insurer may give directions to rectify such details to the insured at any time or may also withdraw any facilities provided to insured.


5) The insured may transfer the insurance policy of his life insurance to any other person in a matter as prescribed.


6) Other provisions related to insurance policy shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 64: Insurance Premium

1) The insured shall calculate the insurance premium as per the tariff prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Insurer shall not accept insurance risk and issue insurance policy without receiving all insurance premium.


3) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (2), if any practical difficulty arises due to any reason for paying the amount in a lump sum, this Section shall not be deemed to be prohibited to issue an Insurance Policy on the guarantee of a bank or the Nepal Government relating to the payment of the outstanding amount within a specified period.


Provided that insurance policy shall not be issued against bank guarantee of period longer than thirty days.


4) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, nothing shall hinder to issue insurance policy in case of life insurance in instalments and in case of specific type/ category of non-life insurance business by payment of partial amount.


5) If insurer do not receive the amount of insurance premium, in time, from the person or corporate body who has responsibility to collect insurance premium and make payment to insurer, the insurer shall provide information about such to the insured.


6) Other provisions related to insurance tariffs and insurance premium shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 65: Group Insurance Fund

In order to bear liability of any special risk area, the Nepal Insurance Authority may direct insurer to establish or cause to establish an insurance pool.

---

### Section 66: Insurance Not to Be Done

1) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this act, insurance company shall not undertake insurance risk of assets or liabilities of any of the following persons or corporate bodies:


(a) Liabilities to be borne by the insurer itself


(b) Substantial shareholder, director, advisor, auditor employees or their family members of the insurer


(c) Substantial shareholder of the insurer or their family members


(d) Any firm, company or corporate body who have rights to nominate or appoint its’ director


(e) Any firm, company or corporate body in which director of the insurer has financial interest or any firm, company or corporate body in which any of it’s Directors or his/her family member is working as a Managing Agent or partner.


Provided that, this provision shall not restrict any life insurance company to insure the above-mentioned person, within the purview of the insurance policy and terms and conditions and
limitations provided by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, the provisions of sub-section (1) shall not be applicable to the insurance company undertaking insurance of assets or liabilities under ownership of the Government of Nepal.

---

### Section 67: Restrictions on undertaking business in partnership or acquire assets in ownership

1) Insurer shall not undertake any business in partnership or acquire assets in ownership with director, director’s family members, substantial shareholders, chief executive officer, person with financial interest, any firm, company or corporate body in which the insurer or its director is managing agent or partner.


2) If any insurer had undertaken any business in partnership or acquired assets in ownership with director, director’s family members, substantial shareholders, chief executive officer, person with financial interest, any firm, company or corporate body in which the insurer or its director is managing agent or partner, before the commencement of this act, such partnership or assets in ownership shall be terminated within six months of commencement of this Act.

---

### Section 68: Possession of Property Prohibited

1) Except for the benefits or privileges granted to the director, chief executive officer, shareholder, employee or insurance intermediary of the company in accordance with the prevailing law, the property of the insurer shall not be used in any way by the director of the insurer, the director’s immediate family, a fundamental shareholder, a person with a financial interest, any firm, company or organisation in which the insurer or its director is a managing agent or a partner, or the chief executive officer.


2) Insurance company shall not take in rent or use in any other way the assets of director, director’s family members, substantial shareholders, chief executive officer, any firm, company or corporate body in which the insurer or its director is managing agent or partner.

---

### Section 69: Activities restricted to be carried on by Insurance Company

1) An insurance company shall not carry out or cause to carry out the following activities:


(a) Purchase or sell moveable or immovable assets in excess of the limit prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(b) Provide loan or any kind of guarantee or security in loans taken to its director, director’s family members, substantial shareholders, chief executive officer, any firm, company or corporate body in which the insurer or its director is managing agent or partner,


(c) Provide any type of loan to insured except on security of life insurance policy,


Provided that these provisions shall not restrict the insurer to provide loan or advance to its employees as per the provisions of employee services, terms and conditions and facilities bye-laws.


(d) Issue insurance policy in any format other than the format prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(e) To issue any type of insurance other than the one approved by the Authority,


(f) Calculate and make assessment of insurance tariffs contrary to the insurance tariffs prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(g) Make investment in shares or securities of another insurance company undertaking same type of insurance business or making investment in insurance brokers,


Provided that these provisions shall not restrict the insurer to make investment in shares or securities of another insurance company undertaking different type of insurance business, by
obtaining prior approval of the Nepal Insurance Authority.


(h) Make any type of investment in any company, firm or corporate body in which any of the director of insurer is holding position of director or in any in any company, firm or corporate body in which any of the director have personal, financial or occupational interest.


(i) Indulging in any kind of activities to mutually create any type of monopoly or any other type of activities that may create control in insurance business,


(j) Doing or causing other to do any kind of act which that may create an artificial obstruction in the competitive environment of the insurance or any activities that may create controlled practice in the insurance business.


(k) Doing such other acts or causing others to do any such acts prohibited from being done as per this act or rules and regulations made hereunder or directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) If any insurer finds out or has strong reason to believe that that any insured have purchased insurance policy from fund obtained from illegal sources or from offence of assets/ money laundering, such insurer shall not conceal or alter or make payment or conceal or disguise the true source and origin of such fund or assist any person for such purpose.


3) On receipt of information about any person that activities as per sub-section (2), had been done or had assisted in doing such activities or has strong reason to believe, the insurer shall, immediately inform about such to the Nepal Insurance Authority or to the Financial Information Unit established as 1per prevailing laws.

### Section 70: The Insurance Appraisal

1) An insurer engaged in insurance business shall have its financial position, assets, liabilities and financial position assessed by an actuary appointed with the approval of the Authority.


2) The concerned insurer shall submit a copy of the assessment report provided by the insurer pursuant to sub-section (1) to the Authority.


3) Other provisions relating to the assessment of the actuary shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 71: Allocation of Savings

1) In the case of a life insurance policy ensuring participation in profits, the insurer shall, on the basis of the valuation report of the actuary, allocate and make provision for savings from the life insurance business.


2) For the purpose of allocating and provisioning the savings pursuant to sub-section (1), the insurer shall, with the approval of the Nepal Insurance Authority, transfer at least ninety percent of savings to the Life Insurance Fund.


3) Other provisions relating to apportionment and provisioning savings from the life insurance business shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 72: Accepting risk based on the property itself

1) The insurance company shall undertake insurance risk on the basis of its own Net Assets.


2) Other provisions relating to undertaking risks by the insurer shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 73: Investment in Subsidiary Company

1) An Insurance company may, with approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority, incorporate its own subsidiary company or invest in such companies.


2) While granting approval for incorporation of its own subsidiary company or making an investment in such companies pursuant to sub-section (1), the Nepal Insurance Authority may prescribe necessary terms and conditions, and it shall be the duty of the concerned insurer to comply with such terms and conditions.


3) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, an insurer shall not incorporate a subsidiary company or make investments in such companies, which are undertaking the same type of insurance business as that of such insurer.


4) Other provisions related to investments to be made by the insurer shall be as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority from time to time.

---

### Section 74: Obtained Approval While Selling Assets

1) The Foreign Insurance company shall, while selling or pledging any immovable assets located in Nepal or while selling or pledging any movable assets more than the prescribed limits or while taking back such assets to a foreign country, obtain approval from the Nepal Insurance Authority in addition to the approval to be obtained as per prevailing acts.


2) The Nepal Insurance Authority may prescribe necessary terms and conditions when granting such approval pursuant to sub-section (1).

## Chapter-8 Micro Insurance Business

### Section 75:  Micro Insurance is Required

Every insurance company shall undertake a micro-insurance business targeting the low-income, indigent and backward-class sectors.

---

### Section 76: Incorporation of Insurance Company for Micro Business Only

1) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this act, an insurance company may be incorporated only for undertaking a micro-life insurance business or a micro non-life insurance business.


2) The capital of micro-insurance companies to be established as per sub-section (1), shall be as prescribed.


3) Provisions relating to incorporation and licensing of micro-insurance companies to be established as per sub-section (1), shall be as per this Act.

---

### Section 77: Contract to be made

1) Before the appointment of insurance agents to carry on activities related to micro-insurance, the insurer shall enter into a written contract with such agents.


2) When entering into a written contract with such insurance agents, if such agent is a corporate body established per relevant Acts, the prescribed rights should be delegated to such agents.

---

### Section 78: Claim of Micro-Insurance

1) The insured carrying on a micro-insurance business shall pay the claims related to life insurance or insurance of assets or liabilities, as provided in the insurance policy, within 21 days from the date of receipt of the claim, in proper form, from the concerned insured.


2) Other provisions related to claims of micro insurance shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 79: Micro-Insurance Regulation Fees

To obtain regulatory services for micro insurance, every insurer shall pay the Board an amount equal to 0.5 percent of the Gross Premium earned from the Insurance Business in each fiscal year.

---

## Chapter- 9 Re-Insurance Business

### Section 80: Reinsurance Required

The Insurer shall re-insure the portion of the risk which is excess of the risk to be held by the Insurer.

---

### Section 81: Re-Insurance Business

1) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this act, provisions relating to the insurance company undertaking re-insurance business shall be as follows:


(a) The insurance company undertaking a re-insurance business can accept reinsurance of life insurance as well as non-life insurance.


(b) The insurance company undertaking the reinsurance business can accept the re-insurance from the foreign insurance company as well as a foreign re-insurance company or provide a re-insurance service to such foreign insurance company as well as a foreign re-insurance company.


(c) Insurance company doing its business in Nepal shall, compulsorily re-insure its risk, not less than the percentage prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority, with a Reinsurance Company established in Nepal.


2) The insurance company undertaking re-insurance business may do such business on its own or through insurance brokers.


3) Other provisions related to the re-insurance business shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 82: Provisions related to re-insurance contracts

1) While undertaking the re-insurance business, the re-insurance company shall approve the format of its re-insurance contract from the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) The Nepal Insurance Authority may, at any time, give directions to review the terms and conditions mentioned in the re-insurance contract if it has reasons to believe that the contract made between the insurance company and the re-insurance company is against the interest of the insured or such contract do not cover the risks completely


3) Other provisions related to re-insurance contracts shall be as prescribed.

---

## Chapter- 10 Accounts and Audit of Insurance

### Section 83: Accounts to be maintained accurately

1) The insurance company shall have to maintain its accounts, books, and records precisely and correctly.


2) The accounts to be maintained pursuant to sub-section (1) shall have to be maintained according to the recognised principle of a double-entry bookkeeping system reflecting the exact status of transactions of the insurance company.


3) Unless otherwise approved by the Nepal Insurance Authority, the accounts and other statements to be maintained pursuant to sub-section (1) shall have to be maintained at its head/ central office.


4) Other provisions related to accounts of an insurance company shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 84: Preparation of Financial Statements

1) The insurance company shall, within thirty days of the expiry of each quarter, submit its quarterly financial statements, including receipts payment accounts, net assets and changes in it, insurance claims, re-insurance claims and investments.


2) The insurance company shall, within six months of the expiry of a financial year, prepare an audited balance sheet and profit and loss account and submit it to the Nepal Insurance Authority in the format prescribed.


3) The insurance company shall disclose the details of its financial position along with its risk position every three months.


4) Other provisions related to the financial statements of an insurance company shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 85: Audit Committee of Insurer

1) The insurance company which had obtained a license to operate insurance business after commencement of this act shall within thirty days of commencement of its insurance business or the insurance company existing at the commencement of this act which had not formed an Audit Committee shall within thirty days of commencement of this act, form an Audit Committee comprising of three members under the convener-ship of a director representative from general shareholders.


2) The chairman or chief executive officer or chief of accounts department of their close relatives shall not be member of the Audit Committee formed as per sub section (1).


3) The functions, duties and powers of the Audit Committee shall be as follows:-


(a) To review the financial statements of the insurer and ensure the basis, authenticity and reliability of information extracted from such financial statements,


(b) To ascertain whether or not the accounts, budget and internal control mechanism of insurer are appropriate or not,


(c) To ensure whether the purchase/procurement policy of the insurer is appropriate, efficient and economical and to supervise and regulate such purchase/procurement policy,


(d) To ensure whether the accounts, documents and records of internal audit systems  and electronic records are maintained appropriately,


(e) To ensure whether the activities related to calculation of insurance risks, claims, investments, and reinsurance had been done properly or not and to ensure whether the documents related to such activities had been adequately maintained or not,


(f) To ensure whether the accounts, audit balance sheet or financial statements of the insurer or such documents are prepared according to the prevailing law, regulation and directives of the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(g) To recommend names of three auditors for appointment of the external auditor,


(h) To furnish opinion on the subjects as required by the Board of Directors,


(i) To ensure whether or not the insurer had complied with the directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


4) The Audit Committee shall submit a report of its activities to the board of directors of the company.

---

### Section 86: Appointment of Auditor of Insurer

1) The insurance company shall appoint auditor as per prevailing laws.


2) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-section (1), any of the following persons shall not be appointed as an auditor of the insurance company:


(a) Substantial shareholder, director or his\her close relative,


(b) Chief Executive Officer or his\her close relative,


(c) Insurance Mediator of the insurer, employees or his\her family member,


(d) In case of ex-employee, if three years had not expired after leaving from the office,


(e) A person working as a partner of any director or chief executive officer, or employee of the insurer,


(f) A person with financial interest with the insurer or his/her family member,


(g) The insured who had undertaken insurance form the insurer in the amount exceeding the prescribed limit,


(h) A person who had been appointed as auditor of the concerned insurer for three consecutive years,


(i) Member of the Board of Nepal Insurance Authority, an incumbent employee of the Nepal Insurance Authority or their partner or their family member,


(j) A person, who is disqualified to be appointed as auditor or disqualified to continue to hold office as auditor as per prevailing laws.

---

### Section 87: Audit of Insurer

1) An insurance company shall, within three months from the date of completion of the Fiscal Year, have to get the external audit conducted.


2) In case the insurance company not complete the auditing within the period referred to in sub-section (1) and makes a request for an extension of time for auditing, stating reasonable cause, the Nepal Insurance Authority may extend the period up to two months


3) The audit report shall include details in case any remarks or irregularities have been found while conducting the audit and, if such remarks or irregularities have been found, whether a reply from the management of the insurer had been obtained or not about such matter.


4) Notwithstanding anything mentioned elsewhere in this section, Nepal Insurance Authority may also prescribe a separate format for the Long Form Audit Report and in such case, the Auditor shall prepare the audit report as per such format and submit it to the Nepal Insurance Authority


5) The insurance company shall, within thirty days of completion of its audit, submit its financial statements to the Nepal Insurance Authority for approval.


6) The Nepal Insurance Authority may, if necessary for discussion about the matters related to financial statement, call for a director or chief executive officer or auditor or other related person, and it shall be the duty of such person to be present in front of the Nepal Insurance Authority in such case.


7) The insurance company shall not submit its report to any authorities except government authorities or publish or call an annual general meeting without submitting its report to the Nepal Insurance Authority as per sub-section (5).


8) The insurer should publish its audited annual financial statement at least 2 times in national-level newspapers and on the suit website.


9) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in any prevailing laws, if the audit of the insurance company could not be conducted even if time had been extended as per sub-section (2) due to the non-appointment of an auditor, the Nepal Insurance Authority may itself appoint an auditor of such insurance company.


10) Remuneration and other expenses of the auditor appointed pursuant to sub-section (9) shall be borne by the concerned insurance company itself


11) Provisions relating to matters that are to be disclosed in the audit report and other provisions related  to the audit of insurance company shall be as prescribed

---

### Section 88: Right to give order with respect to Audit of Insurer

1) Authority may, if deemed necessary, order the auditor of the insurance company to carry out the following additional functions:


(a) To submit additional information as required by the Nepal Insurance Authority concerning auditing,


(b) To carry out other tests on specific matters as directed by the Nepal Insurance Authority or on matters recommended to the insurance company by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) All the expenses related to re-audit pursuant to sub-section (1) shall be borne by the concerned insurance company itself.

---

### Section 89: Recommending Action

The Nepal Insurance Authority shall write to the concerned regulating authority to remove the name of an auditor who does not perform his/her duties as per this Act, from the panel of the auditors to prohibit him/her to carry out audit of any insurance company for one year to three years.

---

## Chapter- 11 Provisions Related to Insurance Mediators or Other Insurance Service Provider

### Section 90: Work should not be done without a license

No person shall carry out the functions of Insurance mediators without obtaining a license under this Act.

---

### Section 91: License to Insurance Mediators

1) Any person who is desirous of obtaining a license in relation to insurance intermediaries shall submit an application for a license to the Nepal Insurance Authority along with the prescribed documents and fees.


2) On receiving the application per sub-section (1), the Nepal Insurance Authority, after examination of documents, if it finds it appropriate, shall issue a license to carry on insurance intermediaries.


3) Validity of the issued license shall be of 3 years.


4) While granting the license of the Insurance Surveyor under non-life insurance, the license shall be issued to conduct the survey of one type of insurance (Portfolio) only.


However, this provision shall not apply to the companies established with the objective of carrying out the business as an insurance surveyor


5) Any person who had obtained the license of insurance surveyor of any insurance portfolio before the commencement of this act shall choose to carry one insurance portfolio within one year from the commencement of this act.


6) License issued under this act, cannot be used by, handed over or transferred to others.


7) When Issuing Insurance agent’s license, only one license of the same type will be issued.


8) The provisions regarding insurance mediators and other insurance service providers shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 92: Qualifications of Insurance Mediators

1) The qualification of insurance mediators shall be as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


2) Before acquiring the license, insurance mediators must pass the prescribed examination.


3) The license of insurance brokers or third-party insurance facilitators shall be granted only to the following corporate bodies incorporated as per prevailing laws:


(a) With the authorised capital as prescribed by the Nepal Insurance Authority,


(b) The person working as the General Manager of the corporate body shall have 4 years of experience in the Insurance Business,


(c) Meeting other criteria as specified by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


4) The provisions regarding insurance mediators and other insurance service providers shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 93: Responsibilities of Insurance Intermediaries

1) The insurance mediators shall keep a copy of the license at their registered office to be clearly seen.


Provided that individual mediators and surveyors must show the license if requested by the concerned person.


2) The insurance mediators shall abide by the rules or regulations made under this act or the directives or directions provided by the Nepal Insurance Authority.


3) The insurance surveyor shall not evaluate or conduct a survey or cause to evaluate the loss of its close relatives or firms or companies where its family members belonging to the same family or their close relatives have a financial interest.


4) The insurance surveyor shall submit the report to the concerned insurer within the prescribed time.


5) The  Insurance  Surveyor shall employ only licensed insurance surveyors to carry out insurance surveys.


6) An insurance agent of any insurance shall not act as an insurance agent of another insurance of the same type during the same period.


Provided that this provision shall not be deemed to have prevented an insurance agent of an insurer carrying on life insurance business from acting as an insurance agent of an insurer carrying on non-life insurance business and an insurance agent of an insurer carrying on non-life insurance business from acting as an insurance agent of an insurer carrying on life insurance business.


7) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (6), an insurance agent engaged in microinsurance activities may act as a microinsurance agent for more than one insurer.


8) An insurance broker or a third-party insurance facilitator shall not make any investment, guarantee or security or give any loan to any corporate body in which any member of its board of directors is a member of the board of directors or has any financial interest.


9) The work performed by an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider, remuneration or commission, official conduct and other provisions shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 94: Renewal of License of Insurance Intermediaries

1) If an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider wishes to renew its license, it must submit an application to the Authority, attaching the prescribed fee and other details, before the license expires.


2) If the application for license renewal cannot be submitted within the period specified in sub-section (1), the authority may extend the time limit for renewal for a maximum of three months if reasonable cause is produced.


3) If an application for renewal is received within the period pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2), the license shall be renewed for a period of three years if, upon necessary investigation, it is deemed appropriate to renew it.


4) If the license cannot be renewed, the Nepal Insurance Authority should provide a letter explaining the reason for non-renewal.


5) Other provisions regarding the renewal of the license of an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 95: Cancellation of License

1) The license of insurance intermediaries or other insurance service providers should be ipso facto cancelled if it cannot be renewed under section 94.


2) If any insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider submits a written application to the Authority to revoke its license, the Authority may, after investigation, revoke such license as prescribed.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this act, NIA cancel the license in the following circumstances:


(a) Acted against this act or rules or regulations made under this act or directives or directions provided by Authority or


(b) If it is proved that such Insurance Mediator or Other Insurance Service Provider had acted against the interest of the insured.


4) Before revoking a license pursuant to Sub-section (3), the Authority shall give the concerned insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider a reasonable opportunity to present its defence.

---

### Section 96: Non-Renewal

Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this act, if the insurance intermediaries are not able to fulfil their responsibilities as per section 93 or have acted against the interest of the insured, Nepal Insurance Authority shall not renew their licenses.

---

### Section 97: Prohibition to act

1) No person shall act as an insurance agent or insurance intermediary without a license or whose license has not been renewed.


2) If any insurer has appointed insurance intermediaries who have not complied with sub-section (1), all the responsibilities shall be borne by the insurer himself.


3) Any liability arising from any insurance business activity carried out by an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider whose license has been revoked during the term of the license shall be borne by the concerned insurer.


4) No insurance agent or intermediaries shall act as a director or, chief executive officer or employee or, advisor of Insurer.

---

## Chapter- 12 Regulation, Inspection and Supervision

### Section 98: To Regulate, Inspect and Monitor

1) The Authority may regulate, inspect, or monitor the activities of any insurer, insurance intermediary, other insurance service provider, or the person or organisation concerned with such insurer, insurance intermediary, or other insurance service provider at any time.


2) In case having suspicion or receiving a complaint about the business of any insurer, insurance intermediary, or other insurance service provider, the Authority may regulate, inspect or monitor at any time by assigning any expert or employee of the Authority to make onsite supervision of the office of the insurer or the insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider or making the insurer to bring the related documents, accounting and financial statements, insurance policies or deeds, related, reports and information in authority.


3) While performing regulation, inspection and monitoring of the insurance business as mentioned in sub-section (1), the authority shall follow internationally established principles, values, standards and, best practices and customs.


4) The expert or employee assigned for supervision as per sub-section 2, may demand, observe, inspect examine, research, investigate the logbook, data, information, notices, reports, electronically created information or any other necessary documents, ask for original or duplicate copies or inquiry about the same with the concerned personnel.


5) It shall be the duty of the insurer, insurance intermediary, other insurance service providers, insured, and persons related to the insurance business to provide the information requested under sub-section (4).


6) If the expert or employee assigned to regulate, inspect or monitor under this Section finds some faults or weakness in the works performed by the insurer, its workings and actions or documents, any actions and working or documents being suspicious or not being as per the rules and regulation framed under this Act or directives issued by the Nepal Insurance Authority, the expert or employee may make concerned official to give detail explanation or description.


7) The expert or employee assigned for regulation, inspection or monitoring, if realised, may issue any directions or instructions by informing the authority, and it shall be the duty of the insurer or concerned personnel to follow this instruction.


8) The expert or employee assigned to regulate, inspect or monitor shall submit the report of inspection or monitoring to the Authority within the time determined by the Authority.


9) In case some sort of cooperation is sought by the Authority from the Government of Nepal, any bodies of the Nepal Government, any bodies of provincial or local level, other regulatory bodies or organisations to perform or to be performed the function of regulation, inspection or monitoring under this Section it shall be the duty of these authorities or organisations to co-operate to the Authority


10) Other provisions related with regulation, inspection or monitoring of insurance business shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 99: Foreign Insurance Company shall inform to the Authority

1) Foreign Insurance Company shall inform the Authority in writing before performing inspections or monitoring their branch established in Nepal.


2) Foreign Insurance Company company shall, after conducting inspection or monitoring in accordance with sub-section (1) at its branch office opened in Nepal, prepare a report and submit a copy thereof to the Authority.

---

### Section 100: Regulation Fee

1) Every insurer obtaining the regulatory service by the Authority shall pay a regulation fee to the Authority every year in lieu of providing regulatory service by it to the insurer, calculated at the rate of 0.75  percent of the total premium earned from insurance fees by the insurer.


2) The insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider shall pay the Authority regulation fees as prescribed in lieu of receiving regulatory service from it.


3) The period, procedure, delay fee and other arrangements for payment of regulation fee as per sub-section (1) and (2) shall be as prescribed.

---

## Chapter- 13 Problematic Insurer

### Section 101: Insurer Deemed to be Problematic

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, the Authority may declare an insurer as problematic for the sake of interest of the insured if the following conditions exist:


(a) In case the insurer fails to maintain the minimum paid-up capital and capital fund to be maintained,


(b) In case the ratio of total assets and liabilities (solvency margin) is not maintained,


(c) In case it is proved that the promoter shareholders of the insurer have established the insurance company with the earnings through fraud or embezzlement, money laundering, corruption, financial investment in terrorist activities, human trafficking,


(d) In case it is proved that the insurance company was established or the license was received by the insurer by deceiving, forgery, or providing false statements or preparing or submitting fake documents,
(e) In case acts performed against the interest of the insured, shareholders, creditors of insurers or the general public repeatedly,


(f) In case the insurer did not follow the instruction provided by the Authority to be merged with another insurer,


(g) Violating this Act or Rules and By-laws framed under this Act, as well as existing laws, terms, and conditions mentioned at the time of issuing the license or the instructions and orders issued by the Authority, repeatedly,


(h) In case insurance fraud is conducted by the insurer,


(i) If the regulation, inspection, monitoring performed by the Authority or audit report or actuary report discloses the inability of the insurer to fulfil its liabilities or being impossible the same,


(j) In case information on the situation pursuant to sub-section (3) of Section 105 is provided,


(k) If it is observed that the insurance business may not be operated continuously due to disputes between the directors regarding the operation and management of the insurer,


(l) Being failure to submit evidence of improvement within the prescribed period as per the instruction provided by the Authority while imposing a full or partial ban on the insurance business being operated by the insurer,


(m) The insurer failed to fulfil its liability repeatedly.


2) Before declaring an insurer as problematic pursuant to sub-section (1), the Authority shall provide notice of 15 days to the insurer to submit clarification with evidence to prove that there is any reason for not declaring so.


3) In case the insurer being failure to submit the clarification within the given period in accordance with sub-section (2) or the clarification provided by the insurer is not deemed satisfactory and reliable, the Authority shall declare such insurer as problematic insurer and publish the information about the same through at least two national daily newspapers and Authority’s own website.


4) Any insurer which has become insolvent or there is the possibility of occurring such situation as per the prevailing laws, being failure to make payment of the liability to the insured, which is to be paid or net assets being decreased to half or less of the paid-up capital, shall inform immediately to the Authority.

---

### Section 102: Measures For the Reformation of Problematic Insurer

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the existing laws, the Authority shall give any of the following orders prescribing the period for the reformation of the insurer being problematic pursuant to Section 101:


(a) To increase capital, cause to issue the new shares or cause to make payment of amount instead of the shares remaining as unpaid within the prescribed period,


(b) To prohibit the distribution of the dividend to be paid to the shareholders for a fixed period or cause to issue bonus shares in instead of dividend including the dividend prohibited for distribution,


(c) To determine limitation for a fixed period on the payment of remuneration, privileges to be made to the directors, chief executive or payment of any other amount,


(d) To determine the new limitation on the management expenses of the insurer,


(e) To fulfil liability of the insurer by selling any property of the insurer,


(f) To make restrictions to extend the branches or cause to make merge or cause to make close existing all branches


(g) To cause a decrease in  the paid-up capital of the insurer,


(h) To prohibit operating any or all types of insurance business allowed to perform under this Act for a fixed period,


(i) cause to reduce the number of board of directors, executives or employees within the period prescribed or cause to make changes  or reconstitute the board of directors,


(j) To suspend rights and authorities of shareholders of the insurer or prohibit to transferring shares of such shareholders or cause to make other shareholders or the insurer itself buy out the share of such shareholders or cause to sell to another person or organisation,


(k) In case the insurer is registered as a listed company, write to remove such insurer from the stock market to prevent any activities against the interest of the general public,


(l) To perform or cause to perform other appropriate actions as deemed reasonable by the Authority to make the insurer competent from financial and managerial point of view.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the existing laws, if the insurer does not reduce the directors, executives or employees within the period prescribed pursuant to clause (i) of sub-section (1), the Authority itself may remove such directors, executives or employees.


3) In case the financial or managerial condition of the insurer could not be improved within the period provided by the Authority to adopt the remedial measures pursuant to sub-section (1), the insurer shall be provided with a notice of thirty days to submit clarification with evidences if there is any reason for not taking the insurer under control.


4) If the explanation submitted by the insurer within the time limit given pursuant to sub-section (3) is found to be reasonable, the Authority may grant the insurer a maximum period of six months to make the desired improvements.


5) If the insurer being failure to make expected improvement even within the period pursuant to sub- section (4), the Authority shall form a Special Administration Team (SAT) pursuant to Section 103 and take operation and management in its control either by suspending or not suspending the Board of directors.

---

### Section 103: Special Administration/Management Team

1) The Authority may constitute a Special Administration Team consisting of three persons possessing prescribed qualifications for the operation and management of the insurer taken under control by the Authority pursuant to sub-section (5) of Section 102, and the Authority may carry forward the operational and managerial activities through this team.


2) The tenure of the Special Administration Team shall be 1 year, and the Authority may extend the tenure for an additional one year based on appropriateness or may dissolve the team.


3) In case performance is unsatisfactory, the Authority may sack (remove) all or any member of the Special Administration Team as prescribed.


4) The Authority may appoint another person possessing the prescribed in accordance with sub-section (1) qualification for the remaining period on the post in case the member of the special administration team is sacked by the Authority before the completion of prescribed tasks.


5) The appointment procedure, remuneration, privileges and other provisions related to the members of the special administration team shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 104: Functions, Duties and Powers of Special Administration Team

1) The special administration team formed pursuant to Section 103 may exercise all the authorities of the General Meeting, Board of Directors, Chief Executive or any other official of the insurer.


2) By remaining under sub-section (1), the team shall have the following power regarding to manage the movable and immovable assets, to carry out resolution (settlement) of any property and liability of the Insurer concerned and operating such Insurer:


(a) To transfer ownership or handover any or all shares issued by the insurer or any equipment to the name of another person or organisation as prescribed,


(b) To acquire any or all property of the insurer or its subsidiary company and sell the same, access and control over the office, ledgers, records and information system,


(c) To acquire or sell the property or rights of the insurer or the same situated with another person or organisation,


(d) To transfer certain rights and liabilities of the insurer to the other person,


(e) To sell the shares of the insurer that remained unsold,


(f) To cancel any or all the shares and other financial instruments issued by the insurer,


(g) To issue other instruments related to share or capital,


(h) To hand over any or all portfolios of the insurance business being operated by the insurer to another insurer,


(i) To persuade to submit the documents deemed as necessary from the concerned person or organisation to carry forward the activities of the team,


(j) To perform or cause to make perform necessary tasks in order to protect the interest of the insured.


3) The team shall not have an obligation to receive consent of promoter, shareholder, insured, insurance intermediary of the insurer, including any person or organisation of public or private sector and also shall not have to inform any person, firm, company or organisation except otherwise mentioned in this Chapter, while carrying out management under this Section.


4) The special administration team may perform or cause to perform the following works on behalf of the insurer:


(a) To give continuation to the works being performed by the insurer and perform all necessary works to give continuation,


(b) To postpone or stop any or all the activities being performed by the insurer,


(c) To withhold the payment of insurance risk assessment or insurance liability or to determine the limitation for the same,


(d) To appoint necessary employee, official or consultant for the insurer,


(e) To summon meeting of shareholders, insured, insurance intermediary, employees or officials to discuss on subjects deemed as necessary and take decision thereof,


(f) To undertake any legal defence or litigate on behalf of insurer,


(g) To perform or cause to perform other necessary activities related with the activities of the insurer.


5) If the special administration team considers that it is appropriate to merge or to merge the insurer being under its control, the team shall propose with the Authority, along with the reasons thereof.


6) Notwithstanding whatever matter has been written in existing law, while selling the property of the Insurer, the special administration team may sell by adopting transparent and professional perspective with more than one way of auction, sealed bid, negotiation and so on, after determining minimum price on the ground prescribed.


7) The special administration team shall submit the statement including the financial statement, property selling plan of that organisation and team’s purview thereof, anticipation of liability of that organisation or its mode of payment, actual transfer of properties and estimation of liability to the Authority from time to time.


8) If the special administration team considers the necessity of performing the works like the sale of properties, collection of properties, and anticipation of liabilities, it may hand over such work to any entity or company which is capable of performing such works.


9) All the expenses incurred by the special administration team in performing the works as per this section shall be borne by the Insurer, and the limitation of such expenses shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 105: Official to be Suspended

1) The directors and chief executive officers of the insurer shall be automatically suspended for the duration of the period during which the special management group takes control of the insurer.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), the special management group may, if it deems appropriate, lift the suspension of the directors and chief executive officers of the insurer and depute them to perform the specified work.


3) The special management group may, at any time, remove a person who does not perform the work assigned by the special management group pursuant to sub-section (2).

---

### Section 106: Notice of the Work

1) The Special Administration Team  shall give notice, including the following particulars, to every office of the insurer within 3 days from the date of commencement of performing the job, taking the insurer under into its control:


(a) Date and time of beginning of work after taking the insurer under control,


(b) Description of the revoked authority and responsibility of the person handling the main management of the concerned insurer,


(c) The procedure, time and place for claiming insurance by the insured,


(d) The procedure, time and place for claiming various claims by other different parties.


2) The notice pursuant to sub-section (1) shall be published every week for a period of four weeks in at least two national daily newspapers and also through its website.


3) The information regarding to the condition of the insurer taken under the control of the Special Administration Team, changes seen in the authorised person and his/her authority shall be submitted to the Authority, Securities Board of Nepal and the Office of Company Registrar

---

### Section 107: Preparation of New Financial Statements

1) A New financial statement disclosing the insurer’s actual situation under the Special Administration Team’s control shall be prepared as prescribed.


2) The financial statement prepared pursuant to sub-section (1) and detailed information related thereto shall be submitted to the Authority.

---

### Section 108: Provisions After the Achievement of Improvement in Condition of the Insurer

If it is felt that improvement is seen in the condition of the Insurer taken under the control by the Special Administration Team it may take following actions:


(a) To release the suspension of the suspended Board of directors,


(b) To hand over the management of the insurer to the Board of Directors of the insurer,


(c) To form a new Board of directors amongst the shareholders of the Insurer and provide instruction to convene the General Meeting within six months,


(d) To convene a General meeting of the Insurer and form a new Board of Directors.

---

### Section 109: Provisions to be made in Case improvement Not Achieved in the Condition of the Insurer

If it is felt that improvement is not achieved in the condition of the Insurer taken under the control by the Special Administration Team, it may take the following actions:


(a) To transfer the forfeited shares by selling the same in auction within the time prescribed,


(b) To recommend to the Authority to merge the insurer into another insurer within the time prescribed,


(c) To recommend to the Authority to cancel the license of the insurer by handing over the insurance business of another insurer,


(d) To recommend to the Authority to make improvements in the condition of the insurer pursuant to Section 110,


(e) To recommend to the Authority to initiate the process of insolvency of the insurer.

---

### Section 110: Recommendation for Improving Financial Condition of the Insurer

1) If it is observed that there is possibility of causing negative effect to the overall insurance sector while advancing the process pursuant to clauses (a), (b), (c) or (d) of Section 109 due to not achieving the improvement of the insurer taken into control under Section 102, the special management team shall mention this information and prepare a report incorporating the position of the assets and liabilities of the insurer to prevent to creating such situation ad shall submit the report to the Authority.


2) After receiving the report pursuant to sub-section (1), if a negative effect is seen to occur in the entire insurance sector while carrying out management or resolution of such Insurer, and if it is seen that financial condition can be improved if the Government of Nepal invests in such Insurer, the Authority shall recommend such description, along with its opinion, to the Ministry of Finance.


3) In case the recommendation is received pursuant to sub-section (2), the Ministry of Finance may make an investment in share ownership of the insurer to improve the financial position of the Insurer.


4) In accordance with sub-section (3), the Ministry may sell its investment of share ownership through bidding within five years after the achievement of improvement in the financial position of the Insurer.

---

### Section 111: Other Provisions Regarding Special Management of The Insurer

1) No one is supposed to bar, obstruct, interrupt or interfere or get the right to access and control of the Special Administration Team barred, obstructed, interrupted or interfered.


2) If anyone does or causes anyone to do anything contrary to sub-section (1), the Special Administration Team may request work committed by the administration or the police concerned for necessary help.


3) If a request is made pursuant to sub-section (2), the body concerned shall have to help to maintain access and control of the Special Administration Team upon the office, property, record, and account book and information technology system by managing essential security.


4) The person being aggrieved with loss by the activities of the Special Administration Team or the Authority under this Chapter may file a petition to the High Court within 60 days from the date of submission of the report by the Special Administration Team to the Authority or in case of dissolution of the team, within sixty days from the date of dissolution.


5) The High Court may provide reasonable financial compensation to the aggrieved party in the following conditions:


(a) Any actions performed beyond the authority during the course of management of the insurer taken into control,


(b) Not completing the settlement process pursuant to this Act,


(c) Performing managerial action of the insurer taken into control without reason sufficient base & reason.


6) The Special Administration Team shall practice the authority in a transparent and justifiable way so as to safeguard the interest of the concerned insured and other concerned parties.


7) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, the insured, shareholders or creditors shall not be allowed to file a case against the works performed by the directors and officials of the concerned insurer to facilitate the management process of the insurer taken under control by the Authority or in the course of implementation of a decision of Special Administration Team.

---

### Section 112: Final Report to be Submitted

In regard to the insurer taken into the control of the SAT, the team shall submit the final report to the Authority, incorporating the prescribed information regarding the activities performed by it, within thirty days from the date of completion of works to be done pursuant to this Act or completion of its tenure or, in case of dissolution of the team by any reason, within 30 days from the date of dissolution.

## Chapter- 14 Transfer of Insurance Business and Merger of Insurer

### Section 113: Insurance Business May Be Transferred

1) Out of insurance business being performed by any insurer, entire or partial portfolio of insurance business may be handed over to another insurer performing the insurance business of same portfolio by receiving an approval from the Authority.


2) If any insurer desires to take or hand over the insurance business pursuant to sub-section (1), the concerned insurers shall pass the special resolution in their respective General meetings, make a mutual agreement, and submit a joint application to the Authority along with documents as prescribed.


3) The Authority may grant approval as prescribed according to the agreement if it concludes that the transfer of insurance business is reasonable while investigating the application and comprehending additional matters if required pursuant to sub-section (2).


4) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (3), the Authority shall not grant approval to take or hand over the insurance business if it seems possible to cause detriment or loss to the insured, increase unhealthy competition in the insurance business or establish a monopoly or controlled commercial practice.


5) After the assignment of the insurance business pursuant to sub-section (3), obtaining approval for assigning from Nepal Insurance Authority, the assets and liabilities of such insurer shall be transferred to the insurer which takes over the insurance business by assignment.


6) Other provisions concerning with assignment of insurance business shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 114: Insurer May Merge or be Amalgamated

1) Insurers engaged in the same type of insurance business may merge or be amalgamated with each other in accordance with the prevailing law with the approval of the Authority.


2) Before initiating the merger or amalgamation proceedings pursuant to sub-section (1), both the merging and the merging insurers shall submit a joint application to the Authority for approval along with the prescribed documents.


3) While investigating upon the application pursuant to sub-section (2), if it is observed as reasonable to merge or to be merged for the insurers, the Authority shall grant theoretical approval (approval in principle) to commence the process of merging.


4) After receiving in-principle approval pursuant to sub-section (3), the merging or merging insurers shall appoint a person qualified to be an auditor of the insurer to evaluate the assets, liabilities and business of their respective companies and have the valuation conducted as prescribed and submit an application to the Authority along with the details.


5) The Authority may grant approval for a merger (to merge or be merged) if it is observed that the merger is reasonable to the applicant insurers while investigating the application received pursuant to sub-section (4) and comprehending additional matters if it required so.


Provided that the Authority shall not provide approval to the insurers to merge or to be merged if there seems the possibility of causing detriment or loss to the insured, increasing unhealthy competition in the insurance business or establishment of monopoly or controlled commercial practice if insurers amalgamated together.


6) Other provisions relating to merging together (to merge or to be amalgamated) of insurers shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 115: The Authority may give directions to merge or to be amalgamated

1) The Authority may issue direction to any insurer to merge with another insurer or to be amalgamated, prescribing the period in the following conditions:


(a) Recommendation made by the Special Administration Team to the Authority pursuant to clause (b) of Section 109 to merge insurer with other insurer, or


(b) If any director or promoter of the insurer has a financial, professional or personal interest, directly or indirectly, in another insurer.


2) In case of direction provided as in accordance with sub-section (1), the insurer shall submit a merger proposal to the Authority within the prescribed period for the approval and the insurer shall be merged with another insurer after receiving approval from the Authority.

---

### Section 116: Insurer Many Acquire Another Insurer

1) Any insurer may acquire another insurer performing the same kind of insurance business.


2) If an insurer desires to acquire another insurer pursuant to Sub-section (1), both insurers shall pass a special resolution in relation to the same from their respective General meeting and shall
submit joint application to the Authority for obtaining theoretical approval (approval in principle).


3) In accordance with sub-section (2), investigating upon the application, if it seems reasonable to grant theoretical approval, the Authority may grant theoretical approval on the following grounds:


(a) If it seems reasonable to acquire the targeted insurer by another insurer due to the reason that the targeted insurer being failure to fulfil its short-term or long-term liabilities,


(b) If it is reasonable to acquire the targeted insurer by another insurer due to the targeted Insurer’s failure to maintain paid-up capital pursuant to Section 36 or failure to maintain capital funds to be maintained under this Act,


(c) If the targeted insurer is unable to fail, issue shares with the general public within the prescribed period.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (3), the Authority shall not grant approval to the insurer for acquisition as if acquiring one insurer by another insurer, it seems possible to cause detriment or loss to the insured, increase unhealthy competition in the insurance business or establishment of monopoly or controlled practice of any insurer.


5) The insurer who has received theoretical approval from the Authority pursuant to sub-section (3) shall appoint a valuator possessing qualifications for being an auditor of an insurer to submit a report performing an assessment on the following matters:


(a) Overall financial position of the acquiring insurer and targeted insurer, including the valuation of assets, liabilities and business,


(b) General anticipation of the possible effect on insurance system if targeted insurer is acquired by the acquiring insurer and the business plan of acquirer insurer,


(c) Management of employees of targeted insurer after acquisition,


(d) Management of short-term and long-term liabilities of the targeted insurer after acquisition,


(e) Other particulars as prescribed.


6) The valuator appointed pursuant to sub-section (5) shall submit one copy of the study report prepared by him to the Authority and one copy to the acquiring insurer as well.


7) If it seems appropriate to acquire targeted insurer from the report pursuant to sub-section (6) the Authority shall grant final approval for making acquisition of such insurer.


8) After receiving the approval pursuant to sub-section (7), both insurers shall make a mutual agreement in relation to the management of assets and liabilities of the targeted insurer, and the acquiring insurer shall purchase the defined number of promoter shares or other shares of the targeted insurer.


9) Other provisions related to acquisition of insurer shall be as prescribed.

## Chapter- 15 Liquidation of Insurance Company

### Section 117: Liquidation of Insurance Company

1) No insurer shall be liquidated notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws.


2) If any insurer desires to be liquidated, an application including an action plan of voluntary liquidation shall be submitted to the Authority having a decision made from the General meeting.


3) On receipt of a request from an insurer pursuant to sub-section (2), the Authority may grant approval to proceed with the liquidation process prescribing the conditions if the Authority is convinced that the insurer is able to pay its entire liabilities while making an investigation upon the application of the insurer.

---

### Section 118: Conditions Relating to Liquidation of Insurer

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, if the approval pursuant to section 117 is received from the Authority, it shall be deemed as the commencement of liquidation process from the date of approval received.


2) The following activities shall be performed while liquidating the insurer:


(a) The notice concerning the insurer being in the process of liquidation is to be published at least twice in two national-level daily newspapers and through the Authority’s website within thirty-five days from the date of approval received from the Authority,


(b) The notice concerned with the insurer being in the process of liquidation to be provided to all insured, creditors or concerned persons and the Office of Company Registrar within thirty-five days,


(c) The amount under insurance policy to be paid to the insured, the amount of loan to be paid to the creditors, and all other liabilities to be fulfilled by the insurer within the period prescribed by the Authority,


(d) The amount to be paid to the insured as per clause (c), which has not been matured for payment as per the insurance policy, shall be deemed as having been matured ipso facto for the payment on the last day of the period prescribed by the Authority,


(e) While making payment or fulfilling the liabilities, if any insured, creditor or concerned person is absent to receive the payment or the insurer being failure to make payment by any reason, the amount equivalent to the fulfilment of all the liabilities in accordance with clause (c), the conditions determined by the Authority to be deposited in a separate account in the bank as fixed by the Authority,


(f) The insurer shall not perform any act or use any authority that will create any liability after the approval received from the Authority, except that it will be necessary for the liquidation.


3) The license of the insurer shall automatically be cancelled from the date of completion of the liquidation process by the Authority.


4) The Authority may formulate and implement the necessary directives relating to the liquidation of the insurer.

---

### Section 119: Power of The Authority to Initiate the liquidation Process

Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act or in the prevailing laws, the Authority may proceed the insolvency process of any insurer in the following conditions if there are sufficient grounds to prove the existence of these conditions:


(a) The liabilities of the insurer being more than the assets for three consecutive fiscal years,


(b) Being failure to fulfil the liabilities being matured for payment or payment to be made immediately till the date prescribed,


(c) If it is decided to commence the process of Insolvency pursuant to clause (e) of Section 109,


(d) If it is found that the insurer, its director or Chief executive has worked against the interest of the insured and repeatedly violated the directions provided by the Authority for its reform,


(e) Capital fund of the insurer being negative for three consecutive years,


(f) If the license of the insurer has been cancelled pursuant to Section 137,


(g) If an application is submitted by the insurer for liquidation pursuant to Section 117.

---

### Section 120:   Appointment of a Liquidator

1) The Authority shall appoint a liquidator to precede the actions pursuant to Section 119 and shall publish this information at least twice in two national-level daily newspapers and on the Authority’s website.


2) In accordance with sub-section (1), all insurance business operated by the insurer shall ipso facto be postponed after the Authority appoints a liquidator.

---

### Section 121: Priorities on the Payment of Liabilities in Case of Liquidation of the Insurance company

Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, in case the insurer is dissolved or insolvent, payment of liabilities shall be made on the basis of the following order of priority:


(a) Expenses incurred for liquidation or insolvency of the insurer,


(b) Amount to be paid or remained unpaid to the insured,


(c) Taxes, duties and fees to be paid to the Government of Nepal as per the prevailing laws,


(d) Amount of regulation fees to be paid to the Authority,


(e) Remuneration or commission to be obtained by insurance intermediary,


(f) Amount remained to be paid to another insurer instead of the insurance,


(g) Payment of salary, allowances, provident fund, gratuity and other amount due to employees which remained as unpaid


(h) Other liabilities to be borne by the insurer,


(i) Any other liabilities to be paid or fulfilled by the insurer,


(j) Amount to be received by shareholders.

---

### Section 122: Insolvency of the Branch Office of Foreign Insurance Company Situated in Nepal

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, the insurance transactions of the branch office of a foreign insurance company situated in Nepal shall automatically be closed as soon as the insolvency process against the foreign insurance company commences as per the laws of the concerned country.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, if it is observed that the liability of the branch office of a foreign insurance company situated in Nepal is affected due
to the commencement of insolvency against that foreign insurance company as per the laws of the concerned country, the branch office shall immediately provide the information to the Authority, including details of properties bold by the branch office in Nepal to fulfil the liabilities created from the Insurance business being operated in Nepal.


3) If the information pursuant to sub-section (2) is received by the Authority, it shall appoint a valuator and cause to make the valuation of liability of insurance business operated in Nepal by that branch and the properties owned by that office in Nepal.


4) In accordance with sub-section (3), the valuator shall have to submit a report disclosing the amount pursuant to Sections 38, 39, 40 and 41 and detail of other current and fixed assets, including their valuated amount and amount of immediate and matured liability instead of an insurance policy issued, amount to be paid to the Government of Nepal and other amount and liabilities to be paid.


5) After the report pursuant to sub-section (4) is received, the Authority shall withhold, as prescribed, the amount instead of the liabilities to be fulfilled by that office in Nepal in such a way that the insurer may strictly be prohibited to expend that amount.


6) In accordance with sub-section (5), the liabilities of that office shall be fulfilled from this amount as prescribed.


7) In accordance with sub-section (6), the amount remaining after fulfilling the liabilities may be repatriated to the concerned country as per the prevailing laws.


8) If it is observed from the report pursuant to sub-section (4) that the liability created due to operating insurance business in Nepal towards the insured and other liability may not be fulfilled, Nepal Insurance Authority shall give direction to manage the required amount so that all the liabilities will be fulfilled.


9) In accordance with sub-section (8), in case such direction is given, it shall be the obligation of the concerned branch office to manage that amount.


10) In case the branch office cannot manage the amount as per sub-section (9), the Authority shall request the Government of Nepal to manage to acquire the said amount from the regulatory body of the concerned country through a diplomatic channel.


11) In case of failure to fulfil liability as per sub-section (10), the Authority shall manage the necessary amount by making the Insured Interest Protection Fund bear the amount.


12) The Authority may request the Government of Nepal to fulfil the liability in case the liability is not fulfilled as per sub-section (11).

## Chapter- 16 Insurance Claim

### Section 123: Settlement of Insurance Claim

1) If an insurance claim has been presented as mentioned in an insurance policy to the insurer, the insurer shall evaluate the loss if required and settle the claim within the prescribed period from the date of presentation of the claim made in case there is not being the necessity of evaluation of loss.


2) If the insured or his/her, pursuant to the prevailing laws, is not found within the prescribed period or payment for the claim cannot beneficiary pursuant to the prevailing laws is not found within the prescribed period or, payment for the claim could not be made, such amount instead of claim shall be deposited in ‘unclaimed fund’ with the condition that it shall be paid at the time claim is made.


3) If the person entitled to receive the amount instead of insurance claim is not found, the insurer shall keep evidence of searching the insurer or beneficiary by any means, tried to contact or attempted adequately to make the payment of amount. The insurance claim is not found, the insurer shall keep evidence of searching the insurer or beneficiary by any means, tried to contact or attempting adequately to make the payment of the


4) The insurer shall submit the statement of unsettled claims to the Authority within the stipulated period.


5) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, if a serious disaster, including other catastrophe situations, has arisen in any place or area of the country, the Authority may form and implement a separate working procedure related to insurance claims for a certain period for such a place that suffered from a catastrophe situation.


6) Other provisions relating to settling insurance claims and unclaimed funds shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 124: Payment of Re-insurance Claim

1) The insurer performing re-insurance shall pay the amount in lieu of re-insurance claim on the basis of re-insurance contract made between the insurer performing insurance business and the insurer performing re-insurance business.


2) Settlement of re-insurance claim and other provisions relating to it shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 125: Settlement of Small Amount Claim

1) The liability, instead of the claim of a small amount that is less than the prescribed limit by the Authority, shall be settled under the settlement of the claim of a small amount.


2) Other provisions relating to payment of a claim of a small amount shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 126: Apparent loss or damage

1) The damage or loss having nature as prescribed by the Authority shall be settled under apparent damage or loss.


2) Other provisions relating to settlement of apparent damage or loss shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 127: Person Entitled to Get Payment

1) In case of the death of any insured before making the payment of the claim under a life insurance policy, the amount prescribed in the insurance policy shall be received by the person nominated by the concerned insured in an insurance policy.


2) In case no one has been nominated by the insured or the nominated person has already died before the death of the insured, such amount shall be received by the beneficiary of the insured as per the prevailing laws.


3) Notwithstanding anything mentioned in sub-sections (1) and (2), if the insured has provided authority to someone, the right of priority to obtain the insurance claim to the extent of the authorised amount shall be secured to the person who has been authorised by the insured.

## Chapter- 17 Provision Relating to Complaint

### Section 128: Complaint Relating to Payment of Claim

1) In case the insurer fails to pay an insurance claim within the period mentioned in the insurance policy, does not determine the liability, or does not determine the liability, causing detriment (loss) to the insured, the concerned insured may file a complaint with the Authority.


2) If a complaint is made pursuant to sub-section (1), the Authority shall provide a time of fifteen days for the concerned insurer to submit clarification after making the necessary investigation upon the complaint.


3) If the clarification pursuant to Sub-section (2) is received, receiving the evidence pertaining to it and if the clarification is not received from the insurer within the period prescribed to submit the clarification, the Authority may perform as follows:


(a) To make a compromise between the complainant and the insurer,


(b) To order to compensate reasonably if the insured suffered a loss due to not making payment of a claim,


(c) To order the concerned insurer to re-determine the liability if the pre-determined liability seems to be inappropriate,


(d) If it seems that the payment of claims  is to be made by the insurer to order the concerned insurer to make the payment,


(e) To give other appropriate orders.


4) If any complaint was made against any insurer due to not making payment as per the insurance policy and it is decided that the claim is to be paid, if so, the concerned insurer shall have to make payment of interest at the rate of ten percent from the date of filing complaint and amount equivalent to the actual expenses incurred for receiving legal remedies in addition to the insurance amount to the complainant.


5) Any insurer, insured, or any other person who is not satisfied with the order made by the Authority pursuant to sub-section (3) may appeal in the concerned High Court within thirty-five days.


6) Other provisions relating to actions upon the complaint under this section shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 129: Complaint Regarding Loss or Damage

1) If any insurer or its director or official or employee insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider cause to make damage or loss to any insured by performing acts against the interest of the insured, the concerned insurer shall be liable for it and shall compensate for this.


2) If damage or loss is caused by performing such an act, the insured may complain to the Authority within the prescribed period from the date of getting it to the knowledge.


3) In case the complaint is received pursuant to sub-section (2), the Authority shall make necessary investigation or verification and shall provide seven days to the concerned insurer, director, official, employee or concerned insurance intermediary to submit clarification.


4) In case the clarification as per sub-section (3) is received or not within the period provided to submit clarification, the Authority shall make an investigation and may order to compensate reasonable amount from the party who has caused to  loss to the complainant for lieu of damage or loss or may order to make a compromise between the insurer and the complainant.


5) The person being dissatisfied with the order to compensate pursuant to sub-section (4) may appeal in the concerned High Court within 35 days.


6) Other provisions relating to actions of a complaint under this section shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 130: Insurance Mediation

In case any dispute has arisen between the parties who have received license under this Act such as between the insurers or between the insurance intermediaries or between the insurer,
intermediary or the parties of insurance, and if there is a failure to solve the dispute on mutual understanding, the Authority may mediate on the request of any one party of the dispute or on the Authority’s own initiation.

## Chapter- 18 Operation and Management of Insurance in Province

### Section 131: Operation and Management of Insurance in Province

1) Provinces may, subject to this Act, operate and manage insurance by formulating laws on the following subject:


a) To make access to the  insurance,


b) To issue and renew the license of an insurance agent as prescribed,


c) To operate training programmes related to insurance,


d) To receive a hearing of complaint on prescribed subjects.


2) While performing the work pursuant to sub-section (1), the work shall be performed pursuant to the standards prescribed by the Authority.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, the Authority shall perform the activities as per this Section until the commencement of own activities by the province formulating the laws.

---

### Section 132: Details to be Provided

1) The Authority shall provide the information of insurers and insurance intermediaries having licenses received under this Act to the province’s concerned body.


2) The insurer shall provide the information of the branches established in the province as well as the information of the insurance intermediaries associated with itself to the concerned body of the province.

---

### Section 133: Annual Report Submit

The insurer operated and managed in accordance with Section 131 by the province shall prepare an annual report as prescribed, incorporating details of the activities performed during the year in the concerned Province, and submit it to the concerned body of the province. One copy of the report shall also be submitted to the Authority.

## Chapter- 19 Actions, Offences and Punishment

### Section 134: Power of The Authority

1) If case an Insurer is found to have violated this Act, Rules, By-laws or Directives framed under this Act or found violation of orders or submitted false statements or data to the Authority, the
Authority may take the following actions upon such insurer or its director, officials, chief executive employees, insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider:


(a) To make aware or issue a warning in writing,


(b) To execute a deed of commitment for improvement or to order for any improvement prescribing the definite period,


(c) To take into control the assets or books of accounts,


(d) To prohibit the distribution of bonuses or issuing bonus shares or right shares to the shareholders,


(e) To suspend or give an order  to the insurer to suspend the director(s), official(s), chief executive or employee(s) and insurance intermediary of the insurer,


(f) To impose a complete or partial ban on the operation of the insurance business,


(g) To file a petition to the concerned High Court to liquidate the insurer.


2) If a promoter, shareholder, director, chief executive or official or any concerned person of any insurer violates this Act or Rules, By-laws, or Directives framed under this Act or order, the Authority may, based on the nature and seriousness of violation, take one or more of the following actions:


(a) To forfeit and freeze the shares of the concerned insurer possessed by him/her and sell or cause to make sell such shares to another person or organisation,


(b) To withhold or suspend or cause to make withhold or suspend some or all privileges including meeting allowance, remuneration to be received by the Board of directors, director, official and other employee,


(c) To remove the chairman, director, chief executive or employee from their post by giving written notice if it is found from regulation, inspection or monitoring that the insurer has not worked in the interest of the insured, shareholder and insurer,


(d) If anyone has taken or given salary, allowances and other privileges in contrary to this Act or Rules, By-laws, Directives framed under this Act; to recover all such facilities or the amount to be involved instead of the same and interest as per the prevailing laws from the persons who have provided such facilities,


(e) To issue an order to the concerned Insurer to write to the concerned agency to take action against a director, chief executive, office bearer or employee of the insurer with whom he/she is professionally affiliated.


3) The person sacked (removed) from the post pursuant to clause (c) of sub-section (2) shall not receive any compensation or facilities in consequence of having retirement and shall not be appointed in the service of any insurer till five years from the date of retirement.


4) If any amount is to be paid by the insurer due to negligence or bad intention of any director, official, chief executive or employee, the Authority may recover or order to make recover such amount from the director, official, chief executive or employee.


5) In accordance with sub-section (4), the concerned director, official, chief executive or employee shall have to pay personally within thirty-five days from the date of obtaining information of order by the Authority.


6) In accordance with sub-section (5), in case such amount is not paid within the prescribed period, the amount shall be recovered from an account maintained in any bank or financial institution in the name of the concerned director, official, chief executive or employee or member of joint family or from movable, immovable properties of his/her own or joint family.


7) In case the Authority carries out regulation, inspection, supervision or any other actions of any Insurer or intermediary under this Act and publishes or broadcasts public notices thereof, the concerned Insurer shall have to pay the expenses to be incurred for publishing such notices.

---

### Section 135: Authority May Give Direction to Withold Payment of Insurance or Revoke the Insurance  Policy

Notwithstanding anything contained in this Act, the Authority may give direction not to issue insurance policy to anyone or withhold the payment of an amount to be paid as  per the insurance policy or revoke (cancel) the insurance policy in the following circumstances:


(a) To prevent from being any action against the interest of the insurance business or the insured,


(b) To assist in the investigation of any offence,


(c) To assist in controlling the offence of money laundering and financial investment in activities pertaining to terrorism, fraud, corruption, human trafficking or other organised crime.

---

### Section 136: Suspension of License or Ban of Insurance Business

1) The Authority may suspend the license of an Insurer for a certain period or impose a full or partial ban to perform the insurance business if any insurer performs any of the following acts:


(a) In case of failure to maintain the minimum paid-up capital pursuant to Section 36,


(b) In case the decision regarding the loss, damage or payment of claim pursuant to the provisions of Section 128 or 129 is not implemented within the specified time,


(c) In case it is deemed to cause serious detriment (loss) to the insured if allowed the insurer to operate the insurance business,


(d) Closing the insurance business without the approval of the Authority,


(e) Performing any other acts prohibited by this Act or Rules, By-laws framed under this Act or the existing laws,


(f) Frequent violations of the directions or order provided by the Authority.


2) The Authority shall provide a reasonable opportunity to the insurer to submit clarification before suspending the license or imposing a full or partial ban pursuant to sub-section (1).


3) The Authority may release the suspension or ban if the clarification submitted by the insurer pursuant to sub-section (2) seems to be satisfactory.


4) In case the insurer is banned from performing the insurance business fully or partially, the information shall be published in at least two national-level daily newspapers and through the Authority’s website for the general public pursuant to sub-section (1).

---

### Section 137: Revocation (Cancellation) of License of the Insurer

1) The Authority may revoke the license of the insurer in the following conditions:


(a) Being failure to release the ban to perform the insurance business or the suspension of the license pursuant to Section 136 up to one year,


(b) In case it is deemed to cause serious detriment (loss) to the insured if allowed the insurer to operate the insurance business,


(c) In case the insolvency proceedings are commenced by the court as per the prevailing laws against the insurer,


(d) In case an application is submitted by the insurer for the dissolution and the process of dissolution is completed.


2) The Authority shall provide reasonable opportunity by allowing an appropriate period, as prescribed to the insurer, for submitting clarification before revoking the license of the insurer except the conditions of clauses (c) or (d) of sub-section (1).


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in this section, in case the condition as mentioned in clauses (a) or (b) of Sub-section (1) is created and the insurer has committed to improve the condition and not to repeat the weaknesses, the Authority may provide a reasonable period for once to the concerned insurer to improve its condition or for correcting weaknesses.


4) In case the license of any insurer is revoked pursuant to this Section, the liabilities taken by the insurer before revocation of the license shall be settled as prescribed.


5) In case the license is revoked as per this Section, the Authority shall publish the name of the concerned insurer in at least two national-level daily newspapers and through the Authority’s website for the information of the general public.

---

### Section 138: Fineable

1) The Authority may impose the following fines to any insurer, insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider in addition to the other punishment pursuant to this Act in case they fail to prepare the statements, reports or documents in accordance with this Act or Rules, By-laws  or Directives framed under this Act, fail to provide the information to be given or fail to submit the documents and statements within the period prescribed or fail to provide documents, statements, data, statistics or This Act or records as required by the Authority or the official appointed by the Authority within the prescribed period in the course of performing the functions related to regulation, inspection or supervision:


(a) In the case of an insurer, twenty-five thousand rupees per day for one week after the expiry of the term and two thousand rupees per day for insurance intermediaries and other insurance service providers,


(b) In the case of an insurer, fifty thousand rupees per day for two weeks after the expiry of the term and five thousand rupees per day for insurance intermediaries and other insurance service providers,


(c) After the expiry of the period of clause (b), in the case of an insurer, one hundred thousand rupees per day at any time and ten thousand rupees per day for insurance intermediaries and other insurance service providers.


2) The Authority may impose a fine on the insurer as follows if the insurer fails to make a valuation of the loss or damage within the prescribed period:


(a) Daily five thousand rupees up to one week of exceeding the deadline,


(b) Daily ten thousand rupees up to two weeks of exceeding the deadline,


(c) Daily twenty-five thousand rupees from the date of expiration of the time limit referred to in clause (b) up to an indefinite period.


3) In case the insurer fails to determine the liability instead of claim or determines liability causing detriment (loss) or injustice, or being failure to settle the liability within the period prescribed, the Authority may impose a fine from two hundred thousand rupees to two million rupees depending on the nature and gravity of the matter.


4) In case the insurer performs any act contrary to this Act or Rules, By-laws or Directives framed under this Act or fails to follow the directions or orders issued by the Authority or does not
perform the insurance business with due process pursuant to this Act or prepare false  statement, the Authority may impose a fine as follows:


(a) Two hundred thousand rupees for the first time,


(b) Twenty-five hundred thousand rupees for the second time,


(c) Five hundred thousand rupees for each subsequent time for the third time or more.


5) If the insurer fails to submit a compliance report, the Authority may impose a fine ranging from one lakh to three lakh.


6) If the insurance intermediary performs any act contrary to this Act or Rules, By-laws or Directives framed under this Act or does not perform the works related to insurance with due process as per This Act or prepares false statement, the Authority may impose a fine as follows:


(a) Five thousand rupees for the first time,


(b) Twenty-five thousand rupees for the second time,


(c) Fifty thousand rupees for each subsequent time for the third time or more.


7) In case the insurer being failure to have auditing within the period specified under Section 87, the authority may impose fine to the insurer as follows:


(a) Fifty thousand rupees for the first week,


(b) One hundred thousand rupees for one week to one month,


(c) Five hundred thousand rupees for one month to three months,


(d) One million rupees for any period of three months to more than three months.


8) In case the insurer, director, official, employee or insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider were asked to submit the clarification pursuant to Section 128(2) or Section 129(3), but failed to submit the clarification within the period defined, the Authority may impose fine as follows:


(a) One hundred thousand rupees to the insurer,


(b) Five thousand rupees to the director, officer, employee, insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider of the insurer.


9) If the insurance surveyor does not submit valuation report in the prescribed time, the Authority may impose fine to that surveyor as follows for making late submission:


(a) Two thousand rupees for one week after the expiry of the period,


(b) Five thousand rupees for two weeks after the expiry of the period,


(c) Ten thousand rupees for one month after the expiry of the period,


(d) Twenty-five thousand rupees for any period after one month after the expiry of the period.


10) If it is found while making investigation or verification upon the complaint submitted by anyone or during the course of regulation, inspection or monitoring performed by the Authority that the insurance surveyor submitted the report making erroneous valuation of loss, the Authority may make aware or impose fine of fifty thousand rupees or punish by both for the first time and if the same act is repeated, revoking his/her license the Authority may impose fine of one hundred thousand rupees and make recover the amount equivalent to the loss incurred from him/her and pay the same to the insured.


11) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in the prevailing laws, if it is found that anyone has kept the property earned from the work liable to fine under this Section in the name of him/herself, joint family, relatives or any other person or concealed the same, it shall write to the concerning body to seize those properties including the properties increased or make increased from the same and it shall be the duty of the body to perform accordingly.


12) High The party being dissatisfied upon the punishment imposed by the Authority may file petition to the concerned High Court within thirty-five days from the date of punishment received.

---

### Section 139: Procedures of Taking Actions

1) The Authority shall give written notice to the concerned insurer, insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider or person providing a time limit of 7 days to submit clarification in reference to the proposed proceedings and the fine before taking regulatory action pursuant to Section 134 or imposing fine pursuant to Section 138.


2) The concerned insurer, insurance intermediary or any person shall have to submit their clarification in writing to the Authority within seven days from the date of written notice received in accordance with sub-section (1).


3) If the clarification submitted seems reasonable pursuant to sub-section (2), the Authority may amend the action or fine or repeal (cancel) the same.


4) Other procedures to be adopted by the Authority under this Act while taking action or imposing the fine shall be as determined by the Authority.

---

### Section 140: Offence

1) The following acts shall be deemed as offences committed under this Act:


(a) In case an Insurance business being operated without obtaining a license pursuant to Section 24,


(b) In case performing works as an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider without obtaining a license pursuant to Section 90,


(c) In case an insurance claim is being paid by the insurer by committing fraudulent acts or preparing or not preparing fake statements or documents aligning or not aligning with an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider or other parties,


(d) If an insurance claim was received by the insured by committing fraudulent acts or submitting or not submitting fake statements or documents aligning or not aligning with an insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider or other parties,


(e) In case of evaluating the loss, if any insurance surveyor performs an overestimate, underestimate or wrong evaluation selfishly or maliciously, causing the insurer or the insured to suffer loss or damage,


(f) If any insurance agent did not hand over the insurance premium payable by the insured to the insurer according to the insurance policy and misappropriated the amount,


(g) In case the employee, official or any person of the insurer commits any irregularities of financial misappropriation while issuing the insurance policy, assessing the loss of insured assets and liabilities, making payment of insurance claims or during the course of operation of insurance business,


(h) If any director, official, chief executive, employee or other person commits irregularities during the course of merger or amalgamation, acquisition, dissolution or auditing of insurance,


(i) If anyone attempted to commit offence from clause (a) to (h) or assisted to commit such offences in any way.


2) If any official or employee of foreign insurer, except in the course of regular transactions  of the branch office, transfers any property of the branch office to another country without obtaining
the approval from the Authority or obtain the approval by submitting fake statements, provide authority or power to do so or perform any act to assist or make to assist that work which shall
be deemed as an offence committed pursuant to this Act.

---

### Section 141: Punishment

1) If any person commits an offence pursuant to Section 140 (1) (a), the involved property instead of the claimed amount shall be seized and be imposed with a penalty equivalent to three times the claimed amount, and the person operating that business shall be imprisoned for five to ten years.


2) If any person commits an offence pursuant to Section 140 (1) (b) (c) (d), the property involved instead of the claim amount shall be confiscated and imposed with a penalty equivalent to the property involved instead of the claimed amount or will be imprisoned for three to ten years.


3) If any person commits an offence pursuant to Section 140 (1) (e ) (f) (g) (h), the properties involved instead of the claimed amount shall be confiscated, and the offender is liable to have punishment as follows :


(a) Imprisonment for up to one year if the amount is up to one million rupees,


(b) Imprisonment for up to two years if the amount is more than one million rupees and up to five million rupees,


(c) Imprisonment for up to three years if the amount is more than five million rupees and up to one crore rupees,


(d) Imprisonment for up to three to five years if the amount is more than ten million rupees, whichever is greater.


4) If any person commits an offence pursuant to section 140 (1) (i), Half of the punishment is to be imposed on the principal offender.


5) If any person commits an offence pursuant to section 140 (2), a fine equivalent to the claimed amount and imprisonment up to 5 years.


6) While determining the amount of claim for the purpose of this Section, the claimed amount shall be determined by calculating the amounts of all transactions.


7) In case of failing to determine the claimed amount under this Section, a fine up to Rs. 1 million and imprisonment up to 2 years shall be imposed, depending upon the extent of the offence.


8) If any work liable to punishment under this Section is performed by any corporate body, the concerned director, chief executive, official or employee who commits such act involving the offence shall be punished.

---

### Section 142: Don&#8217;t work in the Insurance Business

In case director, official or employee or the license of any insurance agent, insurance, a or intermediary of the insurer has been suspended, or a case related with offence pursuant to a  Section 140 has been filed in the court against them, such director, official, chief executive, consultant or employee or insurance intermediary shall not be allowed to perform any work related with insurance business until such suspension is released or final decision of the case is made.

---

### Section 143: Special Provision Related with The Insured

1) If any insured has committed fraud, forgery acts relating to the insurance aligning or not aligning with the insurer or insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider or submitted or not submitted fake statements, not submitted the statements which to be submitted or submitted the statements which need not be submitted, concealed the fundamental facts about own self or insured properties, if there is doubt about making claim of any insurance or doubt of influencing the insurer or insurance intermediary or other insurance service provider, the Authority may inspect or verify upon such insured.


2) It shall be the duty of the insured to assist the Authority while performing the acts of inspection or verification in accordance with sub-section (1).


3) In accordance with sub-section (1), if the Insured is found guilty from inspection or verification, it shall be sent in writing to the authorised body pursuant to the prevailing laws.


4) The Authority may, upon written notice from an authorized body under the prevailing law, blacklist any insured or any other person who does not cooperate in the investigation or inquiry pursuant to this Section, commits insurance fraud or insurance fraud and prohibit such person from insuring any business in which such person is involved for a period of five years.


5) Other provisions relating to the investigation or inquiry of the insured shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 144: Punishment for those who oppose obstacles

If anyone obstructs and opposes the activities related to investigation or verification under this Act, the adjudicating official may, on the basis of the report of the official appointed for investigation or verification, impose a punishment of imprisonment up to six months or a fine not exceeding one hundred thousand rupees or both.

---

### Section 145: Limitation for Complaint

If it comes to the knowledge of the Authority through any channel or from inspection of the Authority and inspection or verification made by authorised officials or bodies pursuant to the prevailing laws about committing of such act which is deemed as offence under this Act or if it is proved that such act is committed, the complaint may be submitted within six months from the date of it being proved and lawsuit, shall be filed in the court within three months from the date of complaint made.

---

### Section 146: Government of Nepal to be Plaintiff

1) The case concerning the offences pursuant to Section 140 shall be prosecuted as the Government of Nepal is the plaintiff, and such cases shall be deemed to be included in Schedule-1 of Criminal Code Procedure, 2074.


2) The Authority shall provide necessary assistance to the concerned official in the process of investigation, collection of evidence and filing of the case in accordance with sub-section (1).

---

### Section 147: Authority to Hear Case and Execution of The Decision

1) The authority to proceed and finalise the case concerning offences under Section 140 shall be vested on the court as prescribed by the Government of Nepal publishing a notice in Nepal Gazette.


2) The Authority itself may take action and give punishment to other works prohibited under this Act, other than mentioned in sub-section (1), after having investigation or verification.


3) For the purposes mentioned in sub-section (2), the Authority shall have the authority as equal to the authority of the District Court in addition to the powers provided in the rules framed under this Act for the purpose of investigation or in the court as prescribed by the verification and imposing punishment.


4) The hearing on the appeal or application submitted in the High Court, as per this Act, shall be made by the commercial bench of the concerned High Court.

---

### Section 148: Request for Details

1) In the course of an investigation or inquiry to be conducted in relation to regulation or action under this Act, if the Authority or the investigating or investigating body considers it necessary to conduct an investigation or inquiry to obtain any document, account statement, document or other particulars and if it considers that any person or organisation has such document, account statement or other particulars or that such person or organisation can produce such particulars, the Authority or the investigating or investigating body may request such person or organisation to produce such particulars.


2) It shall be the duty of all persons or organisations concerned to provide or assist in providing any document, account statement or other particulars requested by the investigating or investigating body pursuant to sub-section (1).

---

### Section 149: No Obstruction for Punishment

If it is found that any person committed any kind of offence pursuant to Section 138 while being incumbent in any post of the insurer, it shall not be obstructed for investigation or verification or impose punishment due to the reason of being retired from that post or not taking the responsibility of that post at present.

## Chapter- 20 Miscellaneous

### Section 150: Insured Insurance Protections Fund

1) There shall be one Insured Interest Protection Fund formed under the Authority.


2) The following amounts shall be credited to the Fund:


(a) The amount earmarked by the Authority for this purpose,


(b) The amount received from the insurer operating life insurance business as prescribed by the Authority,


(c) The amount deposited in unclaimed fund as per Section 125 which was to be paid under life insurance policy but could not be paid for any reason.


3) Other provisions concerning following the fund shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 151: Insurance Development Fund

1) There shall be one Insurance Development Fund in the Authority to protect the interest of the citizens through insurance, extending access to insurance to economically deprived citizens.


2) The following amounts shall be credited in the Fund:


(a)The amount received from the Government of Nepal,


(b) The amount equivalent to fifty percent of micro-insurance regulation fees to be received by the Authority from the insurer pursuant to Section 79 and at least thirty-three percent of regulation fees to be received by the Authority from the insurer pursuant to Section 100,


(c) The amount received from other sources.


3) The Authority shall deposit the amount under clause (b) in the Insurance Development Fund every year.


4) The utilisation of the amount of the Insurance Development Fund shall be made in defined area as prescribed


5) Other provisions concerning with the operation and management of Fund shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 152: Complaint Hearing

1) If anyone does have a complaint related to the activities performed by the insurer, insured or insurance intermediary or director, official, employee or insurance intermediary of the insurer or
any other concerned person pursuant to this Act, the complaint may be submitted to the Authority.


2) Other provisions regarding the hearing of the complaint and the authority to hear the complaint shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 153: Not to Make Insurance Transactions with Anyone Except Insurer

1) Any person or organisation shall not make any transactions related to the insurance business with anyone except the insurer possessing a license pursuant to this Act.


2) The insurance of properties situated in Nepal and liabilities to be created within Nepal shall be made only with the insurer possessing a license pursuant to this Act.

---

### Section 154: Formation of Expert Groups

1) The Authority may form an expert group as prescribed to study, investigate or investigate claims related to fraud, forgery or deceit that may arise or have occurred in the insurance business and recommend necessary opinions for taking action, reform or formulating policies in that regard.


2) Other provisions regarding the expert group shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 155: Insurance Information Centre

1) The Authority may establish an Insurance Information Centre to collect and organise information on the insurance sector in an integrated manner.


2) The Authority shall regulate the Insurance Information Centre established pursuant to Sub-section (1).


3) Other provisions relating to the Insurance Information Centre shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 156: Establishment of Insurance Institutions

1) The Authority may establish an insurance-related institute to produce skilled manpower in the insurance sector, promote insurance education and conduct insurance-related training, study and research.


2) Other provisions relating to the insurance institute shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 157: Collection of Statistics

1) The Authority may collect data required by it, either on its own or from the Government of Nepal or other bodies, to achieve its objectives and perform its functions.


2) For this purpose, the Authority may also seek assistance from authorised persons, associations, institutions and international associations and organisations within or outside Nepal.


3) The Authority shall publish the activities, statistics and other details related to the insurance sector every month for the information of the general public.

---

### Section 158: Not being held accountable for actions done with good intentions

1) No member or employee of the Authority shall be held personally or collectively liable for any act or omission done in good faith in accordance with this Act or any rule, regulation or directive made under this Act.


Provided that he shall be personally liable for any act done knowingly or with the wrong intention.


2) If any suit is filed against any member or employee of the Authority in respect of any loss or damage caused or to be caused by anything done or attempted to be done in good faith in order to comply with this Act or any rule, regulation or directive made under this Act, the Authority shall bear the expenses of such suit.


Provided that the Authority shall not bear the expenses of any suit filed with respect to any act done knowingly or with the wrong intention.

---

### Section 159: Submission of Report to Government of Nepal

1) The Authority shall submit report to the Government of Nepal within four months from the completion of the financial year incorporating the following information in the report:


(a) Statement relating to its own works and action and the work performed and achievement made during the previous fiscal year,


(b) Overall position of the country related to the insurance business,


(c) Statement of achievement corresponding to an annual insurance policy,


(d) Activities to be performed for making reforms in the insurance sector,


2) The Nepal Government shall publish the report submitted by the Authority in accordance with sub-section (1).

---

### Section 160: Delegation of Authority

1) The Authority may delegate its authority received as per this Act or Rules framed under this act to the chairman, member and other committee or sub-committee formed by the Authority.


Provided that the following authorities shall not be delegated:


(a) To provide prior approval to establish an insurance company,


(b) To provide a license of insurer,


(c) To make restrictions in the insurance business,


(d) To revoke the license of the insurer,


(e) To constitute a special management team pursuant to Section 103,


(f) To issue By-laws or Directives.


2) The chairman may delegate authority received under this Act to other officials of the Authority to operate the Authority’s business uninterruptedly.

---

### Section 161: Contact with the Government of Nepal

The Authority shall maintain contact with the Government of Nepal through the Ministry.

---

### Section 162: Coordinate with Public Bodies

To perform the functions pursuant to this Act, the Authority may coordinate, collaborate, and provide assistance and information exchange with constitutional organs and bodies, regulatory bodies, banks and financial institutions, various organised organisations, and other public bodies.

---

### Section 163: International Aid and Relation

1) The Authority shall represent Nepal in international institutions and organisations concerning insurance policies, insurance supervision and regulation, risk measurement, insurance claims, re-insurance, monitoring and other concerned subjects under its jurisdiction.


2) The Authority may provide services related to insurance supervision and regulation to foreign governments, foreign insurance regulatory bodies and international institutions and organisations, and it may also receive such service from them.


3) The Authority shall collaborate with international institutions and organisations and expand cooperation and relationships to achieve financial and economic stability through the regulation of international insurance.


4) The Authority shall take responsibility for works to be fulfilled and to be performed on behalf of its own country while taking part in the capacity of representative of Nepal in international institutions and organisations relating to insurance.

---

### Section 164: The Government of Nepal may Issue Directives

1) The Government of Nepal may issue necessary directions to the Authority for promoting the insurance business and making necessary improvements in it.


2) The Authority’s duty is to comply with the directions pursuant to sub-section (1).

---

### Section 165: Provide Expert Services

If requested by the Government of Nepal, the Authority shall provide the services of its experts.

---

### Section 166: Issue Orders and Directives

The Authority may issue any order, direction, or guidance to the insurer, insured, insurance intermediary, or other insurance service provider in order to achieve the objectives of this Act.

---

### Section 167: Pursuant to this Act

1) The matters written in this Act shall be governed by this Act, and the prevailing law shall be governed by other matters.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), the matters mentioned in the prevailing law relating to the prevention of money laundering or financial investment in terrorist activities shall be governed by the same Act.

---

### Section 168: Power to Frame Rules

1) The Government may frame necessary Rules to implement this Act.


2) The provisions related to the following subjects may be made in the Rules:


(a) Related to functions, duties and powers of Chairperson,


(b) Related to whistleblowing policy,


(c) Related to collective or communal insurance fund (insurance pool),


(d) Related to micro-insurance.

---

### Section169: Enact and Enforce Regulations

The Authority may make and enforce regulations subject to this Act or the rules made under this Act.

---

### Section 170: Implementing Guidelines and Procedures

The Authority may formulate and implement necessary guidelines and procedures for the conduct of its operations, subject to this Act or the rules made under this Act.

---

### Section 171:  Act Implementation Measurement

The Ministry shall measure the implementation of the Act within one year after the completion of five years from the commencement of this Act and within one year after every five years thereafter and shall submit a report thereon to the concerned committee of both Houses of the Federal Parliament.

---

### Section 172: Repeal and Saving

1) The following Acts have been repealed:


(a) Insurance Act, 2049


(b) National Insurance Corporation (Rastriya Beema Sansthan) Act, 2025 (1968 A.D.)


2) Subject to the repealed Act pursuant to sub-section (1)


(a) Insurance Board deemed to be ipso facto converted into Authority after the commencement of this Act.


(b) The Rules, By-laws, Directives, licenses issued, orders given, functions performed, and actions taken by the Insurance Board shall be considered as to have been or performed by the Authority.


(c) The movable and immovable assets or liabilities of the Insurance Board shall ipso facto be converted as the movable and immovable assets or liabilities of the Authority.


(d) The chairperson and the member of the Insurance Board shall ipso facto be the chairperson or be a member of the Authority after the commencement of this Act, and he/she may remain in that post up to his/her tenure.


(e) The employees of the Insurance Board shall ipso facto be converted as the employees of the Authority after the commencement of this act.


(f) Pending cases remained in Insurance Board shall ipso facto transferred in the Authority and proceeded and decided as per this Act.


3) In accordance with the Act repealed pursuant to clause (b) of sub-section (1):-


(a) National Insurance Corporation (Rastriya Beema Sansthan) shall be converted into public limited within one year.


(b) The employees working in National Insurance Corporation shall ipso facto be converted as the employees of the company pursuant to clause (a).


(c) The movable and immovable assets and liabilities of National Insurance Corporation shall ipso facto be converted as the movable and immovable assets and liabilities of the company
pursuant to clause (a).


(d) The insurance policies issued by National Insurance Corporation shall ipso facto be deemed as issued by the company pursuant to clause (a)
