# Cooperatives Act, 2074 (2017)

---

## Preamble

Date of Authentication
2074/7/4


Amending Act


1. An Act to Amend Certain Nepal Acts, 2075             2075.11.19


2. An Act to Amend Certain Acts Relating to the Prevention of Money Laundering and Promotion of Business Environment, 2080         2080.12. 30


Act No. 41 of the Year 2074


Preamble: Whereas it is expedient to amend and consolidate the prevailing laws relating to cooperatives in order to make economic, social and cultural upliftment of members of cooperatives through the integration of capital, technology and talents scattered amongst farmers, craftsperson, labourers, low-income groups and marginalised communities or general consumers in accordance with norms, values and principles of cooperatives; to promote the regulation of cooperative organisation as community-based, member-centric, democratic, autonomous, corporate entity and to develop self-reliant, sustainable and socialism-oriented national economy by way of cooperative farming, industries, goods and services enterprises;


Be it enacted by the Legislature-Parliament referred to in clause (1) of Article 296 of the Constitution of Nepal.


---

## Chapter-1 Preliminary

### Section 1: Short title and commencement

1) This Act may be cited as the “Cooperatives Act, 2017.”


2) This Act shall come into force immediately.

---

### Section 2: Definitions

Unless the subject or context requires otherwise, in this Act, –


a) “Internal Procedures” means the Internal Procedures framed by a Co-operative Organization pursuant to Section 28.


b) “Offence” means an offence referred to in Section 122.


c) “Prescribed” or “as prescribed” means prescribed or as prescribed in the Rules framed under this Act.


d) “Family” means the husband or wife, son, daughter-in-law, daughter, adopted son, adopted daughter, father, mother, stepmother of a member and elder brother, elder sister-in-law, younger brother, younger sister-in-law, elder sister or younger sister to whom the member has to take care of.


Provided that this term does not include a member of the family who has been living separately either upon partition of family property or carrying out his or her own profession or business.


e) “Primary Capital Fund” means the share capital and reserve fund.


f) “Savings” means an amount deposited by a member in a Cooperative Organisation.g) “Ministry” means the Ministry of Government of Nepal looking after cooperative affairs.


h) “Main transaction” means a transaction having 30% or more share in liability of savings mobilised in the commercial activities or in purchase or sales transactions of members until the latest fiscal year.


i) “Registrar” means the Registrar designated pursuant to Section 92,


j) “Accounts Supervision Committee” means the Accounts Supervision Committee referred to in Section 48.


k) “Byelaws” means the Byelaws framed by the concerned Cooperative Organisation pursuant to Section 27.


l) “Department” means the Department of Cooperatives.


m) “Share” means a divided portion of the share capital of a Cooperative Organization.


n) “Local Level” means a Rural Municipality or a Municipality, and the words also include Sub-Municipal Corporation and Municipal Corporation as well.


o) “Director” means a member of the Board of Directors, and the word also includes an official of the Board.


p) “Member” means the person who has obtained membership in a Cooperative Organization.


q) “Board” means the Board of Directors referred to in sub-Section (1) of Section 41.


r) “Cooperative Bank” means a Cooperative Bank Registered pursuant to Section 12.


s) “Cooperative values” means internationally recognised cooperative values, including self-reliance, self-responsibility, democracy, equality, equity, solidarity, integrity, openness, social responsibility, and care for others.


t) “Cooperative business” means commercial activities operated as provided for in the Byelaws of the Cooperative.


u) “Cooperative principles” means internationally recognised cooperative principles, including voluntary and open membership; democratic control by members; financial participation of members; autonomy and independence; education, training and information; mutual cooperation amongst cooperatives and concerns towards the community.


v) “Cooperative Organization” means an organisation or association, and the word also includes a cooperative bank.


w) “Association” means the District Sectoral Cooperative Association, District Cooperative Association, Province Sectoral Cooperative Association, Central Sectoral Cooperative Association and Specialized Cooperative Association and the word also includes the National Cooperative Association formed under the same Section.


x) “Organization” means a Sectoral or Multipurpose Organization formed pursuant to Section 3 and Registered pursuant to Section 15.


y) “General Meeting” means a General Meeting of a Cooperative Organisation.

## Chapter 2 Formation and Registration of Cooperative Organisation

### Section 3: Formation of Organisation

1) Three Nepali citizens, at the minimum, may mutually form a sectoral or multipurpose cooperative organisation.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), in the case of a Cooperative Organization carrying out business based on labour and skills involving labourers, youths and others, even 15 Nepali citizens may form such an organisation.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), participation of at least one hundred Nepali citizens shall be required while forming an Organization carrying out transactions of savings and credits in a Metropolitan City or Sub[1]Metropolitan City.


4) While forming an Organization under this Section, the number referred to in sub-Section (1), (2) and (3) shall be met at the rate of one member per family.


Provided that there shall be no hindrance to acquiring membership by more than one person of a family once an Organization is registered pursuant to Section 15.


5) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), at least one hundred employees, teachers or professors who are incumbent in an office getting remuneration from the Government of Nepal, Provincial Government, Local Level or from a school, University or corporate entity getting a grant from, or under the ownership of, such government or level may, based on the professional organisation formed according to the prevailing law, form together an organisation with a condition to abide by the terms and conditions as prescribed.


Provided that in an office with less than one hundred employees, at least thirty employees, teachers or professors may form together an organisation with a condition to abide by the terms and conditions as prescribed.

---

### Section 4: Formation of District Sectoral Cooperative Association

At least eleven organisations formed pursuant to Section 15 for carrying out works, including the development, promotion, and marketing of Cooperative enterprises, may together form a District Sectoral Cooperative Association in their specific sector.


Provided that at least seven Organizations may together form a district Sectoral Cooperative Organization on their specific sector in the remote districts which the[1]government of Nepal has specified as those belonging to Class “A”.

---

### Section 5: Formation of District Cooperative Association

At least eleven Organizations Registered pursuant to Section 15 and engaged in activities including the development, promotion, and marketing of cooperative enterprises may together form a Sectoral District Cooperative Association.


Provided that:-


1) At least seven Organizations may form a district Sectoral Cooperative Association in their specific sector in the remote districts that the Government of Nepal has specified as belonging to Class “A.”


2) While forming an Association in such a manner, it shall include in the Association more than fifty percent of the Sectoral Associations Registered in the district.

---

### Section 6: Formation of Provincial Sectoral Cooperative Association

At least 25 Sectoral Organizations of at least five districts Registered pursuant to Section 15 for carrying out works including works of development, promotion and marketing of Cooperative enterprise may together form Provincial Sectoral Cooperative Association on their specific sector.


Provided that while forming Association in such a manner, it shall include in the Association more than fifty percent of the Sectoral Associations Registered in the Province.

---

### Section 7: Formation of Provincial Cooperative Association

In order to support the development, promotion, and marketing of cooperative enterprises, at least 31 organisations formed in at least five districts or district cooperative sectoral associations or district cooperative associations or provincial Sectoral associations may form a Provincial Cooperative Association.


Provided that while forming an association in such a manner, more than fifty percent of each of the district cooperative sectoral associations, district cooperative associations, or provincial sectoral associations registered all over the province shall be included

---

### Section 8: Formation of Central Sectoral Cooperative Association

In order to support the development, promotion, and marketing of cooperative enterprises, at least 51 organisations formed in at least seven districts or district cooperative Sectoral associations or district cooperative associations or provincial Sectoral associations may form a Central Sectoral Cooperative Association.


Provided that while forming an Association in such a manner, more than fifty percent of each of the district cooperative Sectoral associations or provincial sectoral associations registered all over the province shall have to be included.

---

### Section 9: Formation of Specialised Cooperative Association

1) At least 25 multi-purpose or Sectoral organisations Registered pursuant to Section 15 may form together a specialised Cooperative Association for carrying out works requiring a large amount of investment and relating to hydropower, chemical fertilisers factory, residential projects, transport, heavy agro equipment, fruits processing, herbs processing, sugar industries, cold storage, hospital, college, technical schools, laboratory or to fulfil the demand of common needs of members. This shall not have the right to choose or to be chosen in any association or federation.


2) Its operational procedures shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 10: Formation of Federation of National Cooperatives

1) The following Organization or Association may together form the National Federation of Cooperatives with the objective of promoting good governance in the cooperatives sector on the basis of values, norms and principles of cooperatives:-


a) More than 50% of the District Sectoral Cooperatives Associations formed pursuant to Section 4,


b) More than 50% of the District Cooperatives Associations formed pursuant to Section 5,


c) More than 50% of the Provincial Sectoral Cooperatives Associations formed pursuant to Section 6,


d) More than 50% of the Provincial Cooperatives Associations formed pursuant to Section 7,


e) More than 50% of the Central Sectoral Cooperatives Associations formed pursuant to Section 8.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), the National Cooperatives Association Registered at the time of commencement of this Act shall be deemed to have been converted as the National Cooperatives Federation under this Act.

---

### Section 11: No Association may be Formed

Notwithstanding anything contained in sections 4,5,6,7,8 and 10, no more than one district Sectoral cooperatives association or district cooperative association of the same nature; no more than one provincial Sectoral cooperatives association or provincial cooperative association of the same nature; or no more than one central cooperative federation may be formed.

---

### Section 12: Formation of Cooperative Bank

1) Organizations and Associations together may, with prior approval of the Nepal Rastra Bank, form a Cooperative Bank with the objectives of accepting savings of the Organisation or Association, disbursing credits or providing banking services to such Organization or Association.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), the national cooperative bank that is in operation at the commencement of this Act shall be deemed to have been formed under this Act.


3) Other provisions concerning the formation of a Cooperative Bank shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 13: No Operation of Cooperative Organisation without Registration

No one shall operate a Cooperative Organization without getting it Registered.

---

### Section 14: Application to be Submitted for Registration

1) The Cooperative Organizations formed under this Act shall, for the purpose of registration, submit application in the prescribed format to the authority authorised by the Registrar in case of the Organization referred to in Section 3, District Sectoral Cooperatives Association referred to in Section 4 or District Cooperatives Association referred to in Section 5; and to the Registrar in case of Provincial Sectoral Cooperatives Association referred to in Section 6, Provincial Cooperatives Association referred to in Section 7, Central Sectoral Cooperatives Association referred to in 8, Special Cooperatives Association referred to in Section 9, National Federation of Cooperatives referred to in Section 10 or Cooperative Bank referred to in Section 12.


2) The following documents shall have to be submitted along with the application referred to in sub-Section (1):-


a) Proposed Byelaws of a Cooperative Organization’


b) Report of the feasibility study of the operation of the Cooperative Organisation,


c) Details as to the number of shares undertaken to subscribe by the members and amount of shares,


d) Other details as prescribed.


3) While submitting an application for registration of a Cooperative Bank pursuant to sub-Section (1), prior approval of the Nepal Rastra Bank, in addition to the documents referred to in sub-Section (2), shall also be included.

---

### Section 15: To be Registered

1) In case the following conditions are found to have been met while conducting an inquiry on the documents along with the application referred to in sub-Section (1) of Section 14, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall, within 30 days from the date of filing of the application, Registrar the Cooperative Organization and issue a certificate thereof in the prescribed format.


(a) The Byelaws submitted along with the application by  consistent with this Act and the Rules framed under this Act,


(b) There is the basis that the proposed Cooperative Organization could be run according to the values, norms and principles of cooperatives,


(c) There is a clear basis that the Cooperative Organization could be run and controlled being community-based and member-centric,


(d) Other bases prescribed are completed.


2) While carrying out inquiry pursuant to sub-Section (1), in case it is found that the proposed Cooperative Organization could not be Registered without amendment to the Byelaws of the organisation, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall issue a notice to that effect to the applicant within fifteen days of the date of receipt of the application.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, the Cooperative Organisations which are registered and operated at the commencement of this Act shall be deemed to have been registered under this Act.


4) While registering a Cooperative Organization under this Act, the authority referred to in sub-section (1) of Section 14 may prescribe any condition to be fulfilled by such Cooperative Organization.


5) In case any condition is prescribed pursuant to sub-Section (4), it shall be the duty of the concerned Cooperative Organization to comply with such condition.


6) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, savings and credit Cooperative Organizations shall be registered in the case of a Municipal Corporation and sub-municipal Corporation and in one Municipality or Rural Municipality according to the prescribed standards.


7) In the case of a specialised Organization referred to in Section 9, such an organisation may be registered without having duplication in the services to be provided by the District Sectoral Cooperatives Association or by the Central Sectoral Cooperatives Association.

---

### Section 16: Registration may be Denied

1) In case there is no condition referred to in sub-section (1) of Section 15, in case the applicant rejects to amend to the Byelaws within the period of time when information has been given to amend the Byelaws under the same Section, in the Byelaws is not amended within thirty days from the date of providing such information or the Byelaws is not amended as stated in the information, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may reject to Registrar such Cooperative Organization.


2) In case the Registrar denies to the Registrar the Cooperative Organization pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall have to give notice thereof to the concerned applicant within three days specifying[1]the reasons thereof.

---

### Section 17: Cooperative Organisation to be a Corporate Entity

1) A Cooperative Organisation shall be an autonomous corporate body having perpetual succession.


2) There shall be a separate seal for carrying out functions and activities of the Cooperative Organization.


3) Cooperative Organisation may subject to this Act, acquire, utilise, sell or dispose of movable or immovable property as a person.


4) A Cooperative Organisation may sue a case as a person, and it may be sued against it in its name.


5) A Cooperative Organisation may conclude a contract as a person.

---

### Section 18: Working Area of Cooperative Organisation

1) The working areas of a cooperative Organisation at the time of registration shall be as follows:


(a) In case of an Organisation carrying out the main transaction of savings and credits, one ward in case of  another Municipal Corporation or Sub-Municipal Corporation,


(b) In case of an Organisation carrying out the main transaction of savings and credits and in case of an  Organisation in a Municipality or Rural Municipality, in one Local Level only on the following basis as prescribed:-


1) Common bond executed for mutual practice of self-reliance among members,


2) Number of the members required for operating services in  a commercial manner,


3) Convenient place for having participatory democratic control of members in the operation of the Organisation.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), after two years of registration and operation of commercial service by an Organisation, it may extend its working areas as follows on the following bases:-


a) Additional working areas required to increase the number of members in the course of increment of commercial operation of the Organisation,


b) Creative measures are followed for maintaining direct control of members in the transaction of business of the organisation,


c) This is not contrary to the standards determined pursuant to sub-Section (6) of Section 15.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (2), working areas may be extended on the grounds of the nature of settlement of members, convenient place, local settings, and other specific commercial needs as topography prescribed.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, the working areas of an Organisation may be extended to one local level or district or more than one district or more than one province which requires wider working areas due to expansion of commercial areas such as hydropower project, education, linguistic, literary cultural, health services, communication, production, storage and pro cessing special agro or forests products or increase of participation of members.


5) The working areas of the Organisations which are in operation at the commencement of this Act shall be deemed to have been maintained time of the under this Act.


Provided that those organisations shall have to submit to the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar business operation report till the date of commencement of this Act within one year from the commencement of this Act specifying the following details:-


(a) Condition of the spread of membership distribution in the existing working areas in terms of province, district, local level and ward,


(b) Condition of commercial service operations according to current working areas in terms of province, district, local level and ward,


(c) Other details as prescribed.


6) After submission of the business operations report pursuant to sub-Section (5), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall conduct necessary inquiry and may issue directive to the concerned Cooperative Organisation to re determine the working areas as follows:-


a) To decrease the working areas by removing province, district, local level and ward which does not have extension of membership referred to in the distribution of clause (a) of sub-Section (5) or which have less number of members than the prescribed number or which does not have geographical continuity,


b) To decrease the working areas by removing province, district, local level and ward where any commercial service is not being operated or commercial services is operated in less than the prescribed amount or proportion than the proscribed amount of the total transaction according to the statement referred to in clause (b) of sub-Section (5),


c) To maintain appropriate working areas by decreasing province, district, local level and ward with a view to increase commercial efficiency of the organisation to sustain the operational case of the Organisation and to maintain participatory democratic control of members in the operation of the organisation;


d) To maintain appropriate working areas by removing the additional working areas of province, district, local level and ward in order facilitate the acquisition of membership of district or province Sectoral association or of the district or province Cooperative Organisation easier for the commercial or promotional services.


7) In case the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar gives directives pursuant to sub-Section (6), the Cooperative Organisation shall have to amend to the Byelaws within one year and re-determine its working areas.


8) In case the concerned Cooperative Organisation does not re-determine the working areas pursuant to sub-Section (7), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall re-determine the working areas of the Organisation within a period of three months subject to sub-Section (2).


9) In case a Cooperative Organisation,, the working areas of which has been re determined by the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar pursuant to sub-Section (8) does not satisfy with the redetermination of the working areas, it may file an application for review to the Registrar in case the decision has been made by the authority authorised by the Registrar and to the ministry in case the decision is make by the Registrar within 35 days from the date of receipt of the information of the redetermination.


10) The Registrar or Ministry shall have to complete the review on the application received pursuant to in sub-Section (9) within sixty days.


11) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, a Cooperative Organisation may at any time bring amendment to the Byelaws to decrease the working areas on voluntary basis.


12) Other provisions relating to redetermination of working areas shall be as prescribed.


13) The process of expansion of working areas pursuant to sub-Sections (2) and (3) shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 19: Working Area of Association and Cooperative Bank

The working areas of the Association and Cooperative Bank shall be as follows:-


a) One district in the case of District Sectoral Cooperative Association or District Cooperative Association,


b) One province in the case of Province Sectoral Cooperative Association or Province Cooperative Association,


c) Throughout Nepal in the case of the Central Sectoral Cooperative Association or National Federation of Cooperatives,


d) In the case of a specialised Association, depending upon collection, distribution or flow of service based on its nature,


e) In the case of a Cooperative Bank, as specified by the Nepal Rastra Bank.

---

### Section 20: Classification may be made on Sectoral Basis

Organisation or Association may be classified based on sectors as prescribed.

---

### Section 21: Transaction, Business, Industries, or Project may be operated

1) After receiving the certificate of registration pursuant to Section 15, the Organisation or Association may operate necessary transactions, businesses, industries, or projects subject to this Act and Byelaws for the purpose of attainment of the objectives referred to in Section 24.


2) The Cooperative Bank may operate its transactions or services only after obtaining a license from the Nepal Rastra Bank.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, the Organisation or Association does not need to Registrar an organisation separately in order to carry on transactions, businesses, industries, or projects referred to in sub-Section (1).


Provided that in case the prevailing law requires such permission, approval or license has to be obtained to carry on such transaction, business, industries, or project, such transaction, business, industries, or project shall be carried out only after obtaining the permission, approval or license, accordingly.


4) In case the Organisation or Association obtains permission, approval or license from the agency or authority authorised according to the prevailing laws, it shall inform the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar within fifteen days of the date of receipt of such permission, approval or license.


5) Two or more Organisations and Associations may, subject to this Act, carry on transactions, businesses, industries, or projects for the marketing of their product or services jointly or in partnership.


6) Other provisions relating to the operation of transactions, businesses, industries, or projects referred to in sub-Section (5) shall be as prescribed.


7) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, the Organisation or Association may sell the product of the transaction, business, industries, or project to be operated pursuant to sub-Section (1) in the brand name of the Co-operative.


Provided that in case the prevailing law requires that the approval of receiving such a brand has to be obtained, such approval shall be obtained accordingly.


8) The process and basis of issuance of the certificate of origin of the goods to be produced by the Organisation or Association shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 22: Liabilities to be limited

1) Liability of a member in terms of a transaction of a Cooperative Organization shall be limited only up to the maximum limit of the shares he/she has subscribed or agreed to subscribe to.


2) The name of a Cooperative Organization shall contain “cooperative”, and it shall contain the word “limited” at the end.

---

### Section 23: Values, norms and principles of cooperative to be complied with

Values, norms and principles of cooperative shall be complied with while forming and operating a Cooperative Organisation.

---

### Section 23A: Standards to be Followed

11) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, the body responsible for regulating, inspecting and supervising a cooperative organisation engaged in the main business of savings and loans shall comply with the instructions and standards issued by Nepal Rastra Bank in this regard.


2) If a cooperative organisation, as referred to in Sub-section (1) in existence at the time of the commencement of this Section, has not established the standards determined by Nepal Rastra Bank, it shall establish such standards within two years from the date of commencement of this Section.

## Chapter 3 Objectives and Functions of Organisation or Association

### Section 24: Objective of Organisation or Association

The main objective of an Organisation or Association shall be to attain economic, social and cultural prosperity based on working areas and on being concentrated on its members.

---

### Section 25: Functions of Organisation or Association

The Organization or Association shall carry out the following functions:-


a) To comply with or cause to be complied with the values, norms and principles of cooperatives,


b) To promote the interests of members and its own and to carry out or cause to be carried out their marketing,


c) To provide education, training and information to members and to promote or cause to promote good governance in an Organisation or Association,


d) To promote or cause to be promoted mutual cooperation between Organisation and Association,


e) To determine standards of the products and services of the organisation or Association and to carry out functions as to quality reform, economic stability and risk management,


f) To submit the internal control system,


g) To operate activities relating to commercial promotion and development of an Organisation or Association,


h) To comply with the directives of the Ministry, Registrar, Province, local level or office,


i) To carry out functions referred to in the bylaws.

---

### Section 26: Functions of National Cooperatives Federation

The National Federation of Co-operatives shall carry out the following functions for the promotion of the interests of members:-


a) To comply with or cause to be complied with the values, norms and principles of cooperatives,


b) To play leading roles in the promotion of the interests of members,


c) To carry out coordination and collaboration with various agencies of the Government of Nepal on the promotion of the cooperative sector,


d) To conduct and cause to be conducted commercial and market-related studies and research for the promotion of cooperative enterprises,


e) To provide or cause to be provided education, training, and information,


f) To develop a market information system,


g) To promote or cause to be promoted mutual cooperation among Organisations and Associations,


h) To promote mutual cooperation in international cooperatives movement and to coordinate and expand relations,


i) To promote good governance in the cooperative sector,


j) To carry out such other functions as referred to in the bylaws.

## Chapter 4 Bylaws and Internal Procedures

### Section 27: Bylaws to be framed

1) The Cooperative Organization shall frame bylaws for conducting its business subject to this Act and the Rules, Directives, Standards, and procedures framed under this Act.


2) The byelaws referred to in sub-Section (1) shall come into force after being approved by the Registrar or the officer authorised by him or her.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (2), the Registrar shall obtain consultation with the Nepal Rastra Bank before approving the Bylaws of the Cooperative Bank.

---

### Section 28: Internal Procedures may be framed

1) A Cooperative Organisation may frame bylaws as necessary for conducting its business subject to this Act and the Rules, Directives, Standards, and Procedures framed under this Act.


2) The internal procedures referred to in sub-Section (1) shall come into force after it is approved by the General Meeting.

---

### Section 29: Amendment to Bylaws and Internal Procedures

1) Bylaws and internal procedures may be amended by the majority of the members of the General Meeting of the Cooperative Organisation.


2) The amendment to the Bylaws referred to in sub-Section (1) shall come into force after the approval by the Registrar or the officer authorised by him or her.

---

## Chapter 5 Membership

### Section 30: Membership of Organisation

1) The following Nepali citizens having completed the age of sixteen years may become members of an Organisation:-


(a) Residing within the working area of the Organisation,


(b) Having subscribed at least one share of the Organisation,


c) Having agreed to comply with the terms and conditions referred to in the Bylaws of the Organisation,


(d) Having agreed to bear the responsibility of the Organisation,


(e) Not having had transactions that are competitive with the transactions being carried out by the Organisation.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, it shall hinder any agencies of the Government of Nepal, Provincial Government, local level, cooperative and community schools, trusts, local clubs, non-profit making organisations Registered at a local level, production-oriented or service-oriented organisations and associations, consumers groups to become a member of the Organization.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, it shall not hinder a Cooperative Organisation from becoming a member of a health Cooperative Organisation.

---

### Section 31: Application to be submitted to acquire membership

1) The concerned person willing to obtain membership in the Organization shall submit an application to the committee of the Organisation.


2) The committee shall, within thirty-five days from the date of submission of the application pursuant to sub-Section (1), have to take a decision whether or not to confer membership subject to this Act, Rules and Byelaws framed under this Act.


3) While taking a decision pursuant to sub-Section (2), in case the committee decides not to confer membership, it shall inform the applicant within seven days specifying the reason thereof.


4) The concerned person may file a complaint to the official authorised by the Registrar against such Organization within thirty days from the date of receipt of the information referred to in sub-Section (3).


5) The official authorised by the Registrar may, if it finds it appropriate to confer the membership following an inquiry on the complaint received pursuant to sub-Section (5), order the concerned Organization to confer the membership.


6) If the order pursuant to sub-Section (5) is received, the concerned organisation shall confer membership on the applicant within seven days of the date of receipt and inform the official authorised by the registrar.

---

### Section 32: Restriction on membership

1) No person shall be a member of more than one Organization of the same nature at the same local level.


Provided that in case a person is a member of more than one Organization of the same nature at the same local level before the commencement of this Act, he/she has to retain membership of only one Organization within a period of three years from the date of commencement of this Act.


2.) In case any agency of the Government of Nepal or any artificial person, other than those referred to in sub-Section (2) of Section 30, is a member of any Organization at the commencement of this Act, it shall terminate the membership within five years from the date of being the member.


Provided that this sub-section shall not apply in the case of health and education Cooperative Organizations registered prior to the commencement of this Act.

---

### Section 33: May become members

1) The District Sectoral Organisations may become members of the District Sectoral Cooperative Association referred to in Section 4


2) The Organisations in the district and district Sectoral Associations may become members of the District Cooperative Association referred to in Section 5.


3) The Sectoral Organisations in the province and District Sectoral Associations may become members of the Province Sectoral Cooperative Association referred to in Section 6.


4) Organisations in the Province, District Sectoral Cooperative Associations, District Cooperative Organisations and Province Sectoral Cooperative Associations may become members of the Province Cooperative Association.


5) Sectoral Organisations, District Sectoral Associations and Province Sectoral Associations may become members of the Central Sectoral Cooperative Association referred to in Section 8.


6) District sectoral organisations, district cooperative associations, provincial sectoral associations, provincial cooperative associations, and central sectoral cooperative associations may become members of the National Federation of Co-operatives referred to in Section 10.


7) Only Organisations and Associations may become members of the Cooperatives Bank.


8) The process of acquiring membership under this Section shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 34: Termination of membership

1) Membership of any member shall be terminated in the following circumstances:-


(a) In case a member withdraws its membership,


(b) In case a member is absent from the Annual General Meeting for three consecutive times without notice,


(c) In case a member frequently violates the provisions it has to comply with according to this Act, Rules or Bylaws framed under this Act.


(d) In the case of a member who has no qualifications referred to in Section 30.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), in case any member has any amount received from, or to be repaid to, a Cooperative Organization, the membership shall not be terminated until such an amount is settled or if such a member has obtained any credit or has any liability to be met or has guaranteed on behalf of any other member, the membership shall not be terminated until such liability is absolved.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in this Section, no one shall be removed from the membership after the decision is taken to hold the General Meeting and until the General Meeting is held.


4) Other matters as to the termination of membership shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 35: Facilities not to be entitled to

In case any member does not repay any liability to be repaid to any Cooperative Organisation within the fixed period or frequently violates the provisions of this Act, Rules or Bylaws framed under this Act, such a shall not be entitled to the facilities as other members.

## Chapter 6 General Meeting, Board of Directors and Account Supervision Committee

### Section 36: General Meeting

1) There shall be a General Meeting as the supreme body of a Cooperative Organization.


2) All members of a Cooperative Organization shall be the members of the General Meeting.


3) The General Meeting of a Cooperative Organization shall be as follows:-


(a) Preliminary General Meeting,


(b) Annual General Meeting,


(c) Extraordinary General Meeting.

---

### Section 37: Functions, Duties and Powers of Preliminary General Meeting

Functions, duties and powers of the preliminary General Meeting shall be as follows:


(a) To get information on actions taken and financial transactions made until the day preceding the holding of the preliminary General Meeting,


(b) To approve the annual program and budget for the current fiscal year,


(c) To approve reports and financial statements,


(d) To elect Committee and Accounts Supervision Committee as specified in the Bylaws,


(e) To adopt internal procedures,


(f) To appoint an Auditor and determine his or her remuneration,


(g) To carry out such other functions as referred to in the bylaws.

---

### Section 38: Functions, Duties and Powers of Annual General Meeting

Functions, duties and powers of the General Meeting shall be as follows:-


(a) To approve the annual program and budget,


(b) To approve the annual audit report,


(c) To elect and dissolve the Board of Directors and Accounts Supervision Committee,


(d) To remove from the office of a director, convener or member of the Accounts Supervision Committee,


(e) To adopt the annual report of the Board of Directors or of the Accounts Supervision Committee,


(f) To adopt Byelaws and internal procedures,


(g) To appoint an Auditor and determine his or her remuneration,


(h) To make a decision as to the unification or dissolution of the Organization,


(i) To determine incentives, including remuneration,


(j) To accept external liabilities,


(k) To write off the liability of a member,


(l) To issue necessary directives to the Board of Directors,


(m) To carry out such other functions as referred to in the bylaws.

---

### Section 39: Meeting General Meeting

1) The Board shall call a preliminary General Meeting within three months of the Cooperative Organization’s registration.


2) The Board shall call an Annual General Meeting within six months from the completion of each fiscal year.


3) The Board shall call an Extraordinary General Meeting in any of the following circumstances:-


(a) On the recommendation of the Accounts Supervision Committee pursuant to clause (g) of sub-Section (1) of Section 49,


(b) In case the Board approves the resolution moved by any Director to call an Extraordinary General Meeting,


(c) In case the Board decides to call an Extraordinary General Meeting for any particular business,


(d) In case fifteen percent of the members submit an application to the Board to call an Extraordinary General Meeting showing reasons thereof,


(e) In case the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar directs pursuant to sub-Section (1) of Section 40,


(f) In case the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar orders pursuant to sub-Section (2) of Section 42.

---

### Section 40: Directives may be given to call an Extraordinary General Meeting

1) Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar may direct the Cooperative Organization to call an Extraordinary General Meeting in case any of the following circumstances are found while carrying out inspection or supervision of any Cooperative Organization or while inquiring into a complaint:-


(a) In case of commission of any act against values, norms and principles of cooperatives,


(b) In case of commission of any act against this Act, the Rules, Byelaws and internal procedures framed under this Act,


(c) In case serious flaws are identified in the course of inspection or supervision of a Cooperative Bank by the Nepal Rastra Bank,


(d) In case of frequent violation of the directives issued by the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar,


(e) In case the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar issues an order pursuant to sub-Section (2) of Section 42.


2) In case of receipt of the directive to call the General Meeting pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Board shall call the General Meeting within Thirty-five days from the date of receipt of the directive, and discussions shall be held on the complaint, or the matter identified in the course of inspection and report thereof shall be submitted to the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar.


3) In case the Board fails to call the General Meeting within the period referred to in sub-Section (1) and (2), the authority authorised by the Registrar in case of the organisation, District Sectoral Cooperative Association and District Cooperative Association; and the Registrar in case of Provincial Sectoral Cooperative Association, provincial cooperative Association, Specialized Cooperative Association, Central Sectoral Cooperative Association, National Cooperatives Federation and Cooperative Bank, may call such a General Meeting.


4) The quorum for the General Meeting of a Cooperative Organization shall be at least fifty-one percent of the number of the then-existing members.


Provided that in case the quorum could not be constituted in the General Meeting called for the first time, it shall be called again within seven days. If one-third of the members, along with the majority of the members of the Board of Directors, are present in the General Meeting called again, the quorum for the General Meeting shall be deemed to be constituted.


5) While convening the General Meeting by a Cooperative Organization having two thousand or more members, the General Meeting may be convened on the same agenda in a local body or a ward thereof on the basis of a prescribed number by sending the members of the Board of Directors, representatives may be selected to certify the decisions of the General Meeting and the General Meeting may take final decisions in the presence of such representatives.

---

### Section 41: Board of Directors

1) There shall be a Board of Directors in a Cooperative Organization elected by the General Meeting.


2) The Board referred to in sub-Section (1) shall, to the extent possible, ensure the presence of at least thirty-three percent women members.


3) No more than one person of the same family may become a candidate and be elected at the same tenure as a director and a member of the Accounts Committee.


4) No director of any Cooperative Organization can become an employee or a director of any other Cooperative Organization except the Cooperative Organization in which he/she is a director.


Provided that it shall not hinder a Director of a Cooperative Organization from having an annual turnover of less than twenty million rupees from working as an employee in the same cooperative Organization. In case any Director of a Cooperative Organization having an annual turnover of more than twenty million rupees is also working as an employee, such a Director shall give up the position of an employee within four years from the date of commencement of this Act and manage for another employee. The committee referred to in Section 51 shall specify the maximum ceiling of the amount with regard to the existing conditions of the market and the rate of inflation.


5) A person may become a director of only one Cooperative Organization at a time.


6) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (4) and (5), in case any person has become a director in more than one Cooperative Organization or an employee in the same or another Cooperative Organization, he/she shall have to be a director of only one Cooperative Organization or an employee, as the case may be within one year from the commencement of this Act.


7) The term of the office of the Board shall be as follows:-


(a) Four years in the case of an organisation or Association or Federation,


(b) Four years in the case of Cooperative Bank.


8) Other provisions relating to meetings of the Board shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 42: Election of Board

1) The Board shall have to conduct the election of another Board before the expiry of its term of office.


2) In case information is received that the election of the Board is not held pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar may order the concerned Board to complete the election within six months from the date of receipt of such information.


3) In case the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar issues the order pursuant to sub-Section (2), the concerned Board shall hold the election within the time stipulated in such an order and inform the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar about the same.


4) In case the election is not held as referred to in sub-Section (3), the Registrar or authority authorised by the Registrar shall carry out entire actions relating to the election of the Board having involved representative of the higher association if any of such Organization in which it is a member.


5) It shall be the duty of the concerned Organization and the Board to render assistance in the election referred to in sub-Section (4).


6) Entire costs to be incurred in the case of conducting the election referred to in sub-Section (4) shall be borne by the concerned Cooperative Organization.


7) The Board shall carry out its functions as provided for in the Bylaws until the next election of the Board is held under this Section.

---

### Section 43: Functions, duties and powers of Board

In addition to the functions, duties, and powers referred to elsewhere in this Act, the functions, duties and powers of the Board shall be as follows:-


(a) To operate the Cooperative Organization according to norms, values and principles of Cooperatives,


(b) To carry out or cause to be carried out financial and administrative functions,


(c) To call preliminary General Meetings, Annual General Meetings, Extraordinary General Meetings,


(d) To implement or cause to be implemented the resolutions of the General Meeting,


(e) To prepare policies, plans, budget and annual programs of the Cooperative Organization and to submit them to the General Meeting,


(f) To grant membership of the Cooperative Organization and to remove from membership,


(g) To carry out functions relating to share transfer and withdrawal,


(h) To acquire membership of the concerned Association,


(i) To frame Byelaws and internal procedures and to submit them in the General Meeting,


(j) To carry out or cause to be carried out necessary functions for the promotion of transactions and commercial interests subject to the working areas of the Cooperative Organization,


(k) To carry out such hold other functions as prescribed.

---

### Section 44: Directors not to continue to hold the office

A Director shall not continue to hold the office in any of the following circumstances:-


(a) In case the resignation tendered by him or her is accepted by the Board,


(b) In case a decision is taken to remove him or her from the office of Director pursuant to sub-Section (1) of Section 45,


(c) In case he/she is the director of another Cooperative Organisation as well,


(d) In case he/she is an incumbent employee in the same Organisation or another organisation,


Provided that in case of the circumstance referred to in the proviso to sub-Section (4) of Section 41 shall be as stated there.


(e) In case he/she is a member of the Accounts Supervision Committee in the same Organisation or another organisation,


(f) In case he/she dies.

---

### Section 45: Directors may be removed from Office

1) The General Meeting may, by a resolution adopted by its majority, remove a director from the office of a director in any of the following circumstances:-


(a) In case he/she incurs loss or damage to the concerned Cooperative organisation by committing fiscal embezzlement,


(b) In case he/she discloses confidentiality of transaction of the concerned Cooperative Organization in an unauthorised manner,


(c) In case he/she is involved in the same nature of business or transaction with the concerned Cooperative Organization in a competitive manner,


(d) In case he/she commits any act against the interests of the concerned Cooperative Organization,


(e) In case he/she is physically or mentally incapable of working,


(f) In case any director does not have qualifications referred to in this Act, Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act.


2) Before adopting a resolution to remove any director from the office, such a director shall be provided with a reasonable opportunity to defend himself or herself at the General Meeting.


3) In case any director fails to submit his or her defence within the period referred to in sub-Section (2), or in case his or her defence is not satisfactory, the General Meeting may remove him or her from the office thereafter.


4) The member removed from the director pursuant to sub-Section (3) is not eligible to become a candidate for two terms of office.


5) In case any director is removed from the office pursuant to sub-Section (3), the General Meeting may elect another person as the director for the remaining term of the office.

---

### Section 46: Not to be involved in the decision-making process having conflict of personal interest

1) The director shall be engaged in the decision-making process that involves his or her personal interests.


2) No Director shall carry out or cause to be carried out any act of Cooperative Organization that renders benefits only to him or her.


3) In case any director carries out or causes to carry out any act in contravention of sub-Section (1) or beyond his or her working areas, such a director shall be personally liable for such act and in case such an act renders loss or damage to Cooperative Organization, to a member or any other person, such loss or damage shall be recovered from him or her.

---

### Section 47: Dissolution of Board

1) The General Meeting may dissolve the Board in any of the following circumstances:-


(a) In case transactions of the Cooperative Organization are at risk due to malafide acts of the Board,


(b) In case of failure to pay the liabilities to be paid by the Cooperative Organization within the specified time,


(c) In case of the commission of an act against the objective and business specified in the bylaws,


(d) In case the Board does not fulfil its responsibilities,


(e) In case of frequent violation of this Act or conditions referred to in Rules or the directives issued by the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar.


2) In case the Board is dissolved pursuant to sub-Section (1), the General Meeting shall elect a new Board.


3) In case the Board does not comply with the directive issued by the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar under this Act or Rules framed under this Act or on the basis of gravity of the subject matter found in the complaint filed or found in the course of inspection according to the report submitted to pursuant to sub-Section (2) of Section 40, the Registrar or office in-charge may give a time of six months for reform and if no reform is taken place even within that period, such a Board shall be dissolved.


4) In case the Board is dissolved pursuant to sub-Section (3), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall have to form an ad hoc Board as prescribed for holding an election of another Board within three months from the date of dissolution so made and to operate the daily transactions of the Cooperative Organization until the election is held.


5) Entire expenses incurred in the case of holding the next election under this Section shall be borne by the concerned Cooperative Organization.


6) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, the Registrar shall obtain consultation from the Nepal Rastra Bank before dissolving the Board of a Cooperative Bank, and it shall be done according to the advice of the Nepal Rastra Bank in this regard.

---

### Section 48: Formation of Accounts Supervision Committee

1) In order to strengthen the internal control system in a Cooperative Organization, the General Meeting shall form through election an Accounts Supervision Committee comprising one convener and two members having met the prescribed qualifications.


2) More than one member of the same family shall not stand as a candidate or get elected at the same tenure as a director convener or member of the Accounts Supervision Committee of the same Cooperative Organization.

---

### Section 49: Functions, duties and powers of Accounts Supervision Committee

1) The Functions, duties and powers of the Accounts Supervision Committee shall be as follows:-


(a) To conduct or cause to be conducted internal auditing of the Cooperative Organization in every quarter,


(b) To comply with or cause to be complied with the basic principles of auditing while conducting internal auditing,


(c) To inspect and evaluate and cause to be inspected or evaluated financial transactions,


(d) To have regular supervision of the actions and activities of the Board and to provide necessary suggestions to the Board,


(e) To monitor whether or not directives issued or decisions made by the General Meeting and decision of the Board have been implemented,


(f) To submit to the General Meeting accounts, report and annual report on supervision of the functions of the Board,


(g) To recommend the Board to call a meeting of the extraordinary General Meeting showing the reasons thereof that adverse impact is caused in the interests of any Cooperative Organization due to non-compliance of the recommendations made by it frequently; that there have been embezzlement or massive misuse of cash or kind assets of such organisation or in case the organisation is likely to undergo serious financial crises.


(h) To make recommendations of names of three persons to be appointed as internal auditors, if required.


2) The convener and members of the Accounts Supervision Committee shall not be involved in daily and administrative functions of the Cooperative Organization.

### Section 50: Transaction of Savings and Credits to be made concentrating on Member

1) A Cooperative Organization may accept savings only from its members, to mobilise such savings and disperse credits only to the members.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), no sectoral or multi-purpose Organization other than the Organization Registered with the main objective of making savings and credit transactions shall be allowed to make savings and credit transactions as the main transaction.


Provided that in case any sectoral or multi-purpose Organization has been carrying out the main objective of making transactions of savings and credit at the commencement of this Act, such an organisation shall, within a period of three years, make provisions of not carrying out the transaction of savings and credits as the main transaction and to have the main transaction of the matter which was stated at the time of registration of the Organization.


3) Except otherwise specified by the Registrar, service charges and renewal fees on credits disbursed by Cooperative Organization to members shall be as stated in the procedures of the concerned Organization.


4) The difference in the rate of interest on savings and on credits (spread) shall not be more than six percent.


5) The interest to be charged on credit disbursed by a Cooperative Organization shall not be charged having capitalised the interest in the main credit amount.


16) Cooperatives shall not use the savings of their members for any purpose other than for loan investment among themselves, purchase of bonds issued by the Government of Nepal or shares of cooperative banks or small farmers’ microfinance institutions, purchase of real estate, construction of infrastructure, investment in transactions, purchase of shares of firms, companies or any other banks or for any other purpose.


7) A Cooperative Organization may collect deposits as much as fifteen times the primary capital.


8) An Organisation or Association may not make credit investments to members other than the members who are listed at the time of registration until a period of three months is completed after acquiring the membership.

### Section 53: Capital

### Section 54: Functions of Cooperative Bank

The Cooperative Bank shall, subject to this Act, the Rules Byelaws and other prevailing laws in force, carry out the following functions amongst the members of the Organization or Association in order:-


(a) To disburse credits to organisation or with association on the prescribed ratio as fixed and current capital for the purpose of agriculture, industries, services, trade, energy, tourism as well,


(b) To accept savings from members and to repay such savings,


(c) To enter into agreements with Association or the agencies of Organization or Government of Nepal or Provincial government agencies or local level being concentrated to members for development of development and promotion of cooperatives and to make joint financing,


(d) To issue a letter of guarantee on behalf of member Organization or Association, to accept mortgage or movable or immovable assets in lieu of such guarantee or accept assets guarantee with consent of a third party,


(e) To borrow institutional credits as may be necessary,


(f) To recover interests on credits, bonds and so on,


(g) To carry out inspection, supervision, and monitoring with regard to whether or not the credits disbursed are utilised,


(h) To purchase or sell bonds issued by the Government of Nepal or Treasury Bills issued by the Nepal Rastra Bank,


(i) To exchange information among Organisations or Associations or members associated with them,


(j) To accept deposits, make payments and transfer amounts through electronic instruments or devices,


(k) To borrow or reborrow credits time and again as may be necessary,


(l) To invest or use the amount received from internationally recognised Organisations related to cooperatives, donor agencies, and banks as development, finance and credits for the promotion of projects and to manage such resources,


(m) To carry out or cause to carry out studies, research, and surveys relating to project establishment, operation and appraisal,


(n) To carry out other banking transactions after obtaining a license from the Nepal Rastra Bank.

### Section 55: Prohibition on Disbursing Credit

No Cooperative Bank may disburse credit against the collateral or security of its own shares.

---

### Section 56: No Distribution of Dividend

No Cooperative Bank may declare or distribute dividends without meeting the preliminary expenses, the loss incurred in the last year, the capital fund, the risk-bearing fund, and the reserve fund.

---

### Section 57: Prohibition of Purchase of Goods with Commercial Objectives

A Cooperative Bank shall not purchase goods or immovable assets with the purpose of carrying out trade or business, except the goods or immovable assets required for it or for carrying out transactions under this Act and in the situation referred to in sub-Section (6) of Section 79.

---

### Section 58: Books and Accounts

1) A Cooperative Bank shall have to maintain books and accounts according to the format approved by the Nepal Rastra Bank, showing the actual status of its transactions.


2) The books and accounts referred to in sub-Section (1) shall contain the actual status of the Cooperative Bank, its investments, credits disbursed and recovered and borrowed, and another financial statement, detailed statements of income and expenditures and statements of assets and liabilities.

---

### Section 59: Nepal Rastra Bank may take over Management

1) the Nepal Rastra Bank may, in any of the following circumstances, by furnishing information to the Department, suspend the Board of Directors of the Cooperative Bank and take over the management on its own or cause another agency to be taken it over:


(a) In case it is found from the auditing report of the Cooperative Bank or inspection report of the bank that it fails to comply with the directives issued by the Nepal Rastra Bank from time to time,


(b) In case it commits any act against the interests of members and incurs a loss to members,


(c) In case the operation of the Cooperative Bank is not efficient and effective, thereby, loss is caused to the Cooperative Bank.


2) Before the Board of the Cooperative Bank is to be suspended pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Cooperative Bank shall be provided with an opportunity of seven days to defend itself.


3) Upon an opportunity to defend given pursuant to sub-Section (2), in case the Cooperative Bank fails to submit the defence or the defence so submitted does not seem satisfactory, the Nepal Rastra Bank shall suspend the Board of the concerned bank and take over the management on its own or cause the management to be taken over by any other agency.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, while taking over the management by the Nepal Rastra Bank on its own or by any other agency, a representative of the Department shall also be involved.

---

### Section 60: De-registration

1) In case the Cooperative Bank seems incapable of paying the debt or external liability due to be paid, the Nepal Rastra Bank may advise the Registrar to de-registration the Cooperative Bank.


2) Before giving the advice referred to in sub-Section (1), the Nepal Rastra Bank shall have to provide such a Cooperative Bank with an opportunity of 15 days to defend itself.


3) In case of receipt of advice to revoke the registration pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Registrar shall deregister the Cooperative Bank and appoint a liquidator pursuant to Section 89.

---

### Section 61: Confidentiality to be maintained

(1) Confidentiality of books and accounts relating to transactions and statements and documents of financial transactions of the cooperative bank shall be maintained.


(2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), there shall be no hindrance to providing the books and, accounts and statements of financial transactions for any of the following purposes:


(a) To court in the course of hearing a case or any other legal proceedings,


(b) To auditor in the course of auditing of accounts,


(c) To the concerned official in the course of inspection,


(d) To the person or authority having authority to ask for the details according to the prevailing laws,


(e) To its own members.

---

### Section 62: Liquid Assets to be maintained

A Cooperative bank shall have to maintain liquid assets as specified by the Nepal Rastra Bank.

## Chapter 9 Mobilisation of Financial Resources

### Section 63: Provisions concerning sale and withdrawal of shares

1) A Cooperative Organisation may sell shares to its members.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), no Cooperative Organisation shall sell the shares that are more than twenty percent of its total share capital to a single member.


Provided that this restriction shall not be applicable to an organisation or body under the full or partial ownership of the Government of Nepal.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), a Cooperative Organisation may sell shares to an organisation or agency under the full or partial ownership or control of the Government of Nepal, in addition to its share members.


4) The face value per share of a Cooperative Organisation shall be one hundred rupees.


5) The share capital of a Cooperative Organisation shall be as stated in the Byelaws.


6) No Cooperative Organisation shall be allowed to sell or purchase shares in the open market.


7) Share of a member, which is the principal capital of a Cooperative Organisation, shall not be sold by auction for any debt or liability other than the debt or liability of the same Cooperative Bank.

---

### Section 64: Provisions Concerning Withdrawal of Amount and Operation of Accounts

1) In case any member desires to get back the money withdrawn from the membership of the Cooperative Organisation, his or her liabilities, if any, have to be paid, and the remaining amount shall be refunded within three months of the date of such payment of liabilities.


2) In case any member demands to withdraw the savings he/she has deposited in a Cooperative Organisation, his or her liabilities, if any, are deducted and the remaining amount shall be refunded to him or her.


3) A Cooperative organisation may operate all types of savings accounts.

---

### Section 65: Loans or Grants may be obtained

1) A Cooperative Organisation may borrow loans or accept a grant from any native or foreign bank or institution, or from any other agency, or work in collaboration.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), before availing a loan or grant from a foreign bank or agency, approval of the Ministry of Finance shall be obtained on the recommendation of the Ministry.

---

### Section 66: Security of Government of Nepal may be obtained

1) In case a Cooperative Organisation requires security against a loan to be borrowed from a foreign bank or agency, it may, for that purpose, request the Ministry of Finance through the Ministry.


2) In case of receipt of a request pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Ministry of Finance may provide security for such loan.


3) While providing security pursuant to sub-Section (2), the Ministry of Finance may prescribe necessary conditions.

## Chapter 10 Fund of Cooperative Organisation

### Section 67: Find of a Cooperative Organisation

1) The following amount shall be deposited in the fund of a Cooperative Organisation


(a) Amount received from the sale of shares,


(b) Amount received as savings,


(c) Amount received as loan,


(d) Amount of grant received from Government of Nepal,


(e) Grant or assistance received from a foreign government or an international organisation,


(f) Amount accrued from commercial activities,


(g) Membership entry fee.


2) Approval of the Ministry of Finance shall be obtained before obtaining the amount referred to in clause (e) of sub-Section (1).

---

### Section 68: Reserve Fund

1) There shall be a reserve fund in a Cooperative organisation.


2) The fund referred to in sub-section (1) shall have the following amount:


(a) At least twenty-five percent of the net savings of a fiscal year,


(b) Capital grant amount provided by any organisation, association or agency,


(c) Amount received from the fixed assets,


(d) Amount received from any other sources.


3) The Amount of the fund referred to in sub-section (1) shall be indivisible.

---

### Section 69: Secured Capital Redemption Fund

1) There shall be a secured capital redemption repayment fund in a Cooperative organisation.


2) At least twenty-five percent of the amount remaining after segregating the amount referred to in clause (a) of sub-section (2) of Section 68 in the reserve fund shall annually be deposited in the fund referred to in sub-section (1).


3) The amount referred to in sub-section (1) shall be made available to the concerned member based on the annual turnover of the member as prescribed.

---

### Section 70: Cooperative Promotion Fund

1) There shall be a Cooperative Promotion Fund in a Cooperative Organisation.


2) For the promotion of cooperative enterprises, zero-point two five percent of the amount allocated after segregating the amount referred to in clause (a) of sub[1]Section (2) of Section 68 in the reserve fund shall annually be deposited in the fund referred to in sub-section (1).


3) The fund referred to in sub-section (1) shall be at the Ministry of Cooperatives and Poverty Alleviation.


4) Operating procedures of the Fund shall be as prescribed.


5) The Ministry may make available the amount as prescribed in proportion to the amount deposited in the fund referred to in sub-section (1).


6) Amount shall be made available as prescribed from the fund referred to in sub[1]Section (1) to the National Federation of Cooperatives; the concerned Central Sectoral Cooperatives Association; Provincial Sectoral Cooperatives Association; Provincial Cooperatives Association; District Sectoral Cooperatives Association; and District Cooperatives Association.


7) For the purpose of distribution of the amount deposited in the Fund referred to in sub-Section (1), there shall be a committee under the convenorship of the Minister of the concerned Ministry and comprising of the co-chair of the National Cooperatives Development Board, Secretary of the Ministry, Joint Secretary of the Ministry of Finance, chair of the National Cooperatives Federation, Chair of the Central Credits and Savings Association and the Registrar as the members.


8) Out of the amount received pursuant to sub-section (6), at least seventy-five percent shall be expended in infrastructure of cooperative business, and the remaining amount shall be expended in activities such as enterprise promotion, education, information, and training, market promotion, and organisation and Association monitoring.


9) The concerned Association shall separately maintain a record of the amount received pursuant to sub-section (6) and get it audited according to the prevailing laws. A statement thereof shall be made public as prescribed.


10) Details as to the expenses incurred and achievements made from the amount received pursuant to sub-section (6) shall be submitted to the National Cooperative Development Board.


11) In case the amount received pursuant to sub-section (6) is found to have been misused or used in other activity, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall recover such amount and prohibit making the amount of the fund available.

---

### Section 71: Provisions concerning Other Funds

1) There may be other funds in a Cooperative Organisation, in addition to the fund referred to in sections 67, 68, 69 and 70.


2) The amount in the fund referred to in sub-section (1) may be utilised as stated in the Byelaws of the fund to achieve the objectives of the fund, including the distribution of dividends.


Provided that the amount of share dividend of one year shall not be more than eighteen percent of the share capital.


3) Based on the total assets and liabilities of other transactions of the Cooperative Bank, the bank shall have other funds, too, as specified by the Nepal Rastra Bank.

(b) Annual programs, policies and plans,

## Chapter 13 Exemptions, Facilities and Discounts

### Section 78: Exemptions, Facilities and Discounts

1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing Nepal laws, a Cooperative Organisation shall be entitled to the following exemptions, facilities and discounts:


(a) No Cooperative Organisation shall be required to register any deed other than the deed relating to immovable assets,


(b) No income stamp ticket fees or registration fees shall be charged to the Cooperative Organisation while it procures necessary land or other immovable assets for its office building or service centre construction.


Provided that –


1) An Income stamp fee and registration fee shall be charged according to the prevailing laws when procuring other immovable assets, including land, for commercial purposes.


2) In case the immovable assets, including land procured with the registration fees exemption, are not used and are sold, the registration fee so exempted shall be repaid.


(c) No fee shall be charged while a Cooperative Organisation lends credits or while endorsing the deed of pledge.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, no income tax shall be levied on the amount allocated for the reserve fund pursuant to clause (a) of sub-Section (2) of Section 68 by a Cooperative Organization, the amount de[1]posited in the secured capital redemption fund pursuant to sub-Section (2) of Section 69 and the amount deposited in the cooperatives promotion fund pursuant to sub-Section (2) of Section 70.


Provided that it shall be according to the laws on the tax liabilities of members who receive an amount from the secured capital redemption fund.


3) The Government of Nepal may, by notification in the Nepal Gazette, grant exemption according to the prevailing laws on the customs duty or value-added tax levied on machinery, industrial and agricultural inputs, equipment, spare parts, raw materials, and means of transport imported by a Cooperative Organisation for its purpose.


4) The Government of Nepal may, after publishing a notification in the Nepal Gazette, grant whole or partial exemption according to the prevailing laws on the excise duty or value-added tax levied on goods produced by a Cooperative organisation for its purpose.


5) The Government of Nepal may, having published a notification in the Nepal Gazette, grant exemption according to the prevailing laws on the export tax on the goods produced by a Cooperative Organisation and may provide a cash grant for export as is given to an industry according to the prevailing laws.


6) In addition to the exemptions referred to in this Section, the Cooperative Organization carrying out industrial enterprises shall be provided with the exemption, facilities and protection according to the prevailing laws which an industry is being provided with and the Cooperative Organization carrying out construction and operation of infrastructure structures, or carrying out special industries, enterprises, or services shall be provided with those facilities as are being provided to such industry, enterprise and service.


7) Government of Nepal, Provincial Government or Local Level may grant partial or full exemption in any type of tax to be imposed according to the prevailing law or make available special financial facilities or technical support, for promotion of cooperative farming by deprived rural women, persons with disability, freed kamaiyas, freed haliyas, landless farmers, unemployed labourers, Dalits and minority communities of marginalised groups or self-employment enterprises based on labour or skills of members.


8) In case labourers desires to bring into cooperative any sick public or private industry and to operate it again on their ownership, Government of Nepal may, considering the nature of the industry, provide grant for seed capital, loan or transfer of the ownership with discounted loan or provide tax exemption in trans[1]fer of private ownership, or provide guarantee or other appropriate assistance.


9) Government of Nepal may, by a notification in the Nepal gazette, provide the facilities of whole or partial exemption of the excise duty or value added tax to be levied on the share participation, infrastructure development, land or land acquisition, payment of compensation for land acquired and production of goods by large scale commercial projects of cooperative industrial village, marketplace development and so on which operate in special federal form.


10) The procedures for the acquisition of exemption, facilities and discounts under this Section shall be as prescribed.

## Chapter 14 Recovery of Debt and Due Amount

### Section 79: Debts to be recovered

1) In case any members of a Cooperative Organization fails comply with the loan agreement or any terms or condition or bond thereof; does not repay the principal, interests and penal interests within the time stated in the deed; or the amount of loan is not used in the current scheme for which it was availed and found embezzled; the concerned Cooperative Organization may recover its principal, interests and penal interests having sold by auction the collateral security mortgaged by such a member while availing the loan or by any other means.


2) In case a member who has mortgaged a collateral in a Cooperative Organisation transfers the title of such a collateral to anyone in any manner or the value of such a collateral decreases for any reason, such borrower may be asked for additional collateral within a certain time frame.


3) In case the borrower does not submit additional collateral pursuant to sub-section (2) or the principal, interests and penal interests could not be recovered even from the collateral security mortgaged pursuant to sub-Section (1) or (2), the principal, interests and penal interests may be recovered from other assets belonging to such a borrower.


4) The amount remaining after recovery of the principal, interests and penal interests or the expenses incurred while making the recovery of the principal, interests and penal interests shall be refunded to the concerned borrower.


5) While selling by auction of collateral security or any other assets of a borrower by a Cooperative Organization under this Section, it shall write to the concerned Land Revenue Office to transfer the title of such collateral security, by registration or writing off the name of the earlier owner, in the name of the person accepting the auction according to the prevailing law and the concerned Office shall also have to carry out registration or the writing off the title and inform the same to the concerned Cooperative Organization.


6) In case the assets of collateral mortgaged in auction under this Section are not accepted by anyone, the concerned Cooperative Organisation may itself accept such assets.


7) In case the Cooperative Organization itself accepts the collateral pursuant to sub-Section (6), it shall write to the concerned Land Revenue Office to transfer the title of such a collateral by registration or writing off the name of the earlier owner and the concerned Office shall also have to carry out the registration or the writing off accordingly.


8) Other matters as to the recovery of credits shall be as prescribed.

---

1) A blacklist along with name and other details of the person misusing the credit amount availed from a Cooperative Organization or not paying the principal and interests within the specified time may be publicised.

### Section 82: Credits Recovery Tribunal

### Section 83: Dues to recover

If anyone fails to pay an amount to be repaid to a Cooperative Organisation, the Cooperative Organisation may recover the amount and interest accrued thereon from the person’s personal property.

---

### Section 84: To be sent for freezing

2) In case of receipt of the request for freezing pursuant to sub-section (1), the concerned agency shall freeze accounts to suspend the sale or transfer of the title of assets of any person according to the laws.

---

### Section 85: Preference to be accorded

In case anyone fails to pay credit or any other liability and in case the Government of Nepal has any claim on the assets of such a person, the amount shall be segregated for such government claim, and there shall be preference for the Cooperative Organisation in the remaining amount.

## Chapter 15 Amalgamation, Dissolution, and Revocation of Registration

### Section 87: Provisions concerning Amalgamation and Division

1) Subject to this Act, two or more Cooperative Organisations may amalgamate and division with each other, or a Cooperative Organisation may be divided into two or more Cooperative Organisations based on geographical business areas.


2) While carrying out amalgamation or division pursuant to sub-Section (1), a decision shall be taken by the majority of the total number of members of the concerned Cooperative Organisation.


3) While taking a decision of amalgamation or division pursuant to sub-Section (1), the terms and conditions of amalgamation or division shall also be stated.


4) Other provisions relating to amalgamation or division shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 88: Dissolution and Revocation of Registration

1) The Board may, in regard to any Cooperative Organisation having taken a decision to dissolve such a Cooperative Organisation by the majority of the then prevailing members of the General Meeting, submit to the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar for approval of the dissolution in the following circumstances:


(a) In case it is not possible to attain the objectives and functions stated in the bylaws,


(b) In case it does not serve the interests of the members.


2) In case it seems appropriate to dissolve the Cooperative Organisation while inquiring into the application submitted pursuant to sub-Section (1), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may revoke the registration of such a Cooperative Organisation.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (2), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may revoke registration of a Cooperative Organisation in the following circumstances:


(a) In case it is inactive for a consecutive period of two years without any transaction,


(b) In case any Cooperative Organisation frequently commits any act in violation of this Act or Rules framed under this Act,


(c) In case it commits any act against its objectives referred to in the Byelaws,


(d) In case it commits an act against the values, norms and principles of cooperatives.


4) Before revoking the registration pursuant to sub-Section (2) or (3), the Registrar or the authorised representative of the Registrar shall provide a time of fifteen days to such a Cooperative Organisation for a hearing.


5) In case of revocation of registration of a Cooperative Organisation pursuant to sub-Section (2) or (3), such Cooperative Organisation shall be deemed to have been dissolved.


6) Notwithstanding anything contained sub-Section (2) or (3), the Registrar shall have to obtain approval of the Ministry before revoking registration of the Cooperative Organization which has obtained a grant amount exceeding the ceiling specified by Government of Nepal and of the Nepal Rastra Bank before revoking the registration of the Cooperative Bank.


7) In case of revocation of registration of a Cooperative Organisation pursuant to sub-Section (2) or (3), a registration certificate of such a Cooperative Organisation, Cooperative Association or Cooperative Bank shall be deemed to have been, ipso facto, void.


8) Nothing stated in this Section shall be deemed to have prejudiced the provisions relating to deregistration of the Cooperative Bank.

---

### Section 89: Appointment of a Liquidator

1) In case of revocation of a Cooperative Organisation pursuant to Section 88, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall appoint a liquidator.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in Sub-Section (1), the Registrar may appoint the authority authorised by the Registrar or an employee as liquidator in case of a Cooperative Organisation having the assets of the prescribed ceiling.

---

### Section 90: Provisions concerning Liquidation

1) The functions, duties, powers and remuneration of the liquidator of the Cooperative Organisation, the registration of which has been revoked under this Act and other provisions as to liquidation shall be as prescribed.


2) In case of appointment of a liquidator pursuant to sub-section (2) of Section 89, the entire expenses of the liquidator shall be borne by the Government of Nepal.

---

### Section 91: Utilisation of Assets after Liquidation

The utilisation of assets of any Cooperative Organisation remaining after payment of all liabilities upon the liquidation shall be as prescribed.

## Chapter 17 Inspection and Monitoring

### Section 95: Inspection and Checking of Accounts

1) The Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may at any time inspect or cause to be inspected a Cooperative Organisation and check or cause to be checked its accounts.


2) The Nepal Rastra Bank may at any time inspect or check accounts or cause them to be inspected or checked, as well as financial transactions of a Cooperative Bank or of a Cooperative Organisation that carries out financial transactions of an amount exceeding a ceiling.


3) For the purpose of sub-section (2), the Nepal Rastra Bank may conduct inspection or supervision, having deputed any officer or expert and asking for necessary information and statements from the Cooperative Bank.


4) While inspecting or checking accounts pursuant to sub-Section (1) or (2), the concerned Cooperative Organisation shall make available the information or statement required by the Nepal Rastra Bank, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar.


5) While inspecting or checking accounts pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2), in case it is found that functioning of any Cooperative Organization is not consistent with this Act, Rules, Directives, Standards, or procedures framed under this Act, the Nepal Rastra Bank or the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may give necessary directives to the concerned Cooperative Organization and it shall be the duty of the concerned Cooperative Organization to comply with such directives.


6) While carrying out inspection and supervision of the Cooperative Bank under this Section, the Nepal Rastra Bank may exercise its power under the prevailing laws.


7) The Nepal Rastra Bank shall make available to the Department the information received upon inspection and supervision of the Cooperative Bank.


8) The Cooperative Bank shall submit the financial statements and other necessary statistics to the Nepal Rastra Bank within the time and in the format specified by the Nepal Rastra Bank.

---

### Section 96: Investigation may be conducted

1) In case at least five percent members of a Cooperative Organization submit an application stating that the commercial transaction of the Cooperative Organization is not satisfactory, act is committed against interests of a member or against the objectives of the Cooperative Organization, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may conduct or cause to be conducted an investigation of the Cooperative Organization.


2) It shall be the duty of the concerned Board to make available a statement or documents while conducting an inspection pursuant to sub-Section (1).


3) Information of the investigation conducted pursuant to sub-Section (1) or (2) shall be made available to the concerned Cooperative Organisation in writing.

---

### Section 97: Special Provisions concerning Inspection and Monitoring of Organisation or Association carrying out transactions of savings and credits

1) The Organisation or Association having savings and credits as the main transaction shall follow the monitoring system as prescribed.


2) The Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall carry out inspection and monitoring of Organisations or Associations with savings and credits as the main transaction based on the prescribed monitoring system.


3) While carrying out inspection and monitoring of an organisation or Association pursuant to sub-Section (2), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may obtain the assistance of the Nepal Rastra Bank and other agencies.


4) Other matters as to inspection and monitoring of an organisation or Association having savings and credits as the main transaction shall be as prescribed.

### Section 98: Ministry to conduct Inspection and Monitoring

2) In case any directive referred to in sub-Section (1) is issued, it shall be the duty of the concerned Cooperative Organisation to comply with such directive.

(b) Status of compliance of this Act, or Rules and Byelaws framed under this Act in the Organisation or Association,


(c) Conditions of services and benefits being received by members,


(d) Level of participation of members,


(e) Economic and financial condition of the Organisation or Association and mobilisation of funds,


(f) Other matters as prescribed.


3) The Association may, while conducting monitoring under sub-section 2, if it finds any defaults in regard to activities of any organisation or association, give suggestion or issue direction to correct such defaults, and it shall be the duty of the Association or Organization, as the case may be, to comply with much suggestions or directing.


4) If the suggestions or directives given or issued under section (3) are not complied with 4, the recommendation shall be made to the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar for the actions.


5) Matters concerning suggestions or directives given or issued pursuant to sub-section (3) shall also be mentioned in the annual report of the Association.

(f) Details on the economic activities and financial condition of the Cooperative Organisations as prescribed,


(g) Condition of internal control in the Cooperative Organisations,


(h) Condition of good governance and accountability in Cooperative Organisations,


(i) Details of funds in the Cooperative Organisations,


(j) Necessary matters to be helpful in the policymaking of cooperatives,

(d) To bear the expenses of the Stabilisation Fund Governing Board and to utilise it in other functions as prescribed.


8) Other matters relating to the operation of the Stabilisation Fund shall be as prescribed.

## Chapter 18 Problematic Organisations or Associations

### Section 104: Organisation or Association may be declared problematic

1) In case any Organisation or Association is found to have the following circumstances from the inspection or checking of accounts conducted under this Act, the Registrar may recommend that the Ministry declare such Organisation or Association as a problematic Organisation or Association:


(a) Commission of any act against the interests of the members,


(b) Failure to fulfil the financial liabilities to be fulfilled by the Organisation or Association, or inability to bear the liabilities to be borne, or occurrence of a situation of inability to make payment,


(c) Failure to refund the savings of members according to the agreed terms and conditions,


(d) Operation of the Organisation or Association against this Act or the Rules and Byelaws framed under this Act,


(e) Emergence of a situation of probable insolvency of the Organisation or Association, or facing a significantly serious financial crisis,


(f) In case at least twenty-five members of an Organisation or Association submitted an application to the Registrar stating that the Organization or Association did not refund the savings amount of members within the time set to refund and it has been found from investigation that any of the circumstance situations referred to in clauses (a) to (e) has seen occurred.


2) In case the recommendation referred to in sub-section (1) is received, the Ministry may declare such an Organisation or Association as a problematic Organisation or Association.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, in case a Commission formed by Government of Nepal according to the prevailing laws recommends to declare any Organization or Association as problematic Organization or Association or if such a Commission has declared any Organization or Association as a problematic or based on the number of complaints filed in such a Commission and on rationality, the Ministry may declare such an Organization or Association as a problematic Organization or Association.

---

### Section 105: Formation of Management Committee

1) In case any Organisation or Association has been declared as a problematic Organisation or Association under this Act, the Government of Nepal may form a Management Committee to manage the assets and pay the liabilities of such Organisation or Association.


2) The Management Committee referred to in sub-section (1) shall comprise the following members:





(a)
A person appointed by Government of Nepal from amongst persons who have already become a judge of the High Court or worked in an equivalent position and has served as a special class officer of the Nepal Judicial Service
Chairperson


(b)
A person nominated by Government of Nepal from amongst persons who have already served as at least a first-class officer of the Nepal Civil Service
Member


(c)
One Director representative designated by the National Cooperatives Association
Member


(d)
Two persons nominated by Government of Nepal from amongst cooperative experts
Member


(e)
A person nominated by the Government of Nepal from amongst persons who have worked for at least a period of three years in at least the ninth level of a bank or financial institution, or in the supervision sector of banks or financial institutions, upon being a Chartered Accountant
Member


(f)
An Officer of Gazetted Second-Class level designated by the Government of Nepal
Member- Secretary



3) The term of office of the chairperson and members of the Management Committee shall be two years from the date of appointment, and the Government of Nepal may extend the term of office for a period not exceeding two years.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (3), in case the work performance of the chairperson and a member of the Management Committee is not satisfactory, the Government of Nepal may remove him or her from office at any time.


5) Before removing the chairperson or a member of the Management Committee pursuant to sub-section (3), a reasonable opportunity shall be given to him or her to defend himself or herself.


6) Government of Nepal shall fulfil the office of the Management Committee that has fallen vacant pursuant to sub-Section (4), subject to sub-Section (2).


7) Procedures of a meeting of the Management Committee shall be as determined by the Committee on its own.

---

### Section 106: Functions, duties and powers of Management Committee

The Functions, duties and powers of the Management Committee shall be as follows:


(a) To exercise all powers which the General Meeting, Board and Accounts Supervision Committee of the problematic Organisation or Association may exercise under this Act, or Rules and Byelaws framed under this Act,


(b) To get presence in the Management Committee of the members of the problematic Organization or Association, his or her family members, relatives, employees of the problematic Organization or Association, other Organization or Association or companies associated with the problematic Organization or Association, employees of such companies, other persons or organisations having relevant information or having transactions in collusion or the persons which the Management Committee deems appropriate, to inquire with them, record their statements require to submit relevant documents,


(c) To collect accurate information on assets and liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association and to collect, study, analyse and evaluate the statistics relating to them,


(d) To take custody of the assets of the problematic Organisation or Association,


(e) To take control of complete management, including decisions and records of the functioning of the problematic Organisation or Association,


(f) To recover the debts, due amount or other amount to be recovered by the problematic Organisation or Association, having followed the procedures of this Act or Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act,


(g) To sell by auction the assets under mortgage of the problematic Organisation or Association, having followed the procedures of this Act or Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act,


(h) To sell, use and manage the assets of the problematic Organisation or Association,


(i) To prepare records of savings and shares amounts of depositors and members of the problematic Organisation or Association,


(j) To repay or return the savings of the members and depositors,


(k) To exercise all powers conferred on the Liquidator by this Act or Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act for the management of assets of the problematic Organisation or Association and to pay the liabilities,


(l) In case it is found that director or employee of the problematic Organisation or Association or his or her family member has used whole or part of savings of depositors in any other company, or organisation or in any enterprise or business, to seize the assets so used and assets accrued from it and to sell by auction such assets and to take other necessary actions of recovery for payment to depositors,


(m) To legally defend on behalf of the problematic Organisation or Association,


(n) To seize the assets of the Organization or Association kept in the name of a director or employee of the problematic Organization or Association or his or her family member or in the name of any other person; or the movable or immovable assets purchased from the assets of the Organization or Association and to sell by auction such assets and to take other necessary actions of recovery for payment to depositors.

---

### Section 107: Inventory to be prepared

For the purpose of Section 106, the Management Committee shall prepare an inventory as follows:


(a) Name list of the members, depositors, borrowers of the problematic Organisation or Association and of the persons or organisations to which the Organisation or Association has to make payments,


(b) Inventory of immovable assets, including apartments in the name of the problematic Organisation, or Association, or having an investment of the Organisation or Association,


(c) Details of cash deposited in banks, gold, silver owned and share investment in the names of members and employees of the problematic Organisation or Association, or in the name of their family members,


(d) Record of land and buildings mortgaged by the problematic Organisation or Association for obtaining loans,


(e) Record of all movable or immovable assets of the problematic Organisation or Association located in Nepal or in any place abroad.

---

### Section 108: Provisions concerning Payment of Liabilities

1) The Management Committee shall set the following order of priority for payment of liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association:


(a) Payment of savings to members according to the terms and conditions set at the time of depositing the amount,


(b) Payment of government dues or claims on the assets of such an Organisation or Association or assets referred to in clause (l) of Section 106,


(c) Payment of persons paying an advance amount to the problematic Organisation or Association for the apartment or land,


(d) Payment of the amount to the creditors of the problematic Organisation or Association.


2) In case the assets of the problematic Organisation or Association are not sufficient to pay all liabilities of such a problematic Organisation or Association under this Section, the Management Committee may refund the savings of and make payment of liabilities to the members on a pro rata basis. While refunding savings of members in such a manner, the Management Committee shall accord priority to small depositors as prescribed.

---

### Section 109: Authority may be delegated

The Management Committee may, while managing assets of the problematic Organisation or Association, delegate some of its authority to the Chairperson or a member of the Management Committee or to a government officer to use and exercise.

---

### Section 110: To be borne from Assets of Organisation or Association

In case the expenses to be incurred in the course of managing assets of the problematic Organisation or Association cannot be met for the time being from the assets of such an Organisation or Association, the Government of Nepal may make available an amount for that purpose.

---

### Section 111: Authority to be suspended ipso facto

1) The authority of the Board, manager, and employees of the problematic Organisation or Association shall ipso facto be suspended from the date the Management Committee is formed under this Act.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), the Management Committee may for the purpose of management of assets and payment of liabilities, release the suspension and engage the members, manager and employees of the problematic Organization or Association for carrying out functions relating to management of assets and payment of liabilities of such Organization or Association.


3) In case the Management Committee deputes for work pursuant to sub-section (2), it shall be the duty of the concerned person to bear the responsibility accordingly.

---

### Section 112: Notice to be given for submission of liabilities

Once the Management Committee begins its work, it shall publish a public notice in a national-level daily newspaper to the members, depositors, and creditors of the problematic organisation or association specifying a time limit of at least fifteen days to submit claims for the amount to be repaid to them.

---

### Section 113: Audit Report to be prepared

The Management Committee shall, within six months from the date of commencement of works by it, prepare an audit report of the problematic Organisation or Association, and the report shall be made public.

---

### Section 114: Remedial Actions may be taken

The Management Committee, in case there are reasons and bases that the problematic Organisation or Association could operate again based on the audit report prepared under this Act, may carry out the following remedial actions:


(a) To order the Board to carry on management or functions and transactions,


(b) To ask to carry on management or functions and transactions having formed an interim committee from amongst the shareholding members of the concerned Organisation or Association,


(c) To dissolve the Board and call a General Meeting of such Organisation or Association and to cause the formation of a new Board for carrying on management or functions and transactions,


(d) In case the Central Sectoral Cooperative Organisation in which the concerned Organisation or Association is a member, or the National Cooperatives Federation submits a credible action plan for resuming the operations of the problematic Organisation or Association, entrust such responsibility to with on terms and conditions prescribed,


(e) To take other reformatory and remedial measures which the Management Committee deems appropriate.

---

### Section 115: Management Committee may write to take action

1) The Management Committee may, in the course of carrying out functions relating to asset management or paying liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association, write to the concerned agency to take actions as follows:


(a) To freeze the transactions, accounts or other movable or immovable assets of a member of the Board or an employee or his or her family member,


(b) Not to issue a passport to a member of the Board, manager or employees or to suspend such passports if issued,


(c) To include the names of the members or persons not paying back the loans of the problematic Organization or Association in the blacklist.


2) In case the writing referred to in sub-section (1) is received, the concerned agency shall carry out the action accordingly and inform the Management Committee about it.

---

### Section 116: Recommendation may be made

1) The Management Committee may recommend taking departmental actions or other actions against any official or employee who does not cooperate in, or obstructs the course of, the functions of management of assets or payment of liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association.


2) In case a recommendation referred to in sub-Section (1) is received, the concerned authority shall take departmental or other actions according to the prevailing laws.


3) While carry out the functions of management of assets or payment of liabilities of the problematic Organization or Association, in case the Management Committee has reasonable ground to believe that offences referred to in Section 122 have been committed, it may write to the agency having authority to conduct investigation and bring prosecution according to the prevailing laws.

---

### Section 117: Report to be submitted

The Management Committee shall, within one month of completion of the performance of management of assets or payment of liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association, submit a report to the Ministry stating the following details:


(a) Refund of the members’ amount of savings,


(b) Liabilities paid,


(c) Details of the remaining assets and liabilities,


(d) Recommendations relating to policies to be adopted by Government of Nepal with regard to the operation of an organisation or Association,


(e) Other details as deemed appropriate by the Management Committee.

---

### Section 118: Terms and conditions of services of chairperson and members of Management Committee

1) The chairperson and members of the Management Committee shall be the whole-time officials.


2) Remuneration and other benefits of the chairperson and members of the Management Committee shall be as specified by the Government of Nepal.


3) No chairperson or member of the Management Committee shall disclose to an unauthorised person any information he/she learns in the course of carrying out official business.

---

### Section 119: Cooperative may be sought

1) The Management Committee may seek the cooperation of other agencies as necessary to carry out its Functions, duties, and powers.


2) It shall be the duty of all concerned to extend cooperation as sought by the Management Committee.


3) While carrying out functions of management of assets or payment of liabilities of the problematic Organisation or Association, the Management Committee may move its activities in coordination with other concerned agencies.

---

### Section 120: Expert Service may be sought

1) The Management Committee may procure the Expert services of technicians or experts in the concerned subjects as necessary.


2) The Management Committee may, for procuring technical or expert services, provide remuneration and facilities, having fulfilled the procedures under the prevailing laws relating to public procurement.

---

### Section 121: Provisions concerning Employees of Management Committee

The Ministry shall make available the necessary human and physical resources for the secretariat of the Management Committee.

## Chapter 19 Offences, Punishment, Fines and Appeal

### Section 122: Offences deemed to be committed

Whoever commits any of the following acts shall be deemed to have committed an offence under this Act:


(a) If one operates a Cooperative Organisation without registration or a Cooperative Organisation registration of which has been revoked, or if any person, firm or company carries out a transaction or operates business or services or other activity with the use of the word “cooperative” or its translated version in English language its name,


(b) If the savings of members are used for any purpose other than the purposes stated in this Act or Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act,


(c) If one disburses credits beyond the prescribed amount without collateral or security,


(d) In case a member of the Board, manager or employee embezzles assets of the Cooperative Organisation or savings or share amount,


(e) If one embezzles an amount by disbursing credits to any member, his or her relative or any other person or employee in a manner that it could not be recovered,


(f) If any member of a Cooperative Organisation on his or her own or in collusion with other members mobilises a share amount or savings amount on his or her free will, rendering a loss to the Cooperative Organisation,


(g) If one avails credits by submitting false or wrong statements, the collateral mortgaged is immature or if credits are embezzled,


(h) If a Cooperative Organisation makes an investment in violation of this Act or Rules or Byelaws framed under this Act or raises money with that objective,


(i) If one avails or provides credits having formed false enterprises,


(j) If credit is availed of or disbursed with unnaturally excessive valuation of collateral,


(k) If credit is availed or disbursed with unnaturally high assessment of project costs on the basis of a false statement,


(l) If credit is availed or disbursed again with the already mortgaged collateral security given to any person or Cooperative Organisation without duly releasing it, or if the credit is borrowed or disbursed exceeding the value of the collateral,


(m) If the credit availed from a Cooperative Organisation for one purpose is abused or caused to be abused by using it for any other purpose,


(n) If any matter written on any document or account or ledger of Cooperative Organization is removed or deleted or tempered by any means or thereby distorting the meaning or sense or writing something otherwise or in case separate account is maintained thereby causing benefit or loss to oneself or to any other person, or forgery is committed for causing loss to anyone by misleading others with representing the untrue or non-existent facts to be true or existent or by getting a document signed with an alteration in the date, number or particular or causing to be committed the same,


(o) If a valuator of assets valuates any movable or immovable assets, while valuating it for collateral security or auction of the collateral or for any other purpose, with excessively high valuation or lower or false valuation, thereby causing loss to the Cooperative Organisation or causing it to be committed,


(p) In case any act is committed with the malafide intention of causing loss to a Cooperative Organization or causing illicit benefit or loss to anyone by getting something to be done or forbearing to do, by doing or not doing favour to anyone, by giving or accepting any amount, by purchasing or selling any goods, services without price or with less price or by giving or accepting donation or gift or souvenir, by preparing false document or false translation,


(q) In case of carrying out or causing to be carried out auditing of any Cooperative Organisation with the intention of causing loss to the Cooperative Organisation or members or depositors or preparing or causing to be prepared a false auditing report thereof.

---

### Section 123: Saving of actions taken in good faith

1) No official or employee of the Ministry, Department or a cooperative Organisation or Association shall be individually or collectively liable for any act done in good faith according to Rules, bylaws or Directives framed under this Act.


2) Notwithstanding contained sub-section (1), no act done without fulfilling the procedures and with malafide intention shall be saved.

---

### Section 124: Punishment

1) Whoever commits any of the offences referred to in Section 122 shall be liable to the following punishments:


(a) For commission of the offences referred to in clauses (a), (c), (m) and (q), an imprisonment up to one year and a fine up to one hundred thousand rupees,


(b) For commission of the offence referred to in clause (l), an imprisonment up to two years and a fine up to two hundred thousand rupees,


(c) For commission of the offence referred to in clause (b), a fine equal to the claimed amount and imprisonment up to three years,


(d) For commission of the offences referred to in clauses (d), (e), (f) and (g) (h), (i), (j), (k), (o) and (p); the claimed amount shall be recovered, and equal amount shall be fined and following amount of imprisonment shall be imposed:


(1) If the claimed amount is up to one million rupees, imprisonment up to one year,


(2) If the claimed amount is above one million rupees and up to five million rupees, imprisonment from two to three years,


(3) If the claimed amount is above five million rupees and up to ten million rupees, imprisonment from three to four years,


(4) If the claimed amount is above ten million rupees and up to one hundred million rupees, imprisonment from four to six years,


(5) If the claimed amount is above one hundred million rupees and up to one billion rupees, imprisonment from six to eight years,


(6) If the claimed amount is whatsoever amount above one billion rupees, imprisonment from eight to ten years,


(e) For commission of the offence referred to in clause (n), an imprisonment of up to ten years.


2) Whoever attempts, abets, or assists to commit any offence referred to in Section 122 shall be liable to half of the punishment the offender in the first degree is liable to.


3) In case any Organisation or Association abets or assists in committing any offence referred to in Section 122, the chief executive or official or the person working in the capacity of an executive shall be liable to the punishment according to this Act.

---

### Section 125: Fines to be imposed

1) In case anyone is found, either from a complaint of anyone pursuant to Section 132 or while inspecting, monitoring or checking the accounts or from any other means, to have committed any of the following acts, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may impose a fine up to five hundred thousand rupees depending upon the nature and gravity of the act so committed:


(a) In case interest is collected on a member in contravention of this Act or Rules framed under this Act,


(b) In case the difference between the interest to be provided for savings and to be charged on credit is more than six percent, interest is charged on,


(c) In case the interest is charged on credit, having capitalized the interest in the main credit amount provided by the cooperative organisation,


(d) In case any member is provided with credit that is more than the prescribed percent of its capital fund,


(e) In case credit investment is made to members, other than those members who are listed at the time of registration of the organisation, without completing a period of three months after acquiring the membership,


(f) In case deposits are collected above fifteen times the primary capital fund,


(g) In case a share dividend of more than eighteen percent of the share capital is distributed,


(h) In case a Cooperative Organisation makes a transaction beyond its working area or carries out transactions with a non-member,


(i) In case a Cooperative Organisation confers membership to an artificial person in contravention of this Act.


2) In case anyone is found, from the content referred to in sub-section (1), to have committed any of the following acts, the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may impose a fine up to three hundred thousand rupees to that person upon the nature and gravity of the act so committed:


(a) In case of non-compliance with any directive issued or standards pre[1]scribed according to this Act or Rules framed under this Act,


(b) In case of not providing of any statement, document, report, notice, or information to be provided according to this Act or Rules framed under this Act,


(c) In case officials of the Board or the Accounts Supervision Committee are changed haphazardly without holding an election according to this Act,


(d) In case any Cooperative Organisation fails to comply with the terms and conditions specified pursuant to sub-section (4) of Section 15,


(e) In case of commission of any other act in contravention of this Act or Rules framed under this Act.


3) Before imposing the fine pursuant to sub-sections (1) or (2), the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar shall give a time of at least 15 days to the concerned person or Cooperative Organisation to defend the case.


4) The power to impose the fines pursuant to sub-section (1) and (2) shall be exercised District Cooperative Association; and by the Registrar in case of the Province Sectoral Cooperative Association, Provincial Cooperative Association, Specialized Cooperative Association, Central Sectoral Cooperative Association, National Cooperatives Federation and Cooperative Bank and by the authority authorised by the Registrar in case of an Organization, District Sectoral Cooperative Association.

---

### Section 126: To freeze transactions, accounts and assets

1) The Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar may, in addition to imposing a fine pursuant to Section 125 on the concerned Cooperative Organisation, recommend that the concerned agency freeze transactions, assets, bank accounts, and property of such a Cooperative Organisation for a period not exceeding months.


2) In case the recommendation is received pursuant to sub-section (1), the concerned agency shall have to freeze transactions, assets and bank accounts of such a Cooperative Organisation and inform the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar.

---

### Section 127: Double Fine to be imposed

1……. If a person or cooperative organisation that has been fined pursuant to Section 125 commits the same offence again, the Registrar or an officer authorised by the Registrar shall impose a double fine on such person or cooperative organisation for each subsequent offence.


1 Excluded by the Act to Amend Certain Nepal Acts, 2074.

---

### Section 128: Nepal Rastra Bank may punish

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, the Nepal Rastra Bank may punish as follows on a Cooperative Bank that violate the terms and conditions specified at the time of granting of a by the Nepal Rastra Bank or orders issued from time to time or directives issued in the course of inspection, supervision or regulation:


(a) To issue a warning or admonition in writing,


(b) To cause the Board of Directors to execute a bond for carrying out reformative actions,


(c) To issue an order in writing to end frequent violations, to refrain from it and to begin reformative actions,


(d) To impose full or partial restrictions on carrying out transactions,


(e) To suspend or revoke the license of the Cooperative Bank.


2) In case a director or employee of a Cooperative Bank violates orders, directives issued by the Nepal Rastra Bank or terms of the license; fails to submit document, statement, statistics or record demanded at the time of inspection and supervision; within the specified time or commits any act against interests of the depositors or members, the Nepal Rastra Bank may impose the following punishment on such a director or an employee:-


(a) To issue a warning or admonition in writing,


(b) To suspend him or her,


(c) To impose a fine not exceeding five hundred thousand rupees,


(d) To order the Board to suspend the payment of the entire benefits, such as salary, allowances,


(e) To order the Board to remove such a person from the office if such a person is a director and to take departmental actions if such a person is an employee according to the Byelaws relating to terms and conditions of service.


3) While imposing the fine under this Section, he/she shall be provided with a reasonable opportunity to defend himself/herself.

---

### Section 129: To refer for investigation

In case the Ministry, Nepal Rastra Bank, the Registrar, or the authority authorised by the Registrar learns, in the course of exercising the functions, duties and powers under this Act, that anyone has committed an offence referred to in Section 122, it may write to the concerned agency to carry on investigation according to this Act with regard to such an offence.

---

### Section 130: Government of Nepal to be a plaintiff

The Government of Nepal shall be a plaintiff in a case involving an offence punishable pursuant to Section 124, and such a case shall be deemed to have been enlisted in Annexe-1 of the State Cases Act, 1993.

---

### Section 131: Authority to adjudicate a case

Judicial proceedings and adjudication of a case involving an offence punishable pursuant to Section 124 shall be held by the concerned district court.

---

### Section 132: Statutory Limitation to file a complaint

1) The person who comes to know that an offence punishable pursuant to Section 124 has been committed or is going to be committed shall file a complaint within ninety days of coming into such information.


2) A person who comes to know that an offence to be fined pursuant to Section 124 has been committed shall file a complaint to the Registrar or the authority authorised by the Registrar within ninety days of coming into such information.

---

### Section 133: Appeal may be filed

1) A person who is not satisfied with the decision of denial to Registrar Cooperative Organization pursuant to Section 16 or of revocation of registration of Cooperative Organization pursuant to Section 88 may, within thirty-five days from the date of getting information of the decision, file an appeal to the Registrar in case the decision has been made by the authority authorised by the Registrar and to the Secretary of the Ministry in case the decision has been made by the Registrar.


2) The person who is not satisfied with the decision of fine referred to in Section 125, and of the punishment imposed pursuant to clauses (d) and (e) of sub-section (1) of Section 128 and clauses (c) and (d) and (e) of sub-section (2) of the same Section, he/she may file an appeal as follows within thirty five days from the date of getting information of the decision:-


(a) To the concerned district court in case the decision has been made by the authority authorised by the Registrar,


(b) To the concerned High Court in case the decision has been made by the Registrar.

---

### Section 134: Due Amount to be recovered

In case any amount of fine or any other due amount to be paid by any person under this Chapter is due, such an amount of fine or amount shall be recovered from him or her as government due.

## Chapter 20 Miscellaneous Provisions

### Section 135: Special Provisions concerning operation of Association

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, buildings, land and similar types of other immovable property of organisations such as Sajha Yatayat, Sajha Prakashan, Sajha Swastha Sewa, Sajha Bhandar, Sajha Sewa, which are under the ownership or control of Government of Nepal, Provincial government or local level and having fifty five percent of share ownership of Government of Nepal at the commencement of this Act, shall not be sold, mortgaged or leased for a period of more than five years without approval of the Government of Nepal.


2) The Government of Nepal, Provincial government, or local level shall accord priority to managing services to be rendered by the organisations referred to in sub-Section (1). Collaboration may also be worked out by providing financial assistance to such organisations.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, other provisions as to the operation and management of the organisations referred to in sub-section (1) shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 136: Exercise of Voting Right

1) Whatever number of shares of a Cooperative Organisation has been subscribed by any member, he/she may exercise the voting right in the operation of functions of the concerned Cooperative Organisation based on the one-member, one-vote principle.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (1), in case of an Association, more than one representative may be sent as prescribed for the purpose of taking part in the General Meeting, and provisions concerning sending of the representatives shall be as prescribed.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-Section (2), no more than one member may be elected from one Organisation or Association in the Board of Accounts Supervision Committee of the concerned Association.

---

### Section 137: Social Audits may cause to be conducted

1) A Cooperative Organization may, for the purpose of attaining the objectives referred to in its byelaws, conduct a social audit on the decisions made and functions carried out by the Board, services received by members and level of satisfaction, positive impact made on economic, social, cultural, and environmental aspects of the members’ standard of life, and other aspects.


2) The Board shall submit to the General Meeting the report of the social audit conducted pursuant to sub-section (1) for the purpose of discussions.


3) The General Meeting shall, after having discussed the report of the social audit submitted pursuant to sub-Section (2), give necessary directives to the Board.


4) The concerned Cooperative Organisation shall submit one copy of the report of the social audit prepared under this Section to the Registrar or the authority authorised by him or her, and in case of a Cooperative Bank, one copy of such a report shall also be submitted to the Nepal Rastra Bank.

---

### Section 138: Cooperative Education

1) The Government of Nepal may submit basic knowledge of cooperative education in the school-level curriculum and render assistance to establish cooperative schools, faculties, or colleges (schools of cooperatives) under universities.


2) Other provisions as to cooperative education, including accreditation of cooperative institutions running academic programs of cooperatives, shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 139: Financial Assistance to be provided

Except for members and for social functions, the amount of a Cooperative Organisation shall not be spent as financial assistance for other functions.


Explanation: For the purpose of this Section, “social functions” means education, health, environmental protection, humanitarian assistance, promotion of cooperatives and so on.

---

### Section 140: Inter-government transactions may be carried out

1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, Cooperative Organisations may carry out inter-cooperative transactions amongst themselves.


2) Procedures for operating inter-cooperative transactions shall be as prescribed.

---

### Section 141: Provisions concerning tax

Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, Cooperative Organisations operating financial services within Municipal Corporation areas, Sub-Municipal Corporation areas, and Municipal areas shall be subject to a ten percent, seven percent, and five percent tax, respectively.


Provided that no tax shall be levied on the Cooperative Organisations operating financial services in the Rural Municipality area.

---

### Section 142: Contract not to be concluded

Cooperative Organisation shall not award a contract of the Cooperative Organisation to any of its directors, chairperson or member of the Accounts Supervision Committee, or employee of the Cooperative Organisation.

---

### Section 143: Settlement of Disputes

In case of any dispute arising between Cooperative Organisations, a Disputes Resolution Committee may be formed as prescribed for the settlement of such a dispute.

---

### Section 144: Encouragement may be given

The person, Cooperative organisation, or other agency making a significant contribution to the promotion of the cooperative sector may be encouraged as prescribed.

---

### Section 145: Departmental actions may be taken

The employee who exercises the authority granted under this Act or Rules framed under this Act with malafide intention, departmental actions against him or her shall be taken according to the prevailing laws.

---

### Section 146: Recovery to be made as government due

In case a grant from the government or service or facility received by a Cooperative Organisation is found to be misused, it shall be recovered as a government due.

---

### Section 147: Prevailing law relating to companies not to apply

In case of a Cooperative Organisation registered under this Act, the prevailing law relating to companies shall not apply.

---

### Section 148: No Hindrance to take actions according to prevailing laws

Nothing contained in this Act shall be deemed to have hindered the filing of a case against a person in court according to the prevailing laws.

---

### Section 149: Power to frame Rules

The Government of Nepal may frame necessary Rules for the implementation of this Act.

---

### Section 150: Special provisions relating to regulation, monitoring and supervision

11) The regulation, monitoring and supervision of a cooperative organisation registered at the local level and having an annual turnover of up to twenty-five million rupees shall be carried out by the concerned local level.


2) The regulation, monitoring and supervision of a cooperative organisation registered at the local level and having an annual turnover of more than twenty-five million rupees and up to fifty million rupees and a cooperative organisation registered in a province and having an annual turnover of up to fifty million rupees shall be carried out by the concerned province.


3) The regulation, monitoring, and supervision of a cooperative organisation registered at the local level or province, with an annual turnover of more than fifty million rupees and a cooperative organisation registered with the Department, shall be carried out by the Department.


4) The regulation, monitoring and supervision of a cooperative organisation with an annual turnover of two or more local levels or provinces as referred to in Sub-section (1) or (2) and having an area of ​​operation in two or more local levels or provinces shall be carried out by the local level or province where the central office of such organisation is located.


5) Notwithstanding anything contained in Sub-section (1) or (2), if the concerned local level or province requests that the Department regulate, monitor, and supervise any cooperative organisation on the basis of risk, the Department may regulate, monitor, and supervise such organisation.


6) In addition to sub-section (1), (2), (3), (4) or (5), the Nepal Rastra Bank shall also regulate, inspect and supervise such organisations on the basis of financial governance and risk of a cooperative organisation having a share capital of more than fifty million rupees or having an annual turnover equal to that amount, upon the request of the Department.


7) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, the Department may regulate, monitor, and supervise any cooperative organisation in the event of a complaint or grievance regarding non-compliance with this Act and the regulations, procedures, guidelines, directions, or instructions made under this Act.


8) The Department may have such organisations regulated, monitored and supervised by a committee consisting of representatives of the specified bodies and subject matter experts according to the nature of the work of the cooperative organisation.

### Section 151: Standards, Directives or Procedures may be framed and submit
