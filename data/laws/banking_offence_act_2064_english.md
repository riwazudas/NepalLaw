An Act Formulated to Provide Legal Provisions in relation to the Banking Offences and Punishments


Preamble: Whereas it is desirable to make legal provisions regarding banking offences and punishments in order to minimise the impact and risks on the banking and financial system from possible criminal acts in the transactions of the banking and financial system and to enhance credibility towards the banking and financial system,


The Legislative Parliament has formulated this Act.


---

## Chapter-1 Preliminary

### Section 1: Short title and commencement

1) The title of this Act shall be “Banking Offence and Punishment Act, 2064.


2) This Act shall be applicable within Nepal and also to the persons and institutions whomsoever, residing abroad after committing banking offence in Nepal.


3) This Act shall come into effect immediately.

---

### Section 2: Definitions

Unless the subject or context otherwise requires, in this Act,


(a) “Bank” means a bank licensed by Nepal Rastra Bank to carry out “A” category financial transactions, pursuant to prevailing law.


(b) “Banking Offence” means the offences as stipulated under Chapter 2.


(c) “Financial Institution” means a financial institution licensed by Nepal Rastra Bank to carry out “B”, “C”, and “D” category financial transactions, pursuant to prevailing law.


(d) “Institution” means a firm or company eligible to maintain a bank account with the banks or financial institutions, pursuant to the prevailing laws. The term also denotes other Organised Institutions or agencies established under the prevailing laws.


(e) “Borrower” means a person, firm, company or Organised Institution incorporated under prevailing law, which borrowed from a Bank or Financial Institution. The term also denotes the person who has provided a guarantee.


1(f) “Cooperative organisation or association” means a cooperative organisation or association that has obtained permission from Nepal Rastra Bank to conduct banking transactions pursuant to Sub-section (2) of Section 26 of the Cooperative Act, 2048.


1(g) “Dhukuti transaction” means a transaction conducted by raising money from each other and taking turns to receive or give money on the basis of an agreement.


1(h) “Financial system” means a bank or financial institution, a cooperative organisation, an institution engaged in financial intermediation, an institution engaged in securities, services and barter transactions, an institution engaged in insurance and security transactions, and an institution engaged in collective fund transactions, and the overall financial system including the services, payment and settlement systems provided by such institutions, established and operating in accordance with the law for the purpose of conducting any banking or non-banking financial transaction.


1(i) “Authorised person” means a person or official authorised to make the final decision in the course of any transaction.


(j) “Family member” means the husband, wife, son, daughter, adopted son, adopted daughter, father, mother, stepmother of the founder, director, shareholder deemed to have a financial interest in accordance with the prevailing law, chief executive officer, employee, managing agent or related person of a bank or financial institution, and this term also refers to the elder brother, younger brother and younger sister who are to be maintained by the latter.

## Chapter-2 Banking Offences

### Section 3: Not to open an account or demand cash payment in an unauthorised manner

While opening an account with a bank or financial institution or demanding cash payment, no one shall undertake the following acts:


1(a) Opening an account by presenting a forged document or knowingly opening an account by presenting such document or depositing or causing to be deposited money in such account or withdrawing money from such account or assisting in such action or opening an account and withdrawing money using an electronic card or device,


(b) Open or allow to open an account in the name of a fictitious or other person or organisation, except otherwise permitted by the laws,


1(c) Knowingly writing a check for which there is no balance in one’s account.

### Section 6: Not to obtain or make payment by way of abuse or unauthorised use of electronic  means

No one shall obtain or make payment by way of abuse or unauthorised use of a credit card, debit card, automated teller machine(ATM) card or other electronic means.

### Section 7: Not to avail or provide loans in an unauthorised manner

While availing or providing loans from a bank or financial institution, no one shall commit the following acts:


(a) Avail or provide loans by submitting a false, fake or unreal financial statement or by creating an artificial business.


(b) Avail or provide excess loans by way of unnatural overvaluation of collateral security.


(c) Avail or provide loans by way of unnaturally hiking the project cost based on false details.


(d) Avail or provide credit, facility or discounts beyond the authority obtained or limit sanctioned.


1(d1) Avail credit or advance facilities by the Chief Executive Officer or employees of the bank or financial institution other than the loan or facilities provided according to a prevailing employee by rule from his/her bank or financial institution.


1(d2) Avail credit by promoter, director, shareholder deemed to have financial interest pursuant to prevailing laws or his/her family member from his/her bank or financial institution.


Provided that restriction shall not be made to avail credit against the security of fixed deposit receipt, gold or government securities.


2(e) Re-avail or re-provide loans from or by another Bank or Financial Institution without having a due release of the collateral security once provided to a Bank or Financial Institution.


Provided that, this restriction shall not be applicable in case of release of loans to be provided under consortium lending under the paripassu agreement up to the value covered by the collateral.


2(f) Avail loans through an entity having established in the name of a person who does not have the financial capability to run the business or who is a person under undue influence or establish a business in the name of such person or provide credit in the name of the person by creating false burrower or extend or obtain loans knowing the said facts.


(g) Extend credit more than the requirement compared to the customer’s business transaction.


(h) Accept or provide any sort of undue benefit in return for granting credit facility.


1(i) Obtain full or partial credit facilities by the promoter, director, chief executive officer or person responsible for sanctioning credit of bank or financial institution by approving credit in the name of a false customer with the intent of utilising credit facilities.


1(j) Supply credit facilities in the name of a person or institution with the financial interest of the promoter, director, chief executive officer or person responsible for sanctioning credit.

### Section 8: Not to misuse credit

No one shall misuse the credit facilities availed from a bank or financial institution or let the same be misused by diverting in the purpose other than for which the credit facilities were availed.

---

### Section 9: Not to misuse banking resources, means and assets

11) The founder, director, shareholder, chief executive officer, employee, managing agent, or related person or organisation, or family member of such person, of a bank or financial institution shall not misuse the resources and means of the bank or financial institution by taking facilities from the bank or financial institution or in any other way.


2) No one shall cause or cause to be caused any expenditure of the assets of a bank or financial institution in a manner that is detrimental to the interests of the depositor or the bank or financial institution.


3) No one shall commit any financial irregularities in any transaction of a bank or financial institution, including non-banking assets.


1 Amended by the First Amendment.

### Section 10: Not to acquire assets or open account by borrower who has over dues

No borrower who has over dues shall remit money to a bank by opening an account with a local or foreign bank or financial institution or, continue such account or, operate the account or purchase any movable or immovable assets in any manner or acquire title or possession over such assets in any manner without settling the dues payable to a bank or financial institution.


However,


(a) An account may be opened for the purpose of repayment of a loan or deposit of money in the account for up to 30 days and make payment of the loan by cheque.


(b) The borrower may withdraw the amount from an account situated in Nepal or a foreign country for daily basic life maintenance up to the prescribed limit.

---

### Section 11: Not to stop credit facility in the way to loss working project of borrower

A Bank or Financial Institution which has once provided the first instalment after approving a credit facility for a project of the borrower, without sufficient basis and important reason, shall not stop the remaining instalments in-between in the way to loss working project of the borrower.

---

### Section 12: Not to Make Loss by Making Alteration in Account or Ledger or by Committing Forgery or  Fraud

No one shall, with a motive of self-benefit or to cause loss or benefit to any other person, commit any forgery by action like tempering any matter written in Bank or Financial Institution’s document or account or ledger whether by removing or by re-writing the same to mean different sense or with a motive to loss other, commit fraud by misleading others representing the untrue or non-existent facts to be true or existent or by getting a document signed with an alteration in the date, number or particular.

---

### Section 12A: Not to make any Fraudulent Activities to Bank and Financial Institutions and Cooperatives

1) No person shall, by collusion or fraud, obtain or cause to be obtained movable or immovable property owned by a bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association, or by making, giving or presenting a forged document that gives him his right to property to which he has no right, or by falsely claiming that he has something that he does not have, or by distorting a false statement as true, or by deceiving him by any other means, or by causing him to lose his right to acquire or sell movable or immovable property owned by a bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association, or by buying, selling, dealing in or bartering the same property, whether or not it is stated in a document that the property of the bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association is his or hers or that it has become his or hers, thereby causing loss or damage to the bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association.


2) No person shall cause any loss or damage to a bank or financial institution by making any false or fraudulent statement, promise or in any other manner whatsoever in order to obtain cash, funds, securities or other movable or immovable property under the control, custody or ownership of such bank or financial institution or cooperative organisation or association, or to interfere with or take control of such property or to assist in the implementation of such a plan.


3) No person shall enter into any transaction with a bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association on the basis of any false or misleading statement or promise or shall falsely state his/her name as if it were not his/her own, but someone else’s, or shall falsely state or give such a name to another person, or shall knowingly claim or give such a thing from another person, etc., in order to avoid punishment for an offence or to deceive, deceive or deceive a bank, financial institution, cooperative society or association in any way, thereby causing loss or damage to himself/herself or to benefit anyone else.

---

### Section 13: Not to Derive Excess, Low or False Valuation or Prepare False Financials

1) While carrying out the valuation of movable or immovable assets held by a bank or financial institution as collateral security of a loan or non-banking movable or immovable asset of a bank
or financial institution, the valuator shall not derive an excess, low or false valuation of such assets while evaluating them for the purpose of auctioning, selling, or other purposes relating to the Bank.


2) Moreover, the valuator shall not carry the valuation of the property of any person or institution in which he/she has a financial interest.


3) No person, firm or, company or institution shall prepare or cause to prepare separate financial statements for a particular date or period for the purpose of availing credit.


4) Auditor shall not certify different financial statements for the same date or period to be submitted by any person or institution to the Government of Nepal, foreign donor agency or other entity without disclosing his/her opinion.

---

### Section 14: Not to Carry Out and Cause to Carry Out Irregular Economic and Financial Transactions

No one shall, with a motive to cause any harm loss to a bank or financial institution, get something done or undone or bargain or forbid bargaining or take or give any amount or take or give any goods or services free of costs; take or give any charity, grant, gift or donation; execute or get executed or translate or get translated a false deed or do work or get the work be done with an ill intention to cause illegal benefits or losses.

---

### Section 14A: Not to carry out Dhukuti transactions

No one shall carry or cause to be carried out Dhukuti transactions.

---

### Section 14B: Not to carry out banking transactions in an illegal manner

Cooperative organisations or associations registered under the Cooperative Act, 2048 BS shall not conduct or cause to be conducted banking transactions without obtaining approval from Nepal Rastra Bank pursuant to sub-section (2) of Section 26 of the same Act.

## Chapter-3 Punishment

### Section 15: Punishment

11) If anyone commits any offence under Section 3, he shall be liable to a fine of the amount of the offence and imprisonment for a term not exceeding three months if the amount is discovered and to a fine of the amount of the offence and imprisonment for a term not exceeding three months, and if he commits any offence under Section 11, he shall be liable to a fine of the amount of the offence and imprisonment for a term not exceeding three months.


12) If any person commits any act as per clauses (d), (d1), (d2), (e), (f), (g), (h), (i) and (j) of Section 5, 6, Section 7, Section 8, 9, 10, 12 or 14, if the following amount is related to such offence, then the following imprisonment and fine shall be imposed according to the severity of the offence:-


(a) Imprisonment for one year if the amount is up to one million rupees,


(b) Imprisonment for two to three years if the amount is more than one million rupees and up to five million rupees,


(c) Imprisonment for three to four years if the amount is more than five million rupees and up to one crore rupees,


(d) Imprisonment for four to six years if the amount is more than one crore rupees and up to ten crore rupees,


(e) Imprisonment for six to eight years if the amount is more than ten crore rupees and up to fifty crore rupees,


(f) Imprisonment for eight to ten years if the amount is more than fifty crore rupees and up to one billion rupees,


(g) Imprisonment for ten to twelve years if the amount is more than one billion rupees, whatever the amount is.


13) The following offences shall be punishable as follows:-


(a) If an offence under clauses (a), (b), (c) of Section 7 or Section 13 is committed, a fine shall be imposed according to the amount of the offence and imprisonment for a term not exceeding four years.


(b) If an offence under Section 12A., 14A. or 14B. is committed, and the amount of the offence is as follows, the following imprisonment and fine shall be imposed according to the amount of the offence, depending on the extent of the offence:-


(1) Imprisonment for one year to three years if the amount is up to five million rupees,


(2) Imprisonment for three years to five years if the amount is between five million rupees and fifty million rupees,


(3) Imprisonment for five years to seven years if the amount is between fifty million rupees and fifty million rupees,


(4) Imprisonment for seven to nine years regardless of the amount of the offence.


4) In case the suit amount cannot be established in accordance with this Section, he/she shall be punished with a fine up to Rupees one million and imprisonment up to two years.


5) In case any organisation commits any offence specified under this Act and if the concerned office bearer or the employee committing such offence be identified, he/she shall be held liable, if the office bearer or the employee cannot be identified organisation, the person working in the capacity of the organisation head at the time of the occurrence of the offence shall be held liable.


16) A person or organisation that attempts to commit a banking offence or a person or organisation that indirectly participates in or aids the commission of such offence shall be punished with half the punishment to be imposed on the offender.


27) If there is an organisation that attempts to commit a banking offence or is indirectly involved in or aids the commission of such offence, the chief executive or officer or person performing work in an executive capacity of such organisation shall be punished in accordance with this Act.


28) If the person who commits the offence under Sections 2, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 12A., 12, 14, 14A. and 14B. is the chairman, director, or chief executive officer of the concerned organisation, he shall be imprisoned for one year in addition to the punishment to be imposed in accordance with this Act.

### Section 16: Punishment to the Persons Creating Hindrance

If anyone creates hindrance protest in the investigation and inquiry proceedings undertaken under this Act, the adjudicating officer may punish him/her with imprisonment of up to six months or a fine of up to five thousand rupees or both based on the report of the Officer involved in Investigation and inquiry.

## Chapter-4 Proceedings and Disposal of the suits

### Section 17: Time and limitation for the Lodgement of First Information Report

1) In regard to an offence under this Act, a First Information Report may be lodged within one year from the date the offence comes to the knowledge, and the suit shall have to be lodged within six months from the date the First Information Report is so lodged with the Court as fixed by the Government of Nepal with publishing a notice in Nepal Gazette.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained hereinabove in sub-section (1), a lawsuit may, at any time, be initiated against an employee or office-bearer of a bank or financial institution, who caused misappropriation or loss of assets of the Bank or Financial Institution during his/her assumption of service in any post thereof and there shall be no obstruction in initiating a lawsuit even after such office-bearer or employee retires from his/her service.

---

### Section 18: Government to be the plaintiff

In the lawsuits, where punishment shall be given as per this Act, the government shall be the plaintiff, and such lawsuits shall be considered to be included in Schedule-1 of Act relating to Muluki Criminal Procedure (Code)Act, 2074.

---

### Section 19: Proceedings may be initiated by detaining into the custody

1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, the Officer involved in the Investigation and inquiry may detain an offender under the Act in custody by giving him/her a warrant as per the prevailing law if there is adequate ground to believe that the person, against whom proceedings have been initiated as per this Act, may extinct or destroy any evidence or create hindrances or obstacles or impact adversely in the proceeding of investigation and inquiry of it or on the basis of the evidence existent presently, if there is reasonable ground to believe that a person, not having residence in Nepal, is the offender of banking offence who can be subject to imprisonment up to 6 months or above.


2) In case the investigation and inquiry initiated in regard to the person detained in custody as stipulated under sub-section (1) above could not be completed within 24 hours and it is deemed necessary to continue the investigation and inquiry process by detaining him/her further in custody, the investigation and inquiry officer shall detain further only after obtaining approval from the adjudicating officer getting the person in presence before the adjudicating officer.


(3) While requesting the adjudicating officer for approval as stipulated under sub-section (2) above, the charges against the person detained in the custody, basis thereon, justification for continuing the investigation and inquiry proceedings by detaining him/her in the custody and description of the affidavit of statement obtained, if any taken from him/her, shall have to be clearly mentioned.


4) If approval for detention as stipulated under sub-section (2) above is sought for detention, the adjudicating officer may, considering, after reviewing the concerned documents, whether the investigation and inquiry progress has been satisfactory or not, grant approval for detention up to a period of maximum 1sixty days at a time or in multiple instances but not exceeding 1seven days at a time if progress in the investigation and inquiry proceedings deemed to be satisfactory.


1 Amended by the First Amendment.

---

### Section 19A: Special investigation team may be formed

1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing law, if it is deemed appropriate to form an investigation team including experts in banking matters to investigate a case related to banking offences, the Government of Nepal may, in consultation with the Attorney General, the Inspector General of Police and the Nepal Rastra Bank, form such a team.


2) The special investigation team formed pursuant to sub-section (1) shall have the same powers as an officer conducting investigation and inquiry under this Act or prevailing law.

---

### Section 19B: Power to request details

1) If the investigating and investigating body considers it necessary to obtain or examine any document, account statement or other details in order to fulfil the objectives of this Act and if it considers that any person or organisation has or can obtain and submit any document, account statement or other details related to the investigation and investigation, the investigating and investigating body may request such person or organisation to submit such document, account statement or other details.


2) It shall be the duty of the person or organisation concerned to provide the document, account statement or other details requested by the investigating and investigating body pursuant to sub-section (1).

## Chapter-5  Miscellaneous

### Section 20: Cooperation to be extended

It shall be the duty of the bank or financial institution or the concerned individual to extend necessary cooperation to the investigation and inquiry officer while carrying out investigation and inquiries on the offence under this Act.

---

### Section 21: Action not be initiated on the ground of breach of secrecy

Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, if the person who provided information on the happening of the offence or possible happening of the same to initiate legal proceedings or to stop occurrence of an offence under this Act is an employee of a bank or financial institution, no action shall be taken on the ground of breach of secrecy in terms of his/her service rules, for the reason of his/her providing/doing the such cooperation.

---

### Section 22: Provisions relating to Delivery of Notice

1) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, a notice to be delivered in the name of a foreign individual, in connection with the offences under this Act, shall be delivered in the name of an office or representative of such individual in Nepal, if any, and the notice so delivered shall be deemed to be duly delivered.


2) In case the office or representative as stipulated under Subsection (1) is not existent, the notice shall be delivered at the main business place of such individual or his/her permanent residential address or at the mailing address if provided by him/her during the course of business, through telex, telefax or other means of recordable telecommunication or through post by registration and the notice so delivered shall be deemed to be duly delivered.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained under Subsection (1) or (2), if there is a separate provision in any treaty where the Government of Nepal or Nepal is a party, there shall be no obstruction in delivering the notice in the name of a person residing in a foreign country in the manner as specified in the same.

---

### Section 23: Notice to be published

In case a report is received in connection with the dispatch or delivery of a notice to any individual under this Act or other prevailing laws detailing that the notice could not be dispatched or delivered for the reason that the address of such individual could not be identified or for any other reasons, notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing laws, a public notice to this effect shall be published in a national level newspaper (in an English daily in regard to a foreigner) at least two times extending him/her a time period of 3o days and furnishing thereon the abridged particulars of the case, whether the case is investigated or is already lodged with the adjudicating officer. If the notice is so published, it shall be deemed to be duly delivered or delivered to such individual, notwithstanding anything contained under this Act or other prevailing laws.

---

### Section 24: Issuance of order for creating encumbrances over the assets of a foreign  individual

1) If any foreign individual does not appear in front of the investigation and inquiry officer as per the notice, even after providing notice by the investigation and inquiry officer or delivering it pursuant to Section 22, the investigation and inquiry officer may issue an order not to take outside Nepal or to create encumbrance over the property, entitlement, interest or concern held by him/her within Nepal, if any, till the person doesn’t appeal in front of the investigation or inquiry officer and it shall be the duty of the all concerned to comply with such order.


2) Person not complying with the order issued pursuant to Sub-section (1) shall be punished with a fine up to Rupees one hundred thousand by the investigation and inquiry officer. The losses or harm caused to the Government of Nepal or public institution, if any, due to such non-compliance of the order shall also be recovered from him/her.

---

### Section 25: Adjudication proceedings and disposal of the case not be affected

Notwithstanding anything contained under the prevailing laws, the adjudication proceedings and disposal of the case initiated or to be initiated pursuant to this Act shall no longer be affected even if the offender has died.

---

### Section 26: Waiver in the Claim of Punishment may be Happen

During the investigation and inquiry, an officer may provide a waiver in the claim of punishment, in full or part, to a person extending cooperation in regard to the investigation and inquiry proceedings initiated under the Act presenting such person as his/her witness.


Provided, notwithstanding anything contained in this Act or in prevailing laws, a lawsuit may be reregistered against such person if his/her cooperation could not be established from other proof/evidence or if he/she made a statement before the adjudicating officer against the cooperation extended by him/her to the investigation and inquiry officer.

---

### Section 27: To be considered ineligible

A person convicted of imprisonment under this Act shall be considered ineligible to work as an employee of a bank or financial institution.

---

### Section 28: To be governed as per the prevailing laws

Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere under this Act, in the offence committed in the involvement of a person holding a public position under the prevailing laws or of a civil servant, if the offence is prosecutable or punishable pursuant to Commission for the Investigation of Abuse of Authority Act, 2048 and the Corruption Prevention Act, 2059, the action shall be initiated and the punishment shall be given in accordance with the same Acts.

---

### Section 29: Regulation may be framed

The government of Nepal may formulate necessary regulations to implement the objectives of this Act.
