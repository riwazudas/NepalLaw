# Non-Resident Nepali Rules, 2066 (2009)

---

## Chapter &#8211; 1 Preliminary

### Rule 1: Short title and commencement

1) These Rules may be called “Non-resident Nepali Rules, 2066.”


2) These Rules shall come into force immediately.

---

### Rule 2: Definition

Unless the subject or the context otherwise requires, in these Rules, –


(a) “Act” means the Non-Resident Nepali Act, 2064 (2008).


(b) “Mission” means the Nepali embassies situated in foreign countries, and this word also refers to the permanent mission of Nepal to the United Nations or the Consulate General of Nepal situated in a foreign country.


(c) “Chief of Mission” means the person employed (assigned) as a chief employee in the mission, and the word also refers to the employee assigned to take care of his functions in his absence.


(d) “Ministry” means the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.


(e) “Secretary” means Secretary of the Ministry.


(f) “Association” means Non-resident Nepali Association registered pursuant to Rule 12.


(g) “Statute/Constitution” means the statute/constitution of the Association.

## Chapter-2 Provision on Registration and Identity Card

### Rule 3: Application has to be submitted for the registration of the name

---

### Rule 3: Application has to be submitted for the registration of the name

1) Any non-resident Nepali or his/her family who intends to register his/her name as a non-resident Nepali shall submit the application pursuant to Schedule-1, before the Chief of Mission if abroad and before the Secretary if in Nepal.


2) While submitting the application under sub-rule (1), the fees prescribed pursuant to Schedule 2 shall be attached.

---

### Rule 4: Name has been registered, and Identity card shall be issued

---

### Rule 4: Name has been registered, and Identity card shall be issued

1) While examining the application received pursuant to Rule 3 by the Chief of Mission, Secretary or officer designed by him/her, if the application and other attached documents prove appropriateness to register the name of the applicant, then the name shall be registered as a non-resident Nepali within the Seven days of the receipt of application and such record shall be maintained according to the format prescribed in Schedule-3.


2) After registering the Name and maintaining the record pursuant to sub-rule (1) and collecting the fees as prescribed in Schedule-4, an identity card shall be issued to such Non-resident Nepali in the format prescribed in Schedule-5.


3) While making an examination pursuant to sub-rule (1), if the applicant’s name is not found appropriate to register, then denial for the registration of the applicant’s name can be made by explaining the reason for such denial.


4) If the denial for registration of the name under sub-rule (3) has to be made, then the notice of such denial with the reason shall be given to the applicant.

---

### Rule 5: Valid period for Identity Card and Renewal

1) The identity card issued under Rule 4 shall remain valid for a maximum of ten years in case of a foreign citizen of Nepalese origin, and for a period of two years in case of a Nepalese citizen residing abroad, pursuant to sub-section (3) of Section 4 of the Act.


2) A Non-resident Nepali, who intends to renew an identity card, shall submit an application to the chief of mission or secretary in the format prescribed in Schedule-6 prior to the expiry of the valid period of the identity card.


3) If an application is submitted pursuant to sub-rule (2), the chief of mission or secretary shall, upon collecting the fee prescribed in Schedule-4, renew the identity card without exceeding the period prescribed pursuant to sub-section (3) of Section 4 of the Act at once.

---

### Rule 6: Cancellation (Revocation) of Identity Card

1) The identity card is deemed to be cancelled in the following situation: –


(a) If ipso facto cancelled pursuant to Section 17 of the Act.


(b) If the valid period of the identity card has expired.


2) The identity card issuing Authority may cancel the identity card at any time if it is found that false information was submitted to obtain the identity card.


3) Before cancelling the identity card pursuant to sub-rule (2), an opportunity to produce (present) his/her clarification shall be provided to the concerned non-resident Nepali.

---

### Rule 7: Changed details/information shall be informed

1) If the details/information submitted with the application for registration of name as a non-resident Nepali pursuant to sub-rule (1) of Rule 4 has been changed, such non-resident Nepali shall provide the information of such change to the chief of mission or secretary within three months.


2) On the basis of the information received pursuant to sub-rule (1), the chief of mission or the secretary shall keep the updated record as maintained pursuant to sub-rule (1) of Rule 4.

## Chapter-3 Provisions related to Investment and Privileges

### Rule 8: Information about Investment shall be provided

1) A non-resident Nepali who makes an investment in Nepal shall provide information about the investment which has been made by him/her, to the ministry in the format prescribed in Schedule-7.


2) The information received pursuant to Sub-rule (1) shall be written in the record maintained pursuant to sub-rule (1) of Rule 4, and the ministry shall give the information of such investment to the Department of Industry.

---

### Rule 9: Certificate of Capital Investment shall be received

A non-resident Nepali who brings the capital investment in Nepal pursuant to Section 7 of the Act, if he intends to receive the certificate of such investment, the bank that transfers such capital investment in Nepal shall issue the certificate in the format prescribed in Schedule 8 to the concerned person.

---

### Rule 10: Provisions Related to the Repatriation of Money

1) A non-resident Nepali who intends to repatriate the investment and the amount of his equivalent to the profit earned (made /received). Pursuant to Section 9 of the Act, an applicant shall submit an application to the Secretary, attaching the following details and documents:


(a) Copy of permission taken for investment pursuant to the Act,


(b) Decision of the board of directors of the concerned company or agency regarding the repatriation of investment,


(c) Copy of tax clearance certificate,


(d) Audited Balance sheet of the fiscal years, previous to the fiscal year in which the application for repatriation has been made,


(e) Copy of evidence for the paid-up of the liability or loan of any person or agency if such loan or liability has been made,


(f) The name, branch, and address of the Bank which has to be used for repatriation,


(g) Letter of affidavit, declaring that there is no due on any revenue or loan that has to be paid back to anybody or any liability has to be paid back in Nepal.


2) If the application pursuant to Sub-rule (1) has submitted, the secretary shall give the permission for repatriation of money under the provision of the Act in such a manner that in case of the investment made by a non-resident Nepali in convertible foreign currency at once or time to time and in full or partial basis and in case of profit made out of such investment, the amount equivalent to such profit at once.

---

### Rule 11: Facilities and Concession for Foreign Citizens of Nepali Origin

1) Subject to the provision contained in sub-rule (3) and (4), any foreign citizen of Nepali origin may purchase any land in any one of the regions and not exceeding the following area, in order to reside within Nepal for him/herself or his/her own family in Nepal: –


(a) Maximum 2 Ropani, in Kathmandu Valley,


(b) Maximum 8 Kattha, in municipalities of the Terai district,


(c) Maximum 4 Ropani, in other municipalities except those mentioned in (a) and (b),


(d) One Bigaha, in village development committees of the terai district, except as mentioned in part (b), or


(e) 10 Ropani, in other areas except mentioned in (a), (b), (c), and (d).


2) The provisions of sub-rule (1) do not prevail if any foreign citizen of Nepali origin has received any immovable property in the status of a Nepali citizen.


3) Any foreign citizen of Nepali origin if intends to purchase the land or the other property pursuant to the Section 10 of the Act and Sub rule (1), shall submit an application mentioning the location, area and the price that has been fixed for the purpose of purchase of the land which he/she intends to purchase in his/her own or his/her family’s name,


4) If the application has to be submitted pursuant to sub-rule (3), the secretary shall give permission to purchase the land and other immovable property within Nepal, in the name of the applicant or his/her own family, after making an inquiry and ensuring that nothing has been against the Act or these Rules.


5) After receiving the permission pursuant to sub-rule (4), the concerned office responsible for the registration pass (transfer deed) of the land shall make the registration of the land in the name of the foreign citizen of Nepali origin who has been intended to purchase the land and other property in his/her own or his/her family’s name, subject to the provisions of the Act and there Rules.


6) Notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing law, a person who is supposed to receive the identity card pursuant to the Act or these Rules shall not be liable to pay the visa fee.


7) Notwithstanding anything contained anywhere in these Rules, purchase of immovable property pursuant to the Act or these Rules shall not create further rights except those provided by the Act or these Rules.


8) The concerned land revenue office shall issue the blue coloured certificate while issuing a landowner certificate of immovable property pursuant to these Rules.

## Chapter-4 Provisions Related to Association

### Rule 12: Provision related to Registration and Renewal of Association

1) Those intending to make registration of an association for the purpose of sub-section (1) of Section 16 of the Act, at least fifty non-resident Nepalese from at least twenty-five countries shall submit an application attached with Five Thousand Rupees for registration fees and the following details, before the ministry.


(a) Objectives of the Association,


(b) The name, family name, and address of those intending to make registration of an Association,


(c) Copies of the identity card of the Non-resident Nepalese mentioned in Part (b),


(d) Details of officials of the Executive Committee of the Association,


(e) Economic Resources of the Association,


(f) Place, where the office of the association has to be situated,


(g) Three copies of the constitution (Statute) of the proposed association, signed by a non-resident Nepalese who intends to make registration of the association.


2) On the examination (inquiry) of the application and the statute received pursuant to Sub-rule (1), if the ministry finds that it is appropriate to make registration of the Association, registration shall be made, and a certificate of registration shall be issued in the format prescribed by the ministry.


3) If the registration of the association is made pursuant to these Rules, one attested copy of the statute shall be provided to the applicants upon issuing the certificate of registration.


4) The registration of the Association shall be renewed every ten years after paying the fees prescribed pursuant to sub-rule (1).

---

### Rule 13: General Assembly of the Association

1) The statute shall include the provision that the General Assembly of the Association shall be held at least once every two years.


2) The other Assembly and other provisions related to the General Assembly of the Association shall be made according to the provisions of the statute of the Association.

---

### Rule 14: Account and Audit of the Association

1) The account of income and expenditure of the association shall be kept according to the account system adopted by the Government of Nepal.


2) The audit of the account of the association shall be held by a registered auditor appointed by the general assembly of the association.


3) The auditor mentioned in sub-rule (2) shall, every year, submit one copy of the Audit report presented after auditing the account of the Association to the ministry.

---

### Rule 15: The Dissolution (Liquidation) of the Association and Its Consequences

1) If the Association has been dissolved (liquidated) due to inability to perform the functions prescribed by the statute of the Association or any other reason, the remaining property of the Association shall be transferred to the Government of Nepal.


2) If the dissolution has been taken pursuant to Sub-rule (1), then in case of the liability of the Association, the Government of Nepal shall take the liability within the limit of the property of the Association.

---

### Rule 16: Supervision

1) The ministry may supervise the account and functions of the association.


2) If it seeks the need for any reform in the functions and procedures of the Association during the supervision, the Ministry shall issue the required directives.


3) It shall be the duty of the Association to follow the directive issued pursuant to Sub-rule (2).

## Chapter-5 Miscellaneous

### Rule 17: Functions, Duties and Powers of the Advisory Board

1) The functions, duties and powers of the advisory broad constituted pursuant to sub-section (1) of Section 15 of the Act shall be as follows:


(a) To provide advice and suggestions to the government of Nepal on the matter of the policies to be adopted in relation to non-resident Nepali,


(b) To provide advice and suggestions to the government of Nepal on the matter of the policies to be adopted in relation to non-resident Nepali,


(c) To identify the role of non-resident Nepali in the construction and operation of infrastructure and overall development of Nepal, and to promote and encourage their investment and cooperation.


(d) To provide suggestions to the Government of Nepal about the legal, policy and organisational reforms that have to be adopted to make the non-resident Nepali’s investment secure, sustainable and long-lasting.


(e) To provide suggestions to the Government of Nepal on the solutions that have to be adopted to resolve obstacles and problems faced in relation to the implementation of the Act and these Rules,


(g) To recommend the actions that have to be taken in order to maintain the good relationship among the non-resident Nepalese,


(h) To provide suggestions on the policies that have to be adopted by the Government of Nepal to keep the cultural unity of the non-resident Nepalese abroad.


2) The meeting of the advisory board constituted pursuant to Sub-section of Section 15 of the Act and other procedures related to it, shall prevail, as determined by the Board itself.


3) The advisory board, constituted pursuant to sub-section (1) of Section 15 of the Act, shall submit the report of the completed activities to the Government of Nepal every year, and the Government of Nepal shall make such report public if needed.

---

### Rule 18: The Central Database (record) has to be maintained

1) Each mission shall, from time to time, send details related to name registration, identity card issuance, and their renewal to the Ministry of Industry.


2) The details received pursuant to sub-rule (1), including the details related to the investment pursuant to Rule 8, the Ministry shall maintain (establish) an integrated and central database (record) of the non-resident Nepalese and shall keep it updated.

---

### Rule 19: Changes and alterations in the schedule have to be made

The Ministry may change or alter the Schedule by publishing a notice in the Nepal Gazette.
