# Bonus Act, 2030 (1974)

---

## Preamble

Preamble: Whereas, it is expedient to provide for the legal provisions for the distribution of bonus to the workers and the personnel working in the Enterprises,


Now, therefore, be it enacted by His Majesty the King Birendra Bir Bikram Shah Dev on the advice and with the consent of the Rastriya Panchayat.

## Section 3: For the Computation of Bonus, Branches and Sub-Branches shall be treated as Parts of the Enterprise

For the computation of bonus under this Act, the branches or sub-branches of any enterprise situated in various places shall be treated as parts of the Enterprise.


---

## Section 4: Submission of the Balance-Sheet to the Labour Office

1) The management of each enterprise shall prepare the balance sheet and the statement of profit and loss of such enterprise pursuant to the Company Act, 2063 and submit it to the Labour Office within six months of the completion of the fiscal year.


2) If a complaint is registered along with the evidence that the balance-sheet and the statement of profit and loss submitted pursuant to Subsection (1) are false, the Labour Office may exercise all remarking and checking powers conferred to the concerned department pursuant to the Companies Act, 2063, as if such powers were conferred to it equally.

1) Each profit-making enterprise shall have to allocate an amount equivalent to ten percent of its net income of one fiscal year for a bonus to the employees.


2) While assessing the net income of any enterprise derived in any one fiscal year pursuant to sub-section (1), the following amounts shall have to be deducted from the net income assessed pursuant to the Income Tax Act, 2058:

3) Notwithstanding anything contained in Sub-section (1), the percentage of bonus and other matters relating to bonus which is to be distributed by the Government-owned enterprises shall be as determined by the Government of Nepal.


3A) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-sections (1) and (3), an enterprise owned by the Government of Nepal established for the promotion of any sector without the objective of earning profit shall not be entitled to distribute a bonus.


3B) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-sections (1) and (3), an enterprise owned by the Government of Nepal shall be entitled to distribute bonus only in the following circumstances:


(a) The amount of expenditure recorded on the accrual basis shall have been paid within the next financial year,


(b) The amount of contingent liabilities for each financial year shall have been determined, and the amount so determined shall have been set up and deposited in a separate fund.


(c) If any loan has been taken from the Government of Nepal or with the guarantee of the Government of Nepal, such loan amount shall have been repaid in accordance with the payment schedule mentioned in the agreement made at the time of taking the loan.

## Section 6: Eligibility for Bonus

1) An employee who has worked for at least half a period in a fiscal year shall be entitled to obtain a bonus under this Act.


Provided that, no employee shall be entitled to obtain a Bonus who has worked casually or on a shift basis.


2) For the purpose of sub-section (1), the following periods shall also be computed as a period where an employee has worked:


(a) A Period of being on standby pursuant to any commitment or pursuant to Section 15 of the Labour Act, 2074,


(b) A period under which an employee is on any leave with salary,


(c) A period of disablement caused by an accident arising in the course of the business of the enterprise.


---

## Section 7: Amount to be Obtained for Bonus and Its Assessment

1) The management shall assess the percentage of bonus amount to be obtained by any employee in a fiscal year pursuant to sub-section (2).


2) While computing bonus amount to be received by the employee pursuant to sub-section (1), the amount so separated for the distribution of bonus by the enterprise for the particular fiscal year shall be multiplied by a sum of One Hundred and the amount so deducted shall be divided by the pay or wage amount of the employee so entitled to receive the bonus and the amount so deducted shall be the percentage of the bonus amount.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (2), the bonus to be received by an employee shall not exceed the following amount:


(a) An employee receiving a monthly salary or wage up to twice the minimum wage prescribed by the Government of Nepal, an amount equivalent to his eight months’ salary or wage,


(b) An employee receiving a salary or wage in excess of clause (a), an amount equivalent to his six months’ salary or wage.


4) The minimum bonus amount to be obtained under clause (b) of sub-section (3) shall not be less than the maximum bonus amounts pursuant to clause (a).

## Section 9: Types of Bonus and Period of Distribution Thereof

1) The bonus to be distributed pursuant to this Act shall be paid in cash.


2) The bonus shall have to be distributed within a period of eight months from the close of the fiscal year.


3) If an application, specifying reasons of not being able to distribute the bonus within the period of Section 2 is submitted to the Labour Office by any Management, the Labour Office may, if the reasons are found genuine, extend the time for a period of three months at maximum for distribution of bonus or may allow to distribute the bonuses of two years at a time in the next fiscal year.


---

## Section 10: Bonus to be given to the Agent or to a Member of the Family

1) An employee who cannot be present to receive a bonus may authorise any person, with a letter of consent, to receive the bonus payable to them.


2) In the event of an employee’s death, the bonus to be received by the employee, if bequeathed to someone by the employee, shall be given to that person. If no person has been designated, or if the designated person has also passed away before the employee, then the legal heir, in accordance with the applicable laws regarding inheritance, shall receive the bonus.


3) A person who is dissatisfied with the bonus distributed pursuant to sub-section (2) may file a complaint with the Labour Court within thirty-five days from the date of such distribution, and in such a case, the decision of the Labour Court shall be final.


---

## Section 11: Bonus May Be Distributed as an Advance

1) If any enterprise fails not submit the balance-sheet and statement of profit and loss of such enterprise within the time limit as provided in sub-section (1) of Section 4, or if bonus could not be distributed within the time limit of Section 9 by the reasons of inquiry or examination conducted by the concerned Labour Office on the balance sheet and statement of profit and loss submitted pursuant to Sub-section (2) of Section 4, the management of such enterprise, after making tentative computation, shall have to distribute at least five percent of the net, income as bonus.


2) After preparation of the balance sheet and statement of profit and loss, or after the final assessment is made in this matter. If the amount of bonus distributed pursuant to the sub-section (1) is found to be less than the amount to be distributed as bonus, the difference amount shall have to be redistributed to the employees proportionately, having considered the previously distributed amount.


3) If the amount distributed as a bonus is found in excess of the amount assessed for distribution of bonus under sub-section (2), the excess amount, whatever may be, shall be deducted for recovery of it, while determining the net income of the enterprise in the next fiscal year.


---

## Section 12: Deduction of Bonus

In the case of the bonus to be obtained by an employee who has not worked the required days in any fiscal year, such an employee shall, subject to Section 6, be entitled to the bonus by deducting proportionally from his/her total bonus, from the day on which he/she has not worked.


---

## Section 12A: Bonus for the Time Period of the Illegal Strike

Where any employee has gone on strike illegally in any factory or enterprise, such an employee shall be entitled to the bonus for the other days only, by deducting a proportionate amount from the bonus for the striking days.


---

## Section 13: Welfare Fund

1) Out of the amount allocated for bonus pursuant to Section 5, seventy percent of the amount remaining after the distribution of bonus shall be deposited in the welfare fund established in accordance with the prevailing law and the remaining thirty percent shall be deposited in the national level welfare fund established by the Government of Nepal to make arrangements for the rights and interests of the employees of the enterprise.


1A) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), an enterprise owned by the Government of Nepal shall deposit twenty percent of the amount remaining after the distribution of bonus out of the amount allocated for bonus pursuant to Section 5 in the welfare fund established in accordance with the prevailing law and the remaining eighty percent shall be deposited in the national level welfare fund established by the Government of Nepal to make arrangements for the rights and interests of the enterprise.


2) The operation of the welfare funds as provided for in sub-section (1) shall be in participation of the employee as prescribed.


---

## Section 14: Details to Submit to the Department of Labour

The management of each factory and Enterprise shall submit details in the prescribed format to the Labour Office within seven days from the date of completion of bonus distribution.


---

## Section 15: Records and Statement of Accounts to Be Maintained

The record of the bonus distributed pursuant to this Act, along with other statements of account, shall be maintained in a manner as may be prescribed.


---

## Section 16: Settlement of Dispute of Bonus

1) If any dispute arises between the employee and management with respect to the bonus to be payable under this Act, the Labour Office shall resolve such dispute by negotiations, having invited both parties.


2) If the dispute could not be resolved by negotiations pursuant to sub-section (1), the Labour Office shall ask the concerned Enterprise and employees to produce necessary documents and statements of accounts and shall give a decision based on such documents and statements.


3) The party who is dissatisfied with the decision of the Labour Office made pursuant to sub-section (2) may appeal to the Labour Court, within thirty-five days of receipt of such notice, and the decision made by the Labour Court shall be final.

## Section 20: Penalty

The Department of Labour may fine with up to Five Thousand Rupees for a person who contravenes this Act or the order issued pursuant to the Rules framed hereunder.

## Section 21: Appeal

The party who is dissatisfied with the fine order made by the Labour Department pursuant to Section 20 of this Act may appeal to the High Court within thirty-five days of receiving the notice of such order.

## Section 23: Liability of the Manager

If any enterprise commits any offence punishable under this Act, a person working in the capacity of a manager of such enterprise at that time shall be liable to the punishment under this Act.


Provided that, if a manager proves that such an offence took place without his/her knowledge or consent, he/she shall not be liable to the punishment under this Section.


---

## Section 24: Protection of the Acts Done in Good Faith

No legal actions shall be taken against the Government of Nepal or any employee of the Government of Nepal in relation to any act done or intended to be done pursuant to this Act or Rules framed under this Act.


---

## Section 25: Power of the Government of Nepal to Remove Difficulties

If any hindrance or obstruction arises while implementing this Act, the Government of Nepal may remove such hindrance or obstruction by issuing a necessary order published in the Nepal Gazette without prejudice to the objectives of this Act.
