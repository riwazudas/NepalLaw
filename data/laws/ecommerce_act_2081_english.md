# Electronic Commerce (E-commerce) Act, 2081 (2024)

---

## Preamble

Unauthorised Translation


Preamble: Whereas it is desirable to make legal provisions to regulate and make the commercial transactions of goods or services conducted through information technology systematic and reliable,


The Federal Parliament has enacted this Act.


---

## Chapter 1 Preliminary

### Section 1: Short Title, Commencement, and Extension

Unauthorised Translation


1) This Act shall be called the “Electronic Commerce (E-Commerce) Act, 2081”.


2) This Act shall come into force on the thirty-first day from the date of its ratification.


3) This Act shall be applicable throughout Nepal and shall also apply to any person residing anywhere outside Nepal who provides goods or services in Nepal through electronic commerce.

---

### Section 2: Definitions

Unauthorised Translation


Unless the subject matter or context otherwise requires, in this Act,


(a) “Consumer” means a person or organisation that consumes or uses any goods or services under this Act.


(b) “Contract” means an agreement under Section 7.


(c) “Purchaser” means a person or organisation that purchases any goods or services under this Act, and that term also includes their representative.


(d) “Transporter” means a person, firm, company, or organisation employed by a businessperson to transport goods, and that term also includes a representative of such person, firm, company, or organisation.


(e) “Inspection Officer” means an inspection officer appointed or designated under the prevailing federal law relating to consumer protection.


(f) “Seller” means a person, firm, company, or organisation that provides goods or services to an intermediary entrepreneur for sale in accordance with this Act, and the term also refers to an entrepreneur engaged in list-based electronic trading.


(g) “Intermediary entrepreneur” means a person, firm, company, or organisation that facilitates the sale of goods or services of a seller to a buyer through an electronic platform.


(h) “Ministry” means the Ministry of Industry, Commerce and Supplies, Government of Nepal.


(i) “Director General” means the Director General of the Department.


(j) “Goods” means a substance made from a mixture of goods or goods consumed or used by the consumer in a way that does not cause harm, loss, or any negative impact on health, and the term also refers to the raw materials, colors, fragrances, or chemicals used in the manufacture of such goods.


(k) “Electronic platform” means a system designed to collect, transmit, or store information through websites, applications, software, the Internet, intranets, social media marketplaces, etc., using computers, mobile phones, or similar electronic devices.


(l) “Electronic trade (e-commerce)” means the process of buying or selling any goods or services through an electronic platform.


Provided that, if an electronic platform is used to provide information about or promote goods or services, it shall not be considered e-commerce.


(m) “Department” means the Department of Commerce, Supplies, and Consumer Protection of the Government of Nepal.


(n) “Businessperson” means an intermediary businessperson, or a businessperson engaged in e-commerce based on a list.


(o) “List-based electronic business operator” means a firm, company, or organisation that prepares a list of goods or services in its possession and sells the goods or services in such list directly to consumers through an electronic platform.


(p) “Service” means electricity, drinking water, telephone, information technology, health, education and consulting, shipping, tourism, entertainment, transportation, sewage maintenance, banking, insurance, or other services of a similar nature, and the term also refers to legal, audit, medical, or engineering services.

## Chapter 2 Operation of Electronic Commerce

### Section 3: Electronic Trading May be Carried Out

Unauthorised Translation


1) Any firm, company, or organisation registered under the prevailing law permitted to trade in goods or services may carry out electronic trading subject to this Act.


2) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), goods or services prohibited from being purchased and sold through electronic means by the prevailing law or by the Government of Nepal by publishing a notification in the Nepal Gazette shall not be bought and sold through such means.

---

### Section 4: Establishment of An Electronic Platform

Unauthorised Translation


1) Every entrepreneur conducting e-commerce shall establish an e-platform.


However, there shall not be any impediment for small entrepreneurs or similar domestic entrepreneurs to conduct e-commerce using other e-platforms.


2) On the platform referred to in sub-section (1), the entrepreneur shall provide at least the following details so that the buyer can make an informed decision regarding his business or profession at any time: –


(a) Name of the electronic platform,


(b) Name of the business, address, registered entity, and registration certificate number,


(c) Details of the registered office, head office, and branches and outlets of the entrepreneur, if any, where transactions are carried out,


(d) Details of any separate license obtained to operate a specific type of business,


(e) Details of which business is to be conducted among intermediary or list-based electronic commerce,


(f) Value Added Tax Registration Number or Permanent Account Number,


(g) Email, telephone, mobile number, telefax, social media address (link) to contact the entrepreneur and the email, phone, and mobile number designated for customer service,


(h) Email, telephone, or mobile number and address of the person or unit responsible for hearing complaints, and


(i) Listing number obtained after being listed on the electronic commerce portal pursuant to Section 5.


3) If there is any change in the details referred to in sub-section (2), the businessperson shall update the details on his/her electronic platform within forty-eight hours of the time of such change.


4) The details referred to in sub-section (2) shall be stated in a complete, clear, and easily accessible manner to the consumer.

---

### Section 5: Registration Required

Unauthorised Translation


1) After establishing an electronic platform pursuant to Section 4, the entrepreneur shall apply electronically for listing on the electronic business portal established by the Department, along with the following details:


(a) Name, address, registered entity, and registration certificate number of the business,


(b) Name and identifying details of the proprietor concerned in the case of a business registered in a private firm, of the partner in the case of a business registered in a partnership firm, and of the director of the company or organisation in the case of a company or organisation,


(c) Clear details of the entrepreneur’s business,


(d) Value Added Tax Registration Number or Permanent Account Number,


(e) Telephone number, email, social media address (link), and contact address of the entrepreneur,


(f) If there is a branch or outlet, its number and location, and


(g) Other details mentioned in the electronic portal.


2) Entrepreneurs who are conducting electronic business at the time of commencement of this Act shall apply for listing on the electronic portal pursuant to sub-section (1) within three months from the date of commencement of this Act.


3) The Department shall provide the information, including the platform registration number, to the concerned entrepreneur electronically within seven days from the date of receipt of the application for listing on the electronic portal pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2).


4) If the listed entrepreneur changes any of its branches or outlets after being listed pursuant to sub-section (3), the details shall be updated on the electronic portal within seven days from the date of such change.

---

### Section 6: Disclosure of Details

Unauthorised Translation


The entrepreneur shall disclose the following details regarding the goods or services offered for electronic trade on the electronic platform:-


(a) Details including the name, nature, design, trademark, shape or image of the goods or services available for electronic trade, or their combination, weight or underlying elements,


(b) Final selling price, including tax of the goods or services,


(c) Details of any additional charges incurred while transporting or transferring the goods,


(d) Information on any precautions to be taken while using the goods or services,


(e) Time and date required for transferring the goods or services,


(f) Means or method of payment for the goods or services,


(g) Period and conditions of warranty or guarantee of goods or services, if any,


(h) Person, firm, company, or institution producing the goods or providing the services,


(i) Country of production or processing of any imported goods or services,


(j) Details of any conditions to be followed after the purchase or sale of the goods or services,


(k) Any goods or services purchased, whether or not the product is refundable, and if so, the terms and conditions of the refund,


(l) If a service is sold, the place where the service is received and the terms of sale of the service,


(m) If a buyer requests a product or service, details of whether such a request can be cancelled before the product is dispatched or the service begins,


(n) The date of manufacture of the product, the date it must be consumed, and other necessary conditions,


(o) The main terms of the contract in a language that is understandable to the general public, and


(p) A mechanism for addressing customer reviews, ratings, complaints, and feedback.

---

### Section 7: Deemed to be a Contract

1) In accordance with this Act, if a contract is concluded between a buyer and a businessperson or a seller and a businessperson through electronic means in relation to the transaction of any goods or services, it shall be deemed to have been concluded in accordance with the prevailing law and if any liability arises in accordance with such contract, the party to the contract shall bear such liability.


2) In making a contract in accordance with sub-section (1), the businessperson shall, in addition to the provisions mentioned in the prevailing law relating to contracts, disclose in such contract the necessary information, terms and provisions including the provision of goods or services to the buyer, cancellation, return, exchange, warranty and guarantee, refund.

---

### Section 8: Electronic Commerce Payments

Unauthorised Translation


1) In conducting electronic commerce pursuant to this Act, arrangements may be made for the buyer or the trader on behalf of the trader to pay the determined price of the goods or services to the carrier before or after the purchase or transfer of such goods or services or at the time of sale or transfer. If such payment is made to the carrier, the trader shall be deemed to have received payment.


2) When making payment pursuant to sub-section (1), payment instruments in accordance with the prevailing federal law relating to payment and settlement may be used.


3) When payment is made pursuant to sub-section (2), the bill of goods or services and electronic or physical receipt of payment shall be made available to the buyer immediately, and a record thereof shall be kept. If such payment is made, the amount shall be deemed to have been duly paid.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Section, the purchase or sale of any goods or services in foreign currency shall be subject to the prevailing federal law.

---

### Section 9: Transfer of Goods or Services

Unauthorised Translation


1) The entrepreneur shall transfer or cause to transfer the goods or services demanded to the buyer or his representative at the place specified in the contract with the buyer and within a specific day, time or period as mentioned in the contract.


2) If the buyer or his representative is physically present while transferring the goods or services pursuant to sub-section (1) and wishes to collect them from any sales centre of the entrepreneur, the goods or services shall be transferred to him from the same centre.


3) If the goods or services are transferred pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2), the goods or services shall be deemed to have been duly transferred.


4) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (2), if the buyer or the entrepreneur wishes to change the place, person, period or time of transfer of the goods demanded by the buyer before the goods are dispatched and both parties agree on the matter, the entrepreneur shall transfer the goods to the changed place, person, period of time.


5) In the event of a change in place or period pursuant to sub-section (4), the fee for such transfer may be increased or decreased by mutual agreement between the buyer and the entrepreneur.

---

### Section 10: Goods or Services May be Returned

Unauthorised Translation


1) If any goods or services purchased by the buyer pursuant to this Act are not as per the description provided by the entrepreneur in accordance with Section 6, the buyer may return such goods or services to the entrepreneur without using them or causing any damage.


2) If the buyer wishes to return the goods or services pursuant to sub-section (1), the entrepreneur shall take back such goods or services unconditionally.


3) If the goods or services are returned pursuant to sub-section (1), the entrepreneur may, if the buyer wishes, provide other goods or services of the same value in place of such goods or services.


4) If the goods or services are returned pursuant to sub-section (1) and the buyer refuses to take other goods or services in place of the returned goods or services in accordance with sub-section (3), the entrepreneur shall return to the concerned buyer the amount, including tax paid while purchasing the goods or services so returned.


5) Other provisions regarding the return of goods or services shall be in accordance with the prevailing federal law.

---

### Section 11: Export and Import May be Carried Out

Unauthorised Translation


1) Subject to the prevailing federal law, any person, firm, company, or organisation may purchase and import any goods or services from any foreign firm, company, or organisation engaged in electronic commerce.


2) If a businessperson sells goods or services to any foreign person, firm, company, or organisation through an electronic platform operating within Nepal, he may export such goods or services after ensuring payment through the banking system, subject to the prevailing federal law, in accordance with a mutual agreement.

---

### Section 12: Personal Information Must be Kept Confidential

1) The entrepreneur shall maintain the confidentiality of personal information or personal details identifying any person related to electronic commerce.


2) The details that must be kept confidential pursuant to sub-section (1) shall not be given to anyone or used by the entrepreneur except in accordance with the prevailing law.


However, there shall be no obstacle to the exchange of information related to the transaction between the buyer, entrepreneur, or transporter involved in the purchase and sale of goods or services pursuant to the contract.


3) The entrepreneur shall not deprive any user of the facility for the buyer to enter their details, amend them, and close the source of their identification by entering the platform.


4) If anyone does any act contrary to sub-section (2), he shall be punished in accordance with the prevailing law.

---

### Section 13: Prohibition of Sale and Distribution

Unauthorised Translation


1) Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, if a separate license is required under the prevailing law for the purchase and sale of any particular type of goods or services or if any condition or procedure is required to be fulfilled, such goods or services shall not be sold or transacted without such license being obtained or without such condition or procedure being fulfilled.


2) If goods or services are sold without obtaining a license pursuant to sub-section (1) or without fulfilling the condition or procedure, action shall be taken in accordance with the prevailing federal law.

---

## Chapter 3 Obligations of Parties Involved in Electronic Commerce

### Section 14: Obligations of the Intermediary Practitioner

Unauthorised Translation


In addition to the obligations specified elsewhere in the prevailing law and this Act, the obligations of an e-commerce entrepreneur shall be as follows:


(a) To mention the details referred to in Section 6 on its platform in a clear and accessible manner for the buyer,


(b) To mention the information and information required to be kept in accordance with the prevailing law on its platform,


(c) Not reviewing or evaluating the quality and characteristics of the goods or services placed on the platform for sale by presenting itself or any other person as a pseudo-consumer and entering feedback on such goods or services,


(d) While advertising the goods or services placed for sale, not to present the actual characteristics of such goods or services, their method of use and other details in a manner that misleads the buyer or consumer,


(e) To preserve the records of the transactions of the goods or services purchased and sold for the period prescribed in the prevailing law on tax, based on the nature of the transaction,


(f) Except in cases of force majeure, to transfer the goods or services sold or provide the services no later than the predetermined time,


(g) To provide a warranty or, if there is a guarantee, to comply with the terms of such warranty or guarantee for the specified period,


(h) If the goods or services sold are not as displayed or as mutually agreed upon or are delivered later than the predetermined time or if such goods or services are defective, to withdraw, cancel or discontinue such goods or services sold or approved for sale and to refund the payment made for such goods or services to the concerned buyer, and


(i) If any commitment has been made in writing regarding the authenticity of any goods or services sold, to bear any liability if any question arises in that regard.

---

### Section 15: Responsibilities of Traders Carrying Out E-Commerce Based on the List

Unauthorised Translation


In addition to the obligations specified elsewhere in the prevailing law and this Act, the obligations of an e-commerce entrepreneur shall be as follows:


(a) To mention the details referred to in Section 6 on its platform in a clear and accessible manner for the buyer,


(b) To mention the information and information required to be kept in accordance with the prevailing law on its platform,


(c) Not reviewing or evaluating the quality and characteristics of the goods or services placed on the platform for sale by presenting itself or any other person as a pseudo-consumer and entering feedback on such goods or services,


(d) While advertising the goods or services placed for sale, not to present the actual characteristics of such goods or services, their method of use and other details in a manner that misleads the buyer or consumer,


(e) To preserve the records of the transactions of the goods or services purchased and sold for the period prescribed in the prevailing law on tax, based on the nature of the transaction,


(f) Except in cases of force majeure, to transfer the goods or services sold or provide the services no later than the predetermined time,


(g) To provide a warranty or, if there is a guarantee, to comply with the terms of such warranty or guarantee for the specified period,


(h) If the goods or services sold are not as displayed or as mutually agreed upon or are delivered later than the predetermined time or if such goods or services are defective, to withdraw, cancel or discontinue such goods or services sold or approved for sale and to refund the payment made for such goods or services to the concerned buyer, and


(i) If any commitment has been made in writing regarding the authenticity of any goods or services sold, to bear any liability if any question arises in that regard

---

### Section 16: Seller’s Liability

Unauthorised Translation


In addition to the obligations specified elsewhere in the prevailing law and this Act, the seller shall have the following obligations:


(a) Before providing such goods or services on the platform of an intermediary entrepreneur for the sale of goods or services through an electronic platform, a written or electronic contract shall be entered into with such an entrepreneur,


(b) Proof of business registration, documents, his/her name, surname, address, mechanism for collecting and addressing consumer complaints, details related to exchange, return, or refund of goods or services, permanent account number or details registered in Value Added Tax shall be provided to the intermediary entrepreneur in writing or electronically,


(c) Details as per Section 6 shall be provided to the intermediary entrepreneur in relation to each good or service sold through an electronic platform,


(d) Except in cases of force majeure, the goods or services sold shall be transferred or the services provided not later than the predetermined time,


(e) Not engage in any kind of unfair business transaction while providing such goods or services to an intermediary entrepreneur for the sale of goods or services through an electronic platform or in any other case,


(f) The seller shall not present himself or any other person as a pseudo-consumer of the goods or services provided for sale to the intermediary entrepreneur, shall not review or evaluate the quality of such goods or services, and shall not enter feedback, and


(g) If the goods or services provided for sale by the seller through the intermediary entrepreneur are not as displayed or as mutually agreed upon or are delivered later than the predetermined time or if such goods or services are defective, such goods or services sold or approved for sale shall be withdrawn, cancelled or discontinued and the payment made for such goods or services shall be refunded to the concerned buyer.

---

### Section 17: Buyer’s Obligations

Unauthorised Translation


1) When purchasing goods or services electronically in accordance with this Act, the buyer shall bear the obligations as per the contract between the buyer and the businessperson.


2) When conducting electronic business in accordance with this Act, the buyer shall not do or cause any act to be done with the intention of causing harm to anyone.


3) When purchasing goods or services electronically in accordance with this Act, the buyer shall check and verify whether the goods or services purchased electronically in accordance with the contract made at the time of purchase are received or not, and in case of any complaint, the complaint shall be made as soon as possible, including through electronic means.

---

### Section 18: Liability Relating to Transportation

Unauthorised Translation


1) If a contract is made to deliver any goods or services requested by the buyer to the place, time, and person specified by him, notwithstanding anything contained in the prevailing law, the entrepreneur shall be liable to transport or provide the service in accordance with such a contract.


2) The entrepreneur may enter into a separate contract with a transporter to transport the goods or transfer the service in accordance with sub-section (1).


3) The liability of the transporter as mentioned in the prevailing law on consumer protection shall be equally applicable to the transporter as mentioned in this Act.


4) If anyone does any act in a manner contrary to sub-section (3), he shall be punished in accordance with the prevailing law on consumer protection.

---

### Section 19: Prohibition of Engaging in Improper Commercial or Business Activities

Unauthorised Translation


1) While conducting electronic commerce pursuant to this Act, a seller, entrepreneur, or other party shall not do or cause to be done any act that is considered to be unfair commercial or business activity under the prevailing law.


2) If any act is done or caused to be done pursuant to sub-section (1), action shall be taken in accordance with the prevailing law.

---

### Section 20: No Exemption from Liability

Unauthorised Translation


Notwithstanding anything contained elsewhere in this Act, if a business receives a complaint pursuant to Section 28 or a complaint in accordance with Section 33 regarding any goods or services provided or sold through its electronic platform, it shall not be exempted from the obligation to address such complaint or complaint solely on the ground that it did not manufacture, import or provide the goods or services sold.

## Chapter 4 Offences and Punishment

### Section 21 Deemed to Have Committed an Offense

Unauthorised Translation


Any person who does or causes or to be done any of the following acts shall be deemed to have committed an offence under this Act:


(a) If he conducts electronic trade without establishing an electronic platform pursuant to Section 4,


(b) If he conducts business without being listed pursuant to Section 5,


(c) If he conducts electronic trade without disclosing details of goods or services pursuant to Section 6,


(d) If he fails to fulfil the obligations pursuant to Clauses (a), (b) and (e) of Section 14; Clauses (a), (b), (e) and (f) of Section 15 and Clauses (a), (b), (c) and (d) of Section 16 or if he does any act that constitutes a breach of the obligations, or


(e) If he fails to fulfil the obligations pursuant to Clauses (c), (d) and (f) of Section 14; Clauses (c), (d), (g), (h) and (i) of Section 15 and Clauses (e), (f) and (g) of Section 16 or if he does any act that constitutes a breach of the obligations

---

### Section 22: Fines to be Imposed

Unauthorised Translation


1) If an offence is committed under clause (a), (b), (c), or (d) of Section 21, the Inspection Officer may impose a fine ranging from twenty thousand rupees to one hundred thousand rupees, depending on the severity of the offence.


2) The party concerned who is not satisfied with the fine imposed by the Inspection Officer under sub-section (1) may file an appeal with the Director General within seven days of the date of receipt of the order of fine.


3) The Director General shall decide on the appeal received under sub-section (2) and the complaint received under Section 28 within thirty-five days, and such a decision shall be final.

---

### Section 23: Punishment

Unauthorised Translation


Whoever commits or causes an offense to be committed as per Clause (e) of Section 21 shall be punished with a fine ranging from fifty thousand rupees to five hundred thousand rupees or imprisonment ranging from six months to three years or both, depending on the gravity of the offense.

---

### Section 24: Investigation and Filing of Cases

Unauthorised Translation


1) The inspection officer shall investigate the offence punishable under Section 23.


2) After completing the investigation pursuant to sub-section (1), the inspection officer shall send the case to the concerned government attorney for a decision on whether or not to prosecute the case. If the government attorney decides to prosecute the case, the inspection officer shall file a case in the relevant consumer court, as per the prevailing law, or, if no such court has been established, in the relevant district court.

---

### Section 25: Government of Nepal to be the plaintiff

Unauthorised Translation


The Government of Nepal shall be the plaintiff in cases of offenses punishable under Section 23.

---

### Section 26: Summary Procedure to be Adopted

Unauthorised Translation


In the processing and disposal of cases filed under Section 24, summary procedures in accordance with prevailing federal law shall be adopted.

---

## Chapter 5 Miscellaneous

### Section 27: Monitoring and Inspection of Electronic Commerce

Unauthorised Translation


1) The Department shall carry out the monitoring and inspection of electronic commerce.


2) In addition to sub-section (1), if there is a provision for monitoring and inspection of any goods or services by a separate body or authority under the prevailing law, the monitoring and inspection of such goods or services shall be carried out in accordance with the same.


3) If any defect is found in the electronic platform or the arrangements related to it during the monitoring and inspection pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2), the Department or the monitoring and inspection body or authority may issue necessary instructions, and it shall be the duty of the entrepreneur to comply with such instructions.

---

### Section 28: Right to File a Complaint

Unauthorised Translation


1) If anyone commits an act contrary to this Act, any person who has information about it may file a complaint with the Director General or the Inspection Officer, along with the information or evidence in their possession. Such a complaint may also be filed through electronic means.


2) If the person filing the complaint pursuant to sub-section (1) wishes to keep his name confidential, the name of such person shall be kept confidential.


3) Notwithstanding anything contained in sub-section (1), if during the course of monitoring, any businessperson or any other person is found to have committed any act contrary to this Act or if any complaint is received in this regard, the monitoring report or complaint shall also be treated as a complaint.


4) If it is deemed necessary to conduct further investigation into the complaint received by the Director General pursuant to sub-section (1) or (3), he shall send such a complaint to the Inspection Officer concerned for taking action under this Act.


5) The Inspection Officer shall investigate the complaint received pursuant to sub-section (1), (3) or (4) within six months and, if there is a matter for imposing a fine, shall impose a fine and if there is a matter for initiating a case, shall take action to initiate a case in accordance with this Act.


6) If it appears that action on the complaint received pursuant to this Section should be taken by any other body, the Director General or Inspection Officer shall send a letter to the concerned body for action as soon as possible.


7) The body concerned shall take action in accordance with the prevailing law on the matter for which action has been taken pursuant to sub-section (6) and inform the Department thereof.

---

### Section 29: Coordination Required

Unauthorised Translation


While monitoring and inspecting the electronic platform or its related arrangements, the department will coordinate with the government of Nepal’s body that regulates information technology-related matters in accordance with prevailing laws and will conduct such work in collaboration with subject matter experts.

---

### Section 30: Criteria to be Followed

Unauthorised Translation


If the Nepal Government agency regulating information technology has determined and implemented the necessary standards in accordance with prevailing law for the improvement and upgrading of electronic platforms, the concerned businessperson will have to comply with those standards.

---

### Section 31: Pursuant to the Consumer Protection Act

Unauthorised Translation


Except as otherwise provided in this Act, the quality of goods or services, labels to be affixed to goods, procedures and grounds for returning sold goods, pricing of goods or services, investigation, inspection and monitoring procedures, resolution of consumer disputes, compensation and the basis for determining compensation, and other provisions relating to the rights, interests or protection of consumers shall be as provided in the prevailing laws relating to consumer protection, and the provisions of such laws shall apply mutatis mutandis to goods or services traded electronically under this Act.

---

### Section 32: Power to Give Directions

Unauthorised Translation


1) The Department may issue necessary instructions to the businessperson to make the operation and management of electronic commerce more effective in accordance with this Act.


2) If there is a provision under the prevailing law for the regulation of any goods or services by a separate body or authority, such body or authority may also issue instructions pursuant to sub-section (1).


3) It shall be the duty of the businessperson concerned to comply with the instructions pursuant to sub-section (1) or (2).

---

### Section 33: Grievance Management

Unauthorised Translation


1) If a buyer or consumer has purchased any goods or services from a business or has started the purchasing process and is not satisfied with it, he or she may file a complaint with the person responsible for hearing complaints or the unit arranged by the business through electronic means or by giving a written notice or by appearing in person.


2) If any complaint is received pursuant to sub-section (1), the business shall register the complaint and immediately inform the buyer or consumer thereof through electronic or other means.


3) If a complaint is received pursuant to sub-section (1), the business shall investigate it and give a decision within fifteen days.


4) If it is necessary to request additional information from the buyer or consumer in connection with the investigation of the complaint pursuant to sub-section (3), the person responsible for hearing the complaint may request such information.


5) If the person responsible for hearing the complaint can address the complaint of the buyer or consumer, then the person responsible for hearing the complaint shall, if it can be addressed, provide written information along with the reason for the same to the buyer or consumer concerned within fifteen days from the date of receipt of the complaint.


6) The business should develop and implement an electronic system-based mechanism (online redressal mechanism) for grievance management pursuant to this section.

---

### Section 34: Deemed to Have Been Done by the Entrepreneur Himself

Unauthorised Translation


Pursuant to this Act, if an employee, worker, or representative of a business conducts electronic commerce on behalf of the business, such commerce shall be deemed to have been conducted by the business itself.

---

### Section 35: May Grant Exemptions, Facilities, or Concessions

Unauthorised Translation


The Government of Nepal may, by publishing a notice in the Nepal Gazette, provide necessary exemptions, facilities, or concessions to micro, cottage, and small industries that transact goods or services produced in Nepal in excess of the limit specified by the Ministry, or sell their products through an electronic platform.

---

### Section 36: No Impediment to Prosecuting Cases in Accordance with Prevailing Law

Unauthorised Translation


If an act deemed to be an offense under this Act is also deemed to be an offense under other prevailing laws, this Act shall not be deemed to prevent the prosecution of a case under such laws.

---

### Section 37: Power to Make a Directory

Unauthorised Translation


The Ministry may formulate guidelines as required for the implementation of this Act.

---

### Section 38: Measures to Implement the Act

Unauthorised Translation


The Ministry shall measure the implementation of this Act within five years of its commencement and within one year after the completion of every subsequent five-year period and shall submit a report thereon to the relevant committees of both Houses of the Federal Parliament.
